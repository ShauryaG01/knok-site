01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Getting Hired at Veeva

Veeva Systems builds cloud software, data products, and services for the life sciences industry. Its customers include pharmaceutical companies, biotech firms, and medical device manufacturers who rely on Veeva for CRM, content management, regulatory workflows, and clinical data tools. That niche focus makes Veeva distinctive as an employer: enterprise software with a deep healthcare angle, and a business model that has stayed consistently profitable through market cycles.

As of July 2026, Veeva has 778 open roles globally, spanning engineering, professional services, customer success, sales, and operations. India is a core part of Veeva's global workforce, with active hiring in Pune and Hyderabad. Roles range from early-career software development to senior consulting and solutions delivery.

Getting hired here takes genuine preparation, but the process is structured and transparent. Candidates who understand the life sciences domain and can demonstrate Veeva's values clearly tend to progress quickly. This guide covers every stage of the process and what to do at each one.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

The Interview Process, Stage by Stage

The process typically runs five to six stages depending on the role and level.

Stage 1: Application and Resume Screen

Your resume is matched against the job description. Domain keywords help: life sciences, CRM, Veeva Vault, clinical trials, pharma commercial, and regulatory affairs all signal relevant familiarity. A generic software or consulting resume without any life sciences context may not pass the first filter, even if your technical skills are strong.

Stage 2: Recruiter Call

A recruiter will reach out to confirm your interest, walk through your background, and explain the role. Your current CTC and salary expectations come up early. Have a clear answer ready on both. The recruiter is also gauging communication clarity and genuine interest in the life sciences space.

Stage 3: Technical or Functional Interview

For engineering roles, this commonly includes a coding round or system design discussion depending on your level. For consulting, customer success, or solutions roles, expect scenario-based questions grounded in real life sciences workflows. Candidates commonly report questions like 'how would you approach a CRM implementation for a pharma field force team.'

Stage 4: Behavioral and Values Interview

Veeva ties its interviews directly to its four core values. Expect structured behavioral questions asking you to walk through specific situations from past work. Interviewers probe for depth, so prepare STAR-format answers (Situation, Task, Action, Result) tied to each value before you go in.

Stage 5: Final Round or Case Discussion

For senior or consulting roles, this often includes a presentation or case study. You may need to walk through a solution approach, a project plan, or a client scenario. Engineering candidates at senior levels commonly report a system design or architecture deep-dive at this stage.

Stage 6: Offer and Background Verification

If all rounds go well, an offer follows the final stage. Standard background verification applies before the joining date is confirmed.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

What Veeva Actually Looks For

Life Sciences Domain Awareness

You do not need pharma work experience, but you need to understand how life sciences businesses operate. Knowing what a field force does, how regulatory submissions work, or what clinical data management involves gives you a clear edge. Candidates who treat this as a detail to pick up on the job often struggle in scenario-based rounds.

Customer-First Thinking

Veeva's business depends on customers renewing and expanding. They want people who measure success by customer outcomes, not by tasks completed. Come with specific examples of times you drove a real, measurable outcome for someone you served.

Ownership and Accountability

Vague answers about team contributions do not land well. Interviewers want to know what you personally decided, changed, or fixed. Prepare examples with concrete outcomes you can point to, not just processes you were part of.

Communication Clarity

This comes up consistently in publicly reported interview feedback, across engineering, consulting, and sales roles alike. If you cannot explain a technical decision in plain terms or structure a business problem clearly, it will show.

Speed and Pragmatism

Veeva values moving fast and deciding with conviction. Candidates who describe how they made a sound decision quickly, rather than waiting for perfect information, fit the culture well.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

How to Prepare for a Veeva Interview

1. Understand the life sciences context

Spend time learning how pharmaceutical and biotech companies operate. Focus on the commercial side (sales, marketing, medical affairs) and the R&D side (clinical trials, regulatory submissions). Veeva's publicly available blog and customer case studies are a practical starting point.

2. Know the Veeva product suite

Veeva CRM, Veeva Vault, Veeva Data Cloud. Read the publicly available product pages so you can describe what each product does and what customer problem it solves. You do not need to be certified, but you should not be learning the product names for the first time during the interview.

3. Prepare your STAR stories

For each of Veeva's four values (Do Right, Customer Success, Employee Success, Speed), prepare at least one specific example from your past work. Write them out and practice saying them aloud. Interviewers probe for detail, so surface-level answers will not hold up.

4. Practice the technical side

For engineering roles, standard data structures and algorithms preparation applies. Senior levels commonly involve system design discussions. For consulting and solutions roles, practice walking through an implementation approach for a hypothetical pharma client scenario.

5. Be clear on compensation

Veeva asks early. Know your current fixed CTC, your variable component, and your target range. Glassdoor and levels.fyi carry some India-specific Veeva data points, but sample sizes are limited, so treat any figures as rough indicators rather than firm benchmarks.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Culture and Values at Veeva

Veeva's four publicly stated values are Do Right, Customer Success, Employee Success, and Speed. These are not just listed on a careers page. Candidates who have read publicly reported employee reviews consistently note that these values come up directly in interviews and shape how decisions get made day to day.

Do Right means acting with integrity even when it costs business. Veeva has publicly chosen not to work with certain customers when the fit conflicted with this value. In interviews, expect questions about times you raised a concern, pushed back on something, or prioritized long-term trust over short-term convenience.

Customer Success means delivery is only valuable if the customer actually achieves the outcome they needed. Completing a project on time but leaving a dissatisfied customer is not a win here.

Employee Success reflects Veeva's stated belief that treating people well is good for business. Publicly reported reviews describe a culture that is demanding but low on internal politics and hierarchy.

Speed means moving fast, deciding with available information, and correcting course as you go. Long processes with no output are not rewarded here.

Veeva is also publicly known as a permanent work-anywhere company. For Indian candidates outside Pune or Hyderabad, this is a practical advantage worth noting when you apply.

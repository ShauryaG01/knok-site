01 Salary Overview

Salary Overview

Product Designer compensation in India varies by city, company stage, and whether the role is product, platform, or growth-focused. Fixed pay is quoted in LPA (lakhs per annum); variable pay and ESOPs are common at startups. Always clarify fixed vs CTC before negotiating.

04 Negotiation Tips

Negotiation Tips

  1. Anchor with data from 3+ offers or trusted peers at the same level.
  2. Negotiate fixed first; treat variable and ESOP separately.
  3. Ask for signing bonus if base is capped.
  4. Get the offer letter with break-up (basic, HRA, PF) before resigning.
  5. If competing offers exist, share timelines, not numbers, to accelerate decisions.
05 Total Comp Breakdown

Total Comp Breakdown

CTC typically includes: Basic, HRA, special allowance, PF employer contribution, gratuity provision, and projected variable. Ask for in-hand estimate after tax. ESOPs at startups may not appear in CTC, model dilution and vesting cliff separately.

09 How To Stand Out

How To Stand Out

1 Lead with outcomes, not deliverables Companies like DevRev, LendingKart, and Khatabook care about business results. In your case studies, connect design decisions to metrics, reduced drop-off, improved task completion, fewer support tickets. Even a rough before/after comparison carries more weight than polished screens with no context behind them.

2 Tailor your portfolio to each company's domain Applying to LendingKart? Lead with financial flows or onboarding journeys. Applying to Okta or AtomicWork? Highlight enterprise UX, role-based access screens, or internal tool design. A focused portfolio that mirrors a company's product is more convincing than one that only 'shows range'.

3 Demonstrate design system thinking Several B2B SaaS companies on this list are actively building or scaling design systems. If you have worked on component libraries, design tokens, or cross-team design standards, make that visible in your portfolio and during interviews.

4 Practise take-home exercises Series A-C startups commonly use short design assignments as a first filter. Practise exercises where you define the problem clearly, explore directions, and explain your final rationale, not just what you designed, but why you chose it over the alternatives.

5 Get your Figma files in order Many companies will ask for a shareable Figma link during portfolio review. Clean, labelled, well-organised files signal strong handoff practice, something every hiring team notices and remembers.

10 Application Strategy

Application Strategy

Prioritise by experience band first The salary bands reflect clear tiers of expectation. Before applying broadly, identify which band genuinely matches your experience and portfolio depth. Applying for a senior role with an entry-level portfolio can close the door for future applications at that same company.

Go deep on fewer companies Companies like Glance, DevRev, and Toddle receive high application volumes. A personalised cover note that references their actual product, a specific flow you found interesting, a design decision you noticed, stands out clearly against generic messages that say 'your exciting company and mission'.

Engage on LinkedIn before you apply Designers and PMs at these startups are often active on LinkedIn. A thoughtful comment on a design post, or a genuine reaction to a product launch, puts your name in front of hiring managers before your application even arrives in their inbox.

Apply early in the posting cycle Series A-C roles often get filled quickly or go on hold once a strong candidate pipeline forms. Applications submitted in the first two weeks a role is live typically get more attention than those that arrive later.

Follow up once, politely If you have not heard back within a week or two, one brief follow-up message to the recruiter or hiring manager is completely normal in Indian hiring culture. Q Questions

Frequently asked

Which companies here are a good fit for freshers or designers with under 2 years of experience?

Hyreo and Toddle have both posted multiple openings and are known to consider early-career designers who show genuine, well-explained portfolio work. HR-tech and EdTech startups often give junior hires broader scope than larger companies do, which means faster skill-building on the job. Focus your application on one or two strong case studies rather than trying to show volume across many weak ones.

Is Okta a good option if I want a more structured, MNC-style work environment?

Yes. As a GCC of a global identity management company, Okta Bangalore offers more structured design processes, clearer reporting lines, and a more predictable work rhythm compared to the startups on this list. The trade-off is that design decisions may go through more review layers, and the product roadmap is shaped at a global level. If you value stability, career ladder clarity, and process rigour over speed and autonomy, Okta is worth prioritising.

How important is Figma proficiency for Product Designer roles at these companies?

For B2B SaaS companies like DevRev, AtomicWork, and Zluri, strong Figma proficiency is a baseline requirement, they commonly ask for shareable Figma links during the portfolio review stage. Consumer product companies like Glance and Popclub rely on Figma too, but place equal weight on visual thinking and interaction quality. Across all companies in this list, the ability to explain your design rationale clearly matters as much as how polished your screens look.

Do these startups typically offer ESOPs, and should I negotiate for them?

Most Series A-C startups in this list, including Hyreo, DevRev, and AtomicWork, include ESOPs in the offer package. A commonly cited vesting structure in Indian startups is a one-year cliff followed by monthly vesting over a multi-year period. Whether ESOPs are worth prioritising depends on the company's growth stage and your own risk appetite, always ask about the strike price, vesting schedule, and whether a buyback programme exists before comparing offers.

How does Bangalore compare to Delhi for Product Designer openings right now?

Bangalore leads with 58 openings versus Delhi's 31, as of 2026-07-08. More importantly, Bangalore's openings skew toward product-first startups and SaaS companies, while Delhi's market includes a broader mix of enterprise and services-adjacent roles. For designers who want to work on consumer or B2B product-focused teams, Bangalore is the stronger market at this point in time.

What is a realistic hiring timeline from application to offer for these companies?

Based on commonly cited timelines in Indian startup hiring, fast-moving startups typically complete the full process, portfolio review, a design exercise, and two or three interview rounds, within a few weeks of first contact. GCCs like Okta may take longer due to additional approval layers and global coordination. Applying early in a role's posting cycle and following up politely can meaningfully improve where you sit in the pipeline.

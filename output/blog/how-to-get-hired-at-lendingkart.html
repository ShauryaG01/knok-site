01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Lendingkart is one of India's well-known fintech lenders, focused on giving working capital loans to small and medium businesses across the country. The company uses technology and data models to approve loans faster than traditional banks, which is why it hires a broad mix of engineers, data scientists, product managers, credit analysts, sales professionals, and operations staff.

As of early July 2026, Lendingkart has 72 open roles, signalling active hiring across multiple functions. Openings span technology, credit and risk, business development, collections, and customer support, so this is not a narrow push for one team alone.

Compensation, based on publicly reported figures on Glassdoor and levels.fyi, is generally competitive for a growth-stage fintech. The trade-off many candidates weigh is that Lendingkart offers exposure to real financial products and mission-driven work, even if pay may not always match the largest product companies in metro cities.

If you are looking to break into fintech or deepen your career in lending, credit, or financial technology, Lendingkart has active openings worth a serious look.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

The exact process can vary by role and level, but here is what candidates have commonly reported:

Resume and application screen. A recruiter checks whether your background matches the role's core requirements. For technical roles, there may be an automated skill filter before a human reviewer looks at your profile.

Recruiter call. A short introductory call to verify your experience, discuss your current and expected CTC, and confirm your notice period and location. Be ready with these details before the call.

Technical or functional round. This is the core assessment stage and looks different by role. Engineering and data candidates typically face coding problems, SQL questions, or a short take-home task. Credit and risk candidates are often asked to interpret financial statements, discuss loan products, or walk through a credit case. Business development candidates may be given a scenario and asked how they would approach it.

Managerial or panel round. One or two rounds with the hiring manager or a cross-functional panel. Expect a mix of behavioural questions ('tell me about a time you disagreed with your team') and role-specific depth questions. This round often decides the offer.

HR and offer discussion. A final call to align on compensation, start date, and background check documents. Lendingkart has been reported to move quickly at this stage once a decision is made internally.

The total timeline from application to offer can vary. Tech roles tend to be faster because of structured assessments, while senior or specialised roles may include an additional round with a business head.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Lendingkart generally looks for a mix of domain knowledge and the ability to work fast in a changing environment. Specific signals differ by function:

Technology and data roles. Strong fundamentals in your area (backend engineering, data science, ML, or product) and ideally some exposure to financial data or regulated products. Interviewers often probe how you think about data quality, edge cases, and building for users who may be first-time digital borrowers.

Credit and risk roles. Understanding of SME or retail lending, comfort reading financial statements, and awareness of RBI guidelines for NBFCs. The ability to make a credit call with incomplete information is valued here more than textbook knowledge.

Sales and business development. A track record of hitting targets, comfort working in field or hybrid setups, and the ability to explain financial products in plain language to business owners who are not finance experts.

Operations and support. Attention to process, accuracy under high volume, and clear communication. Lendingkart handles a large number of loan cases, so reliability matters more than flair in these roles.

Across all functions, the company tends to value people who take ownership rather than waiting to be directed.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Research the product before your first call. Know what Lendingkart actually does: working capital loans for small businesses, and the use of alternate data for credit decisions. Candidates who connect their own experience to this product context tend to stand out during screening.

For technical roles, practise SQL and Python. Data and analytics are at the core of how Lendingkart assesses creditworthiness, so even non-data engineers may be asked basic analytical questions. Data and ML candidates should review feature engineering and model validation concepts before the interview.

For credit and risk roles, brush up on NBFC basics. Understand how a working capital loan works, what NPA means, and how RBI classifies different lending entities. Being able to walk through a basic credit case confidently will carry you a long way.

Prepare your compensation numbers in advance. Recruiters ask about current and expected CTC early in the process. Know your numbers and be ready to explain them without hesitation.

Have two or three strong examples ready. Behavioural rounds will ask about ownership, handling conflict, and working under pressure. Pick examples where you made decisions, not just completed tasks assigned to you.

Apply to the right level. 06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Lendingkart positions itself as a mission-driven company working to give small business owners access to credit that traditional banks have historically overlooked. The internal culture, from what employees and former employees have commonly described, tends to be execution-focused and moves at a pace typical of a high-growth fintech.

Teams are generally cross-functional, meaning an engineer may work closely with a credit analyst or a product manager on the same problem. Decision-making is reportedly faster than at large banks or established NBFCs, which suits people who want to see their work go live quickly rather than wait through long approval chains.

The environment rewards self-starters. If you prefer a structured role where tasks are handed down step by step, this pace may feel uncomfortable. If you enjoy owning problems end-to-end, you are likely to find the work energising.

Lendingkart has offices across India, with a significant presence in Ahmedabad, where the company is headquartered. Some roles are hybrid or field-based, particularly in sales and collections.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does Lendingkart hire freshers or only experienced candidates?

Lendingkart does hire freshers, particularly for technology, operations, and support roles. For credit, risk, and senior business development roles, they typically prefer candidates with prior experience in lending or financial services. Always check the specific job description for the experience requirement before investing time preparing for that particular role.

How long does the hiring process take from application to offer?

Candidates commonly report that the process takes a couple of weeks to about a month, depending on the role and the team's hiring urgency. Technology roles with structured assessments tend to move faster than senior or cross-functional roles. If you have not heard back within a couple of weeks after an interview round, it is entirely reasonable to follow up with the recruiter by email or LinkedIn.

Is coding knowledge required for non-engineering roles at Lendingkart?

Not strictly, but some familiarity with data tools like Excel or SQL is helpful even for credit and operations roles. Lendingkart is a data-driven company, and interviewers across functions may ask how you have used data to make decisions in the past. Being comfortable with numbers and basic analysis will serve you well regardless of which function you are applying to.

What is the work-from-home or hybrid policy at Lendingkart?

Policies vary by role and location. Technology and product roles have been reported to offer some hybrid flexibility, while sales, collections, and operations roles are generally field-based or in-office. Confirm the specific arrangement for your role during the recruiter call, as policies can also change over time with business needs.

How competitive is compensation at Lendingkart compared to other fintechs?

Based on publicly reported data on Glassdoor and levels.fyi, Lendingkart's compensation is broadly in line with mid-sized fintechs in India. It may be lower than the top-paying product companies, but many employees cite the mission, the product exposure, and the pace of learning as real benefits worth weighing alongside the number when evaluating an offer.

My application has been sitting without a response. What should I do?

Try connecting directly with the hiring manager or a relevant team member on LinkedIn with a short, specific message about why you are interested and how your background fits the role. A targeted note referencing something concrete about the position tends to work far better than a generic connection request. Also make sure your resume clearly reflects the keywords from the job description, since initial filters are often automated.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Glean's hiring process typically moves through several stages, though the exact structure varies by role and team. Candidates on forums commonly describe a flow like this:

Stage 1: Recruiter screen. A brief call where the recruiter checks your background, explains the role, and confirms expectations on both sides. Come with a clear summary of your experience and a specific reason for interest in Glean.

Stage 2: Hiring manager conversation. A focused discussion on your past work, how you approach problems, and why this role fits your goals. For engineering roles this may include an early technical question; for business roles it leans toward your track record and way of thinking.

Stage 3: Technical or role-specific screen. For engineers, this is usually a coding or system design session. For product managers, expect a product sense or strategy discussion. For sales and customer success roles, expect scenario-based questions on handling enterprise accounts.

Stage 4: Full interview loop. A panel of interviews covering depth in your core skill set, cross-functional collaboration, and alignment with Glean's values. This is often the most intensive part of the process and may involve multiple back-to-back sessions in a single day.

Stage 5: Offer and reference check. If the loop goes well, a recruiter reaches out with an offer. References are usually checked at this stage or just before.

Total calendar time from first screen to offer varies by role and urgency. Enterprise AI hiring has picked up since 2024, so loops at companies like Glean tend to move faster than at slower-paced firms.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Glean hires people who can move fast, think clearly under ambiguity, and care deeply about the end user's experience. A few qualities come up repeatedly across their job descriptions and candidate feedback:

Strong first-principles thinking. Glean works on hard problems in AI and search. They want people who can reason from the ground up rather than copy a playbook.

Genuine ownership. This is not a company that rewards waiting for direction. They look for people who see a gap, form a point of view, and act on it.

Cross-functional fluency. Because Glean's product touches nearly every team inside an enterprise, employees often work across sales, engineering, product, and customer success. Comfort moving between these contexts helps.

Technical depth where it matters. For engineering roles, solid fundamentals in data structures, distributed systems, or ML are important. For non-technical roles, the bar is on understanding how software products work at a conceptual level.

Customer obsession. Glean sells to enterprise buyers who expect results. Anyone in a customer-facing role needs to show they prioritize outcomes over effort.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Learn the product hands-on. If you can access a Glean demo or trial, use it. Understand what problems enterprise search solves and how Glean's AI layer differs from basic keyword search. Interviewers notice when a candidate has actually thought about the product.

Study enterprise AI context. Read about how large companies manage knowledge sprawl across tools. Knowing why enterprises pay for a product like Glean gives you strong material for 'why this company' questions.

Prepare your stories carefully. Glean interviews lean on behavioral depth. Use a clear situation-action-result structure and pick examples that show ownership, impact, and learning from mistakes.

For engineers: practise coding and system design. Focus on problems involving search, indexing, or large-scale data retrieval if you can. Review distributed systems fundamentals. Candidates on forums mention that Glean's technical bar is high but fair.

For product roles: build a point of view on Glean's roadmap. Think about what features are missing, what enterprise customers ask for most, and how you would prioritise them. You do not need to be right, but you need to be specific.

Research Glean's competitive landscape. Know who the other players in enterprise AI search are and how Glean positions itself. This kind of preparation shows strategic awareness that stands out in later-stage interviews.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Glean is a product-first, fast-moving company built around the belief that AI can fundamentally change how people work. A few things shape day-to-day culture there:

High ownership, low bureaucracy. Teams are small relative to the scope of what they ship. People are expected to define their own work and push it forward without waiting for layers of approval.

Intellectual honesty. Glean values directness and candour. Disagreement is welcome as long as it is backed by reasoning. Candidates who hedge every answer or avoid committing to a point of view may struggle in interviews and on the job.

Customer outcomes as a north star. Whether you are in engineering or sales, success is measured by what customers actually achieve with the product, not just by internal metrics.

Collaboration across roles. Engineers sit close to go-to-market teams. Product managers work directly with large customers. The boundary between functions is deliberately thin so the company can move and learn quickly.

Growth mindset in AI. The field is moving fast, and Glean hires people who stay curious, update their views with new evidence, and are comfortable with the fact that what works today may be replaced by something better next year.

Frequently asked

How many open roles does Glean have right now?

As of July 2026, Glean has 146 open roles according to knok's jobradar data. The openings span engineering, product, sales, customer success, and design, so there is something for both technical and non-technical candidates. The number changes frequently as roles are filled and new ones open, so it is worth checking their careers page regularly.

Does Glean hire in India?

Glean is US-headquartered but has been expanding its India presence, with Bengaluru commonly cited as a hub for engineering talent. Some roles are listed as remote-friendly. Check each job description carefully for location requirements, as this varies by team and seniority level.

What is the salary range at Glean for Indian candidates?

Publicly reported compensation data for Glean India roles is limited given the company's stage and India headcount size. Glassdoor and levels.fyi list some Glean data points, but treat these as directional since sample sizes are small. Industry surveys suggest engineering compensation in Bengaluru varies widely by level and function. Always clarify the full package, including base, equity, and benefits, during the offer stage.

How long does the Glean interview process take from start to offer?

Candidates commonly report that Glean moves faster than many enterprise companies, particularly for roles the team needs urgently. The full process from recruiter screen to offer can span a few weeks or stretch longer depending on team availability and role priority. Staying responsive to recruiter messages and scheduling interviews quickly helps you stay at the top of their pipeline.

Is it hard to get into Glean?

Glean is a well-funded, high-growth AI company, so competition for roles is real. The technical bar for engineering is commonly described as high but not unusually so compared to other top enterprise AI firms. Preparation, a clear narrative about why Glean fits your goals, and demonstrated curiosity about the product will set you apart from candidates who apply broadly without tailoring their pitch.

What should I highlight if I am switching from a service company to Glean?

Candidates moving from IT services or consulting backgrounds should lean into any product-facing work, customer outcomes they drove, or instances of ownership beyond their defined role. Glean values people who think like owners, so reframe your service experience around the decisions you made and the impact those had, not just the tasks you completed. Showing genuine curiosity about Glean's product and the enterprise AI space will also help bridge the gap.

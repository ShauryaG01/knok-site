01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

UiPath is a global leader in robotic process automation (RPA), and its India offices in Bengaluru and other cities are among its most active hiring hubs. As of early July 2026, UiPath has 123 open roles spanning engineering, product, sales, customer success, and professional services. The company builds software that lets businesses automate repetitive tasks, so it naturally prizes people who think in workflows and genuinely want to make processes smarter.

Hiring at UiPath is structured but moves at a reasonable pace. Most candidates go through three to five rounds depending on the role. The process is largely virtual, which is useful if you are based outside a metro. Decisions tend to come within a couple of weeks of the final round, though timelines vary by team and seniority.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Stage 1: Application and resume screen
Apply through UiPath's careers page or a major job portal. Recruiters scan for keywords aligned to the job description, particularly around automation, RPA, or the specific domain (engineering, sales, and so on). Tailoring your resume to the role is not optional here.

Stage 2: Recruiter screening call
If shortlisted, a recruiter reaches out for a brief screening conversation. Topics covered: your current role, notice period, salary expectations, and interest in UiPath specifically. Keep answers concise and have a tight one-minute summary of your background ready before the call.

Stage 3: Hiring manager round
A video call with the manager for the open role. Expect questions about your day-to-day experience, specific projects, and how you handled challenges. For tech roles this round often includes a short problem or case. For sales and customer success roles, expect scenario questions about handling enterprise clients.

Stage 4: Technical or domain panel
For engineers, this round tests coding ability, system design, or RPA-specific knowledge covering UiPath Studio, Orchestrator, and attended versus unattended bots. For non-tech roles, a panel of two to three team members probes domain depth and cross-functional thinking.

Stage 5: Final round (culture and leadership fit)
A senior leader or cross-functional panel asks questions tied to UiPath's values around customer obsession, bold thinking, and inclusion. Behavioural questions using the STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result) work well here.

Offer and background check
Offers typically arrive within a week of the final round. A standard background verification follows before your start date.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

UiPath hires across a wide range of profiles, but a few themes appear consistently across roles.

Automation mindset. Even non-technical candidates benefit from understanding what RPA does and why businesses invest in it. Being able to speak intelligently about process automation, even at a high level, signals genuine interest rather than just job-hunting.

Domain depth. UiPath values people who are strong in their lane: engineers who can build and explain their architectural choices, sales professionals who understand enterprise procurement cycles, and customer success folks who can manage complex stakeholder maps.

Problem-solving under ambiguity. The company operates across many geographies and industries. Interviewers want to see that you can figure things out without a step-by-step playbook, and that you recover quickly when plans change.

Communication clarity. UiPath works with large enterprise clients where a single miscommunication can stall a deal or a delivery. Clear, structured communication is taken seriously at every level of the company.

Ownership and follow-through. Past examples of taking initiative, seeing something end-to-end, or recovering a project going off-track resonate well with UiPath interviewers across functions.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Learn the product before round one. Download UiPath Community Edition (it is free) and try building a simple bot. Even a basic understanding of UiPath Studio separates you from candidates who only read a summary of what the company does.

Map your resume to the job description. UiPath's job posts are specific. Pull out the top five requirements and make sure your resume addresses each one with a concrete example from your work history.

Prepare STAR stories. Behavioural questions are central to UiPath's process. Prepare six to eight stories covering: a time you led without authority, a project you rescued, a time you disagreed with a manager, and a customer situation you turned around. Keep each story under two minutes when spoken aloud.

Research the company's recent direction. UiPath has been expanding into agentic automation and AI-powered workflows. Being aware of this shift and having a view on where RPA is heading shows you are thinking ahead, not just responding to the job listing.

Practice system design or case questions. For engineering roles, revise distributed systems fundamentals and practice designing scalable pipelines. For business roles, practice structuring a go-to-market or account expansion case before your panel round.

Prepare smart questions to ask. Ask about the team's current challenges, how success is measured in the first six months, and how the India team interacts with global counterparts. These questions signal that you are evaluating the role seriously.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

UiPath describes itself as a company built by people who 'do what they say and say what they do.' In practice, this means the culture rewards directness and follow-through over internal politics and over-promising.

Fast but intentional. UiPath moves quickly on product and go-to-market decisions, but it is not chaotic. Employees are expected to make decisions with the information available and course-correct fast rather than waiting for perfect data before acting.

Customer-first thinking. Across every function, UiPath teams connect their work back to customer outcomes. In interviews and on the job, asking 'how does this help the customer?' is never the wrong question to raise.

Inclusion in practice. UiPath has publicly committed to building diverse teams, and its India offices reflect a mix of engineering, business, and support roles at various seniority levels. The interview panel itself is usually diverse in function and background.

Remote-friendly but collaboration-heavy. Most India-based roles operate in a hybrid or remote model, but UiPath's collaboration culture is strong. Slack, video calls, and shared documents are the default working environment. Being proactive about communication matters more than being physically present.

Growth mindset is expected. The automation industry is evolving fast with AI layered on top of RPA. UiPath values people who stay curious, upskill continuously, and do not treat their current knowledge as a ceiling.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

How many jobs is UiPath currently hiring for?

As of early July 2026, UiPath has 123 open roles across locations and functions. The mix includes engineering, sales, customer success, professional services, and corporate roles. The number changes as roles are filled and new ones open, so checking the careers page regularly or setting up job alerts is worth the effort.

Do I need to know RPA or UiPath tools before applying?

For engineering and implementation roles, hands-on knowledge of UiPath Studio and Orchestrator is genuinely important and will come up in technical rounds. For sales, marketing, and corporate roles, a conceptual understanding of what RPA does is enough to start. Either way, spending a few hours with the free Community Edition before your interviews gives you a real advantage over candidates who skip this step.

How long does UiPath's hiring process take from application to offer?

Most candidates complete the process in three to five weeks from first contact with a recruiter to receiving an offer, though this varies by team and seniority. Senior roles tend to have more rounds and take a bit longer. If you have not heard back within two weeks of a round, a polite follow-up email to the recruiter is perfectly appropriate.

What salary can I expect at UiPath India?

UiPath does not publish salary bands publicly. Publicly reported figures on platforms like Glassdoor and levels.fyi suggest compensation varies widely by role, seniority, and function. Come prepared with a clear expectation based on your current CTC and your own market research, since the recruiter screening will almost always ask. Negotiation is normal and expected at offer stage.

Is there a referral route to getting noticed faster?

UiPath does not commonly run walk-in drives. The most effective route besides a direct application is an internal referral, which puts your resume in front of a recruiter faster and signals a basic credibility check. LinkedIn is a practical place to identify UiPath employees in your second-degree network who might be willing to refer you before you apply.

What should I focus on for the final leadership round?

The final round is less about technical skills and more about fit with UiPath's values and your potential to grow with the company. Prepare concrete examples of taking ownership, influencing without authority, and handling situations where the right path was not obvious. Interviewers at this level also want to see that you have done your homework on UiPath's business direction and can clearly articulate why this role is the right next step for you specifically.

01 Overview

Overview

Agoda is a global travel technology company, part of Booking Holdings, with engineering teams spread across Asia and a growing presence in India. As of July 2026, Agoda has 299 open roles in India, making it one of the more active travel tech employers right now. The Engineering Manager role typically sits above senior individual contributors and covers a team focused on consumer-facing products, platform infrastructure, or growth engineering.

Candidates report a process that typically runs four to five stages: a recruiter screen, a hiring manager conversation focused on leadership style and past experience, a technical leadership discussion covering system design or engineering strategy, a cross-functional panel with product and design stakeholders, and a final leadership values conversation. The full process can take three to five weeks depending on team availability.

Salary data from knok jobradar puts the Manager band at 35-60 LPA, Senior Manager at 55-90 LPA, and Director at 90-150+ LPA. Agoda is well known for a data-driven, experimentation-heavy engineering culture, and that ethos shows up directly in how they assess Engineering Manager candidates.

02 Most Asked Questions

Most Asked Questions

These questions come up frequently, based on what candidates typically report from Agoda EM interview processes.

  1. How have you managed a team through a high-traffic product launch where downtime had direct revenue impact?
  2. Agoda operates at large scale across time zones. How do you keep a distributed team aligned without adding excessive process overhead?
  3. Tell me about a time you used data to make an engineering decision that your team initially disagreed with.
  4. How do you balance paying down technical debt against shipping new features in a fast-moving product environment?
  5. Describe how you have built psychological safety in a team with engineers from diverse cultural and educational backgrounds.
  6. Walk me through your hiring process. What does your ideal senior engineering hire look like, and how do you evaluate them?
  7. Tell me about a time you pushed back on a product or business stakeholder on scope or timeline.
  8. How have you run or supported an A/B experimentation culture within your engineering team?
  9. Describe an architecture or system design decision you made that had a significant business outcome.
  10. How do you handle an underperforming engineer? Walk me through a real example.
  11. Agoda values ownership and autonomy. How do you give engineers real ownership without losing visibility into delivery risk?
  12. Tell me about a time your team had to pivot quickly due to a sudden change, whether technical, product, or market-related.

03 Sample Answers (STAR Format)

Sample Answers (STAR Format)

Use the STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result) for behavioral questions. Here are three examples tailored to the Agoda context.

---

Q: How have you managed a team through a high-traffic launch where downtime had direct revenue impact?

*Situation:* At my previous company, we were shipping a flash-sale feature for a peak travel season. The feature would surface heavily discounted inventory to millions of simultaneous users.

*Task:* My responsibility was to ensure the team delivered on time and that our infrastructure could absorb the traffic spike without affecting checkout flows.

*Action:* I ran three load-testing cycles in the weeks before launch, looped in the SRE team early, and set up a war room with on-call rotations across time zones. I also worked with product to agree on a circuit-breaker plan: if error rates crossed a set threshold, we would throttle incoming traffic automatically.

*Result:* The launch went live without any major incident. The circuit breaker was never triggered. The team's confidence in handling scale grew noticeably, and we reused the same runbook for two subsequent launches.

---

Q: Walk me through a time you pushed back on product or business stakeholders on scope or timeline.

*Situation:* Our product team wanted to ship a new search-ranking feature in two weeks to meet a commercial deadline.

*Task:* I had to communicate clearly that the timeline would require cutting test coverage in ways that raised the risk of a ranking bug affecting conversion.

*Action:* I prepared a one-pager with two options: ship in two weeks with reduced coverage and a rollback plan ready, or ship in four weeks with full coverage and a phased rollout. I brought our QA lead and a senior engineer into the stakeholder meeting so the risk assessment came from the team, not just from me.

*Result:* The stakeholders chose the four-week plan. The feature launched cleanly, and the phased rollout caught a minor edge case before it reached all users. After that, the product team started pulling engineering into roadmap planning two sprints earlier.

---

Q: How do you handle an underperforming engineer? Walk me through a real example.

*Situation:* A senior engineer on my team was consistently missing sprint commitments and their code reviews had become a bottleneck for the rest of the team.

*Task:* I needed to address the performance issue without losing someone with deep knowledge of our payments systems.

*Action:* In a private one-on-one, I discovered they were repeatedly blocked by unclear requirements from the PM side and felt uncomfortable escalating. I set clear weekly goals with them, paired them with a mid-level engineer for structured accountability, and worked with the PM to fix the upstream requirements problem.

*Result:* Within six weeks, their delivery rate improved meaningfully and the review backlog cleared. They later led a payments refactor that shipped on time. The situation reinforced my habit of checking for upstream blockers before treating something as a pure individual performance issue.

04 Answer Frameworks

Answer Frameworks

STAR (for behavioral questions)
Most Agoda EM interviews lean heavily on behavioral questions. For each answer, set up the Situation briefly (one to two sentences), state your specific Task or responsibility, walk through your Actions in enough detail to show how you think, and close with a concrete Result. Avoid vague outcomes like 'the team improved.' Name what actually changed.

Data-first framing (for Agoda's analytical culture)
Agoda is known for its experimentation culture. When answering questions about decisions, lead with the signal or metric you looked at, not just the intuition. For example: 'We were seeing a drop in a key funnel metric, and that drove the decision to...' This signals that you are comfortable making and defending data-backed calls.

Situation/Complication/Resolution (for conflict or stakeholder stories)
For questions about disagreements or pushback, this three-part structure keeps the story crisp. State the situation, name the complication or tension clearly (not just 'we disagreed' but why), then explain how you resolved it and what you learned.

The ownership signal
Agoda interviewers pay attention to whether the candidate says 'I' or 'we' at the right moments. Own your decisions and your mistakes. Use 'we' for team outcomes, but make sure it is clear what you personally drove.

05 What Interviewers Want

What Interviewers Want

Technical credibility without live coding
Agoda EMs are not expected to write production code day-to-day, but interviewers want to see that you can engage meaningfully with system design, reason through architecture trade-offs, and earn the trust of strong engineers. Be ready to discuss a real architecture decision you influenced and explain why you made it.

People-first thinking, backed by specifics
Agoda is a people-heavy engineering organisation. Interviewers are listening for how you develop engineers, handle underperformance, and build team culture. Generic answers about 'mentoring' land flat. Specific stories with outcomes land well.

Data-driven decision making
This is core to Agoda's engineering identity. Candidates who default to gut instinct without tying it to metrics or experimentation results tend to score lower. If you have run A/B tests, set up dashboards, or used error rates to drive engineering decisions, surface those stories.

Cross-cultural communication
Agoda's teams are genuinely international. Interviewers pay attention to how you describe working across cultures, time zones, and communication styles. If you have managed or collaborated across geographies, prepare a specific example.

Bias for action, balanced with judgment
Agoda moves fast. They want managers who can make decisions under ambiguity and keep things moving, but who also know when to slow down and align stakeholders. Show both sides in your answers.

06 Preparation Plan

Preparation Plan

Week 1: Research and story-mining
Read Agoda's engineering blog (search 'Agoda engineering blog' to find it) to understand their technical priorities. Common themes include scale, experimentation, search, payments, and data. Then list eight to ten professional situations from your past that map to the questions above. For each, write out the STAR structure in bullet points before you practice speaking it aloud.

Week 2: Behavioural practice
Practice answering out loud, not just in your head. Record yourself for two to three questions per session. Focus on being specific and cutting filler phrases like 'I basically...' or 'kind of...'. Aim for two to three minutes per answer. Ask a peer to give you feedback on whether your outcomes sound concrete.

Week 3: Technical and domain prep
Review one to two system design problems relevant to travel tech: search ranking, availability calendars, pricing engines, or booking-flow reliability. You do not need to be the architect, but you should be able to discuss trade-offs at the EM level. Also prepare to explain how A/B testing and experimentation methodology work inside an engineering org.

Final days: Logistics and questions
Prepare four to five questions to ask your interviewers. Strong options: how the team measures engineering quality, what the biggest technical challenge is right now, and how engineering and product planning works in practice. Avoid asking about salary or benefits in early rounds. 07 Common Mistakes

Common Mistakes

Going too deep on individual contributor work
Many EM candidates spend too long explaining code they wrote or systems they built personally. Agoda interviewers want to know how you led, not just what you built. Keep technical detail to what is needed to show your decision-making, then pivot quickly to the people and process side.

Vague outcomes
Saying 'the team became more efficient' or 'morale improved' without any concrete signal reads as thin. Even if you cannot cite a precise metric, name what changed: 'Our cycle time on sprints dropped noticeably' or 'We went from consistently missing sprint goals to reliably hitting most of them.'

Ignoring the data angle
If you describe a major decision without mentioning what signal or metric drove it, you are missing what Agoda most wants to hear. Even for people decisions, tie your judgment to observable signals rather than instinct alone.

Sanitising your stories
Some candidates smooth over the hard parts of their stories to look good. Agoda interviewers are experienced and can tell when a story has been cleaned up too much. Naming the real tension, what you got wrong initially, or where the situation was genuinely difficult makes your answers more credible, not less.

Asking no questions
Not asking thoughtful questions at the end signals low interest. Agoda is a competitive employer with 299 open roles in India alone: they have options. Showing genuine curiosity about the team and the engineering challenges goes a long way.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

How many rounds does the Agoda Engineering Manager interview typically have?

Candidates typically report four to five rounds: a recruiter call, a hiring manager conversation, a technical leadership discussion, a cross-functional panel, and a final leadership or values conversation. The exact structure can vary by team and the seniority of the role. The full process typically takes three to five weeks from first contact to offer.

Does Agoda ask coding questions in the Engineering Manager interview?

Candidates generally report that Agoda EM interviews do not include live coding rounds. The technical focus is on system design, architecture trade-offs, and engineering strategy rather than writing code. That said, you should be ready to discuss technical decisions you have made in depth, since interviewers will probe your reasoning and judgment carefully.

What salary can I expect for an Engineering Manager role at Agoda in India?

Based on knok jobradar data, the Manager band in India is typically 35-60 LPA, Senior Manager is 55-90 LPA, and Director is 90-150+ LPA. Actual offers depend on your level, years of relevant experience, and the specific team you are joining. It is worth clarifying early in the process which level Agoda is hiring at, as this shapes both the compensation and the scope of the role.

How important is travel industry experience for this role at Agoda?

Candidates report that deep travel industry experience is not a strict requirement for Engineering Manager roles at Agoda. They prioritise engineering leadership fundamentals: team management, technical judgment, data-driven thinking, and cross-functional communication. That said, showing you have thought about the travel domain and can relate your past experience to their context (scale, seasonality, global users) does strengthen your candidacy.

What is Agoda's engineering culture like, and how does it affect the interview?

Agoda is known for a strong experimentation and data-driven culture, with a high degree of team autonomy. This shows up directly in interviews: expect questions about how you use data to make decisions, how you give teams genuine ownership, and how you balance speed with quality. Candidates who can speak specifically about running or supporting A/B testing and metric-driven development tend to do well.

Is it worth applying if I am based outside Bangalore?

Yes. As of July 2026, knok jobradar shows Agoda has 299 open roles in India across multiple cities. Engineering Manager roles may be open to candidates in major tech hubs or, in some cases, to remote applicants. It is worth applying and clarifying location flexibility during the recruiter call.

01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Grab started as a ride-hailing startup and has since grown into one of Southeast Asia's largest superapps, covering food delivery, digital payments, financial services, and logistics under one platform. Its India engineering hub, primarily in Bengaluru, handles significant product and infrastructure work for the wider group, making it a genuinely interesting place for Indian tech and product professionals to build a career. As of July 2026, Grab has 384 open roles listed on knok jobradar, spanning software engineering, data science, product management, operations, and corporate functions. That is a substantial pipeline, which means hiring is active across seniority levels. Competition for roles is real, Grab's brand and compensation attract strong applicants, but the process is structured and learnable if you invest time in preparation. Most India-based roles are in engineering and data, though product management, design, and business operations positions do come up regularly. The company hires both experienced professionals and, for select programmes, fresh graduates.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Grab's hiring process is structured and typically runs through the following stages. Timelines and exact steps can vary by role and team, so treat this as a representative path based on publicly reported candidate experiences. Stage 1, Application and resume screen
Your resume goes through an ATS or a recruiter screen. Matching your resume language to the job description matters here. Highlight measurable outcomes, not just responsibilities. Stage 2, Recruiter call
A short call, commonly reported as lasting around twenty to thirty minutes, to verify your background, notice period, and salary expectations. The recruiter will also explain the process and timeline. Have a clear, honest answer ready for why you want to join Grab specifically. Stage 3, Online assessment or take-home task
For engineering roles, this is usually a coding challenge on a platform like HackerRank. Data roles may receive a SQL or Python problem. Product and business roles often get a written case study or analytical exercise. This stage filters heavily, so take it seriously. Stage 4, Technical interviews (one to two rounds)
For engineers: data structures, algorithms, and, at senior levels, system design. For data roles: SQL, statistics, and product sense. For product managers: product case studies, metrics deep-dives, and prioritisation questions. Each round is commonly reported as lasting roughly an hour. System design questions at Grab often involve real-world scale, think ride matching, payment processing, or feed ranking. Stage 5, Hiring manager or cross-functional interview
A role-fit and behavioural conversation. Expect questions about how you have handled ambiguity, managed stakeholders, and driven impact in past roles. This round evaluates whether you are someone the team can work with, not just someone who can solve problems in isolation. Stage 6, Final round or panel (select roles)
Some senior or specialised roles include a final panel or a conversation with a senior leader before an offer is made. Offer and background check
Offers typically include base salary, performance bonus, and equity (RSUs for eligible levels). A background verification check follows the verbal offer. End-to-end, the process commonly spans two to four weeks. Grab recruiters are generally reachable, if you have not heard back within a week of completing a stage, a polite follow-up email is appropriate.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Grab evaluates candidates across a few consistent dimensions, regardless of the specific role: Ownership and initiative
Grab's culture prizes people who take full responsibility for outcomes, not just tasks. Interviewers probe for whether you identify problems proactively and follow through without being micromanaged. Data fluency
Whether you are in engineering, product, or operations, comfort with data is expected. Engineers are expected to reason about system behaviour at scale. Product managers are expected to define and track metrics clearly. Ops roles need analytical rigour. Cross-functional collaboration
Grab's teams span multiple countries and time zones. Your ability to work across engineering, design, business, and operations, often without formal authority, is valued highly. Customer and market empathy
Grab's products serve everyday users across diverse Southeast Asian markets. Interviewers want to see that you think about real people's experiences, not just technical correctness or feature completeness. Adaptability
The business operates in a fast-changing competitive landscape. People who get stuck in rigid processes or struggle with ambiguity tend not to thrive here. Bring examples of how you have navigated uncertainty.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Know the product deeply
If you can access Grab's app, use it across multiple verticals, GrabFood, GrabPay, GrabMart. If you are outside Southeast Asia, study it through public demos, earnings calls from 2024 and 2025, and analyst write-ups. Understanding how the superapp model creates flywheel effects will help you in product and business conversations. Practice system design for scale
For senior engineering roles, system design is a major evaluation axis. Focus on high-traffic, real-time use cases: matching engines, payment processing, location services, notification systems. Practice drawing clear architecture diagrams and explaining trade-offs. Sharpen algorithms and data structures
Standard coding-round preparation applies. Focus on graphs, dynamic programming, trees, and sliding window problems, as these appear frequently in publicly reported Grab interviews. Build strong STAR stories
Behavioural questions follow the Situation-Task-Action-Result structure. Prepare three to five stories that demonstrate ownership, cross-functional impact, and handling of ambiguity. Quantify your impact wherever your past role allows. Research Grab's business direction
Read Grab's investor relations materials and news from 2024 to 2026. Understanding where the company is investing, financial services, advertising, AI-driven logistics, lets you ask sharp questions and show that your interest is genuine. Prepare smart questions
Interviewers at Grab consistently note that strong candidates ask thoughtful questions about the team's roadmap, how success is measured, and what the biggest challenges are. Avoid generic questions you could have answered with a Google search.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Grab's stated culture framework is built around three ideas: 'Heart, Hunger, and Honour.' In practice, this translates to caring genuinely about the people your product serves, a relentless drive to improve, and doing the right thing even when it is inconvenient. Day-to-day, the culture tends to be direct and results-oriented. People are expected to flag problems early, debate ideas openly, and move fast once a decision is made. Hierarchy exists, but Grab's environment is generally less formal than older enterprise companies, contribution is valued over title. The Bengaluru office works closely with counterparts in Singapore, Jakarta, Kuala Lumpur, and Ho Chi Minh City. This means a genuinely international working experience without needing to relocate. Teams routinely collaborate across time zones, so asynchronous communication skills matter. Grab's engineering culture in India is technically strong. Teams work on core infrastructure, machine learning platforms, mapping, payments, and consumer product features that reach tens of millions of users. If you want work that has visible, real-world impact at scale, that is a genuine draw here. Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does Grab hire freshers or only experienced professionals?

Grab primarily hires experienced professionals, but it does run graduate and early-career programmes for select functions. Most India-based roles require at least one to two years of relevant experience. If you are a fresher, look specifically for 'associate' or 'graduate programme' postings rather than applying to general engineering or product roles, where you will be competing against candidates with deeper experience.

What salary can I expect at Grab India?

Grab does not publish salary bands publicly, and sample sizes for India-specific data are small. Glassdoor and levels.fyi list figures for Grab India engineering roles, but verify these against current postings since compensation can shift significantly with seniority and team. Grab's packages typically include base salary, a performance bonus, and RSUs for eligible levels, so ask the recruiter to break down the total compensation clearly, not just the fixed component.

How long does the end-to-end hiring process take?

Based on publicly reported candidate experiences, the full process from application to offer commonly takes two to four weeks for most roles. Timelines can stretch longer for senior or specialised positions where multiple stakeholders need to align. If you are running a deadline, say, another offer, let the recruiter know early; Grab recruiters can often move faster when given a clear reason.

Is Grab's Bengaluru office fully in-office, hybrid, or remote?

Grab's India offices have moved to a hybrid model, with team norms varying by function and manager. Most roles expect some in-office presence during the week rather than being fully remote. Confirm the specific arrangement during your recruiter call, since expectations can differ between engineering, product, and operations teams.

Can I apply to more than one role at Grab at the same time?

Yes, and it is generally fine to apply to multiple roles if you genuinely fit each one. Be transparent with your recruiter if you are being considered for more than one team, they can sometimes coordinate internally so you are not duplicating interview effort. Applying to roles that are clearly misaligned with your profile just to increase chances tends to backfire, as recruiter notes are shared across teams.

What is the biggest reason candidates get rejected at Grab?

Based on publicly reported feedback, the most common rejection reasons are weak system design (for senior engineering roles), poor product sense (for PM roles), and generic behavioural answers that lack specific, measurable examples. Candidates who cannot clearly articulate the impact of their past work, not just what they did, but what changed because of it, tend to struggle in Grab's structured interviews. Preparation on these fronts meaningfully improves your odds.

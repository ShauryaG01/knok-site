Q Questions

Frequently asked

Will an AI job agent spam recruiters with my resume?

A reputable tool won't — it tailors each application and applies only to relevant roles, ideally with your review before anything goes out. The services to avoid are the ones that blast an identical resume to hundreds of unrelated postings, which burns your reputation with recruiters and lowers your response rate. knok, for example, requires your approval on outbound applications rather than auto-firing them.

What should I look for in a job-search agent?

Judge tools on five things: how broad their job-board coverage is (India plus global remote), the quality of their tailoring, how transparent they are about what gets auto-applied, whether they track your pipeline end-to-end, and how they handle your data. Data privacy matters especially in India — check for DPDP-aligned handling and confirm your resume and personal details aren't shared or sold.

How much time can automation realistically save?

Job seekers commonly spend 15–20 hours a week on repetitive browsing, form-filling, and follow-ups. Automation can reclaim most of the scanning and first-draft tailoring time, letting you redirect it to referrals, interview prep, and outreach — the parts that actually move offers. The catch is that you still need to review every application, so treat it as a force multiplier, not a hands-off autopilot.

Is it worth paying for a job-search tool?

It depends on your search intensity. If you have fewer than five target companies, a free spreadsheet and manual applications are plenty. If you need volume with consistent quality, a paid tool can pay for itself in a single faster placement — but trial it for about two weeks and measure your interview rate per 50 applications before committing to a subscription.

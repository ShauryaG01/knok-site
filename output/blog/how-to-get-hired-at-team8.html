01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Team8 is not a typical employer. It is a company creation platform that builds and co-founds deep-tech startups, primarily in cybersecurity, AI, and enterprise data. When you join Team8's platform team, you work alongside founders and operators building the next generation of B2B companies. When a role sits at one of their portfolio companies, you are joining an early-stage or growth-stage startup backed by Team8's network and expertise.

As of early July 2026, 81 open roles were listed across Team8's platform and portfolio companies. The positions span engineering, product, security research, business development, and operations. Because Team8 operates at the intersection of deep tech and enterprise sales, many roles ask for a blend of technical depth and business awareness, even for individual-contributor positions.

If you are looking to work at the frontier of cybersecurity or AI infrastructure, and you are comfortable with the pace and ambiguity of company building, Team8 is worth a serious look.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

The Team8 hiring process typically moves through several stages, though the exact flow varies by role and by which entity within the Team8 ecosystem you are applying to. What follows is based on commonly reported patterns; your experience may differ.

Initial screening. A recruiter or talent team member checks your background, motivations, and basic fit. Be ready to explain why you are interested in cybersecurity or deep-tech company building specifically, not just the role itself.

Hiring manager interview. You speak with the direct hiring manager or a senior leader. Expect questions about your domain expertise, past projects, and how you handle ambiguous problems. For technical roles, this may include a light technical discussion.

Technical or case assessment. Engineering candidates commonly face a coding exercise or system-design discussion. Business and product roles may receive a case study or strategy exercise. Team8 values practical problem-solving over textbook answers.

Panel or team interviews. You meet multiple team members across functions. Expect depth questions in your area as well as cross-functional questions about how you collaborate and communicate under pressure.

Leadership or founder conversation. For senior roles, a final conversation with a founder, partner, or C-level executive is common. This round focuses heavily on vision alignment and your ability to operate in a fast-moving, high-stakes environment.

Offers typically include equity, which carries real weight at a company builder. Clarify vesting schedules and cliff periods early so you can evaluate the full compensation package.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Team8 looks for people who are comfortable operating in environments where the problem space is still being defined. A few qualities that show up consistently in their hiring signals:

Deep domain expertise. Whether you are a security researcher, a product manager, or a data engineer, Team8 wants people who know their field well enough to spot problems that most people miss.

Startup operating instincts. They are building companies, not managing a steady state. People who have worked at early-stage startups, or who have led projects with high uncertainty and lean resources, tend to fit well.

Enterprise mindset. Team8's portfolio targets large enterprise customers. Understanding how big organisations buy, deploy, and measure security or data products is a genuine advantage, even in purely technical roles.

Clear communication. Because teams are small and cross-functional, the ability to explain complex technical ideas to non-technical stakeholders matters more here than at a large company with rigid swim lanes.

Curiosity and intellectual honesty. Team8 builds in fast-changing domains. They want people who read widely, change their minds when the evidence demands it, and bring new perspectives to hard problems rather than defaulting to received wisdom.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Before your first call, spend time understanding Team8's portfolio companies. Know which companies they have built, what problems those companies solve, and how each fits into Team8's broader thesis around cybersecurity and AI. This signals genuine interest and gives you context for every conversation in the process.

For technical roles, practise system design questions focused on security or large-scale data problems. Team8's engineering culture values architectural thinking, so be ready to discuss tradeoffs, not just solutions. Walk the interviewer through your reasoning step by step.

For product and business roles, prepare a specific point of view on a cybersecurity or AI problem you find genuinely interesting. Interviewers tend to respond well to candidates who have formed an opinion rather than candidates who give safe, generic answers.

Brush up on how enterprise software sales and procurement works, even if you are applying for a non-sales role. Knowing how a CISO or CTO evaluates a vendor purchase will help you frame your answers in terms the team actually cares about.

Finally, prepare questions that show you have thought about Team8's strategic bets. Asking about a specific portfolio company's go-to-market approach, or about Team8's thesis on an emerging threat category, will set you apart from candidates who ask only generic questions.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Team8 operates with a founding-team mentality. The culture rewards people who take ownership of outcomes, not just tasks. You are expected to bring your own perspective and push back when you see a better path, even if you are new to the organisation.

The environment is intellectually intense. Conversations move fast, the bar for rigour is high, and ambiguity is a constant companion. People who thrive here tend to enjoy the early, messy stage of building, when the right answer is not yet known and the stakes are real.

Team8 places a genuine emphasis on trust and long-term relationships. Their model depends on founders, investors, and operators working together over years, so interpersonal integrity and reliability carry real weight in hiring decisions and in day-to-day work.

Work-life balance varies by role. Positions closer to the platform tend to have more structure. Roles at early-stage portfolio companies can be more demanding, particularly around product launches or fundraising milestones. Ask directly about team rhythms during your interviews so there are no surprises.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

How many open roles does Team8 have right now?

As of early July 2026, 81 open roles were listed across Team8 and its portfolio companies. These cover a wide range of functions including engineering, security research, product, and business development. The number changes regularly as new portfolio companies launch and existing ones scale. Check Team8's careers page for the most current listings.

Do I need a cybersecurity background to get a role at Team8?

Not necessarily. Team8 hires across functions including finance, legal, talent, marketing, and operations, where a deep security background is not required. That said, even in non-technical roles, having a basic understanding of what cybersecurity or enterprise AI companies do will help you in interviews. For engineering and research roles, security or data domain experience is a strong plus.

What does compensation look like at Team8 companies?

Team8 does not publish salary bands publicly, and specific figures depend heavily on the role, seniority, and which entity within the ecosystem you are joining. Publicly reported data on platforms like Glassdoor and levels.fyi can give you a rough benchmark for comparable deep-tech or cybersecurity companies. Equity is often a meaningful part of the package at portfolio companies, so factor that into your total evaluation alongside the base.

Does Team8 hire candidates based in India?

Team8 is headquartered in Israel with offices in the US, and most roles are based there or are listed as global. However, some portfolio companies do hire remotely or have distributed teams across geographies. When applying, check the location details on each listing carefully, and reach out to the recruiter early to clarify whether they consider candidates based in India before investing time in the process.

How long does the Team8 hiring process typically take?

Based on commonly cited feedback for similar deep-tech company builders, hiring cycles can range from a few weeks to a couple of months depending on the seniority of the role and the pace of the specific team. Senior or specialised roles tend to take longer because they involve multiple stakeholder conversations. Keep following up politely after each stage if you do not hear back within a week or two.

Is it worth applying if I do not match every requirement in the job description?

Yes, within reason. Job descriptions at company builders like Team8 often reflect an ideal candidate rather than a strict checklist. If you meet most of the core requirements and can speak credibly to the domain, applying is worthwhile. What matters most is demonstrated expertise, intellectual curiosity, and the ability to operate in ambiguous, high-stakes environments.

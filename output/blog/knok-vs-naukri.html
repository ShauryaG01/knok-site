01 Overview

Overview

Naukri and knok both help you find a job, but they work in completely different ways. Naukri is India's most established job board. You search, you browse listings, you upload your resume, and you apply. Recruiters across India post here and actively search candidate profiles. It is familiar, free for basic use, and deeply embedded in how Indian hiring works.

knok is an AI job-search agent. You connect your resume once, and it checks 150+ job sites every night, applies to roles that match your profile, and messages HR on your behalf. You do not need to log in daily or hunt for listings.

Both can help a tech professional land a job. The question is which fits how you actually search, how much time you have, and what stage you are at in your career.

03 Best For

Best For

Pick Naukri if:

  • You want a free option and are comfortable doing daily search and apply
  • You are targeting non-tech or traditional sectors where Naukri's recruiter base is strong
  • You prefer writing a personalised note for each role rather than automated applications
  • You want your resume sitting in a database that Indian recruiters actively search every day
  • You are early in your career and want to explore what roles are out there without committing to a paid tool

Pick knok if:

  • You are in tech and want to apply to many companies at once without logging into each site separately
  • You are currently employed and cannot search every single day
  • You want an agent to message HR for you and keep your applications moving without manual follow-up
  • You want coverage across 150+ sites, not just one board
  • You would rather spend your time preparing for interviews than filling out application forms
04 Pros And Cons

Pros And Cons

Naukri

Pros
- Free for basic use, no monthly cost
- Strong recruiter presence: Indian hiring managers and HR teams post here regularly
- Resume database visibility means recruiters can find you even without you applying first
- Trusted brand with a long track record in the Indian job market
- Works well across industries, not just tech

Cons
- All the work is on you: searching, applying, and following up takes real daily effort
- Recruiter cold calls and inbox spam can become a problem once your resume is live
- Better profile visibility often requires paid upgrades to rank higher in recruiter search results
- Easy to miss good listings if you skip a few days
- Less useful if you want to look at opportunities outside India

knok

Pros
- Runs automatically every night across 150+ job sites, no daily effort required
- Applies to matching jobs and messages HR directly, so you stand out rather than sitting in an unread pile
- Shows you a match percentage for each job so you know how well it fits your resume before anything goes out
- Covers global listings alongside Indian boards, useful if you are open to remote or international roles
- Saves real hours each week for professionals who are currently employed and searching on the side

Cons
- Costs ₹2,500/month. It is not free like Naukri.
- Automated applications can feel impersonal for senior or highly specialised roles where a tailored approach matters more
- You give up some control: the agent decides what counts as a good match based on your resume
- Works best when your resume is polished and up to date. A weak or generic resume gives the agent weak material to work with.
- Newer in the market with less brand recognition compared to Naukri's long history in India

05 Recommendation

Recommendation

For most tech professionals who are actively looking, knok has a clear practical edge. It covers more ground, applies faster, and follows up with HR without you lifting a finger. At ₹2,500/month, that trade-off makes sense if even one extra interview call comes from it.

That said, Naukri is not a tool you should drop. Indian recruiters use it every day, and having your resume visible there costs nothing. The smartest approach is to keep your Naukri profile updated and active, and let knok run the search across 150+ sites at the same time. Use both, not one or the other.

If you are just starting out or testing the waters, begin with Naukri (free) and add knok once you are serious about landing interviews quickly. If you are already employed and searching on the side, knok removes the daily grind that Naukri demands.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Is knok better than Naukri for tech jobs?

For active tech job seekers, knok covers more ground by checking 150+ job sites nightly and applying automatically, which saves hours compared to manual searching on Naukri. Naukri is free and has strong recruiter presence in India, so it still matters for visibility. The best setup for most people is both: Naukri for recruiter inbound, knok for the active outbound search and HR messaging.

Can I use Naukri and knok at the same time?

Yes, and that is actually the approach most active job seekers end up using. Keep your Naukri profile active so Indian recruiters can find you directly, and let knok handle the nightly search and applications across 150+ platforms. They cover different ground and work well together rather than as substitutes for each other.

Is ₹2,500 per month worth it if Naukri is free?

It depends on how you search. If you are employed and cannot search daily, the time knok saves is usually worth more than ₹2,500 a month. If you have the time, enjoy the process, and are targeting a small number of specific companies, Naukri's free plan may be enough. One strong interview call from a role you would otherwise have missed often makes the month's cost easy to justify.

What if knok applies to jobs I do not actually want?

This is a real concern with any automated tool. knok matches jobs to your resume and shows a match percentage before applying, which gives you some visibility into what is being targeted. Keeping your resume focused and specific helps the agent apply more accurately. For senior roles or positions where you want to write a custom message, reviewing what goes out is still a smart habit.

My Naukri profile already gets recruiter calls. Why would I need knok?

Recruiter inbound on Naukri is passive: you receive whatever recruiters choose to send you. knok is active: it searches across 150+ sites and applies to companies that may never post on Naukri at all, including newer tech firms and global companies with India roles. If you are happy with the volume and quality of what you are already getting, you may not need to add knok. If you want wider coverage with less daily effort, it fills that gap.

Does knok work for senior tech roles or mainly mid-level?

knok works across experience levels, but the benefit is strongest for professionals applying to multiple companies at once. For very senior roles, hiring often happens through referrals and executive search, and a tailored personal approach usually matters more than volume. If you are at the senior individual contributor or mid-management level and applying to multiple companies simultaneously, the automation genuinely helps and saves you a lot of repetitive effort.

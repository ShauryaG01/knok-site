01 Overview

Overview

Naukri and knok solve different halves of the same problem. Naukri is India's largest job board: free for job seekers, tens of thousands of live listings across every industry, and a resume database that most Indian recruiters still search first. knok is an AI job-search agent: for $29/month (roughly ₹2,500), it searches 150+ job boards overnight — including Naukri-class sources — applies only to roles that genuinely fit your profile, and drafts outreach to hiring managers so you're not sitting in an ATS queue.

The honest framing: Naukri is a marketplace you work; knok is an agent that works for you. Naukri gives you maximum surface area but leaves the filtering, applying, and follow-up to you. knok trades breadth of industries (it's focused on tech roles) for time saved and application quality.

If you're a software engineer, data scientist, or product manager in India applying to 30+ roles a week while holding down a full-time job, this comparison matters. If you're hiring into non-tech functions — sales, operations, finance — stop reading and use Naukri.

03 Best For

Best For

Choose Naukri if:

  • You work outside tech — BFSI, manufacturing, sales, HR, operations. Naukri's non-tech depth is unmatched in India and knok simply doesn't cover those roles.
  • You're early-career and want volume. A 0–2 YOE candidate benefits from being visible in the recruiter database, even if half the inbound calls miss the mark.
  • Your budget is zero. Naukri's free tier is genuinely usable; you're paying with time, not money.

Choose knok if:

  • You're in a tech role — engineering, data, product, design — and your problem is time, not access. Ten hours a week of searching and form-filling is the tax knok removes.
  • You're currently employed and job-hunting quietly. knok runs overnight; you review a shortlist of high-fit roles instead of doomscrolling boards at midnight.
  • You care about application quality. Spray-and-pray on Naukri gets low reply rates; targeted applications plus hiring-manager outreach is how senior tech candidates (12 LPA and up, typically) actually land interviews.

Use both if: you're a tech candidate who wants full coverage. Keep a sharp Naukri profile so recruiters searching the database find you, and let knok handle outbound applications. They don't conflict — one is inbound visibility, the other is outbound execution.

04 Pros And Cons

Pros And Cons

Naukri — pros

  • Free, and the largest listing volume in India by a wide margin.
  • Recruiter database visibility: a well-tagged profile generates inbound calls without you lifting a finger.
  • Covers every industry, city, and seniority level — from fresher roles in Indore to CXO searches in Gurgaon.

Naukri — cons

  • Irrelevant recruiter calls are the most common complaint: profile keyword matching is loose, so a Java developer gets calls for QA contract roles.
  • Stale listings. Jobs stay live long after positions are filled, so a chunk of your applications go nowhere.
  • The default behaviour it encourages — mass-applying to everything vaguely relevant — produces low response rates and application fatigue.

knok — pros

  • Time. The agent searches 150+ boards overnight and applies only where fit is high; you review outcomes, not listings.
  • Fit quality: no applications to roles two levels below you or in the wrong stack.
  • Hiring-manager outreach drafts, which most Indian candidates never send — and which is often the difference between an ATS black hole and a reply.

knok — cons

  • It costs $29/month while Naukri is free. If you have more time than money, that matters.
  • Tech roles only. No coverage for non-tech functions.
  • Lower raw application volume by design — if your strategy is maximum shots on goal, knok will feel restrained.
05 Recommendation

Recommendation

For most Indian tech candidates the answer isn't either/or — it's sequencing. Keep your Naukri profile live and updated so recruiter-database searches surface you; that's free inbound and there's no reason to give it up. But if you're spending more than a few hours a week searching boards and filling application forms, that time has a cost — usually higher than ₹2,500 a month if you're earning 12 LPA or more.

That's the case for adding knok: it searches 150+ boards overnight, applies only to high-fit tech roles, and drafts hiring-manager outreach for each one. Run it for a month during an active search and compare reply rates against your manual Naukri applications — the data will settle the debate better than any comparison page.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does knok search Naukri listings too?

knok searches 150+ job boards overnight, including the large Indian aggregator sources where Naukri-class listings appear. In practice you shouldn't lose coverage of mainstream Indian tech openings by using knok — but keeping your Naukri profile live for recruiter-database visibility costs nothing and is still worth doing.

Is knok worth ₹2,500 a month when Naukri is free?

Do the math on your own time. If a manual search costs you 8–10 hours a week and you earn 12 LPA, that's several thousand rupees of time monthly on searching and form-filling alone. knok makes sense if your constraint is time and you're in a tech role; it doesn't if you're budget-constrained or hunting outside tech.

Why do I get so many irrelevant recruiter calls from Naukri?

Naukri's recruiter search is keyword-driven, so consultancies mass-call anyone whose profile loosely matches. You can reduce noise by tightening your headline and key skills to your actual target role, setting your expected CTC honestly, and removing outdated skills. It won't eliminate the calls, but it cuts the worst mismatches.

Can I use knok if I'm not in a tech role?

Not effectively — knok is built for tech roles: engineering, data, product, design, and adjacent functions. For sales, finance, operations, or HR roles in India, Naukri plus LinkedIn remains the right stack.

Which gets faster results for an active job search?

Neither wins on speed alone; they compound. Naukri gives immediate inbound visibility the day your profile goes live. knok's targeted applications plus hiring-manager outreach typically produce fewer but higher-quality conversations. In an active search, run both from day one and put your saved hours into interview prep — see our [PM interview questions guide](/blog/product-manager-interview-questions-india.html) if that's your track.

01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

CoreWeave is a GPU-first cloud infrastructure company built to run the world's most demanding AI and machine learning workloads. It started as a small crypto mining operation and scaled into one of the fastest-growing cloud providers in the US, now serving major AI labs and enterprises that need serious compute power.

As of July 2026, CoreWeave has 299 open roles across engineering, infrastructure, product, sales, and operations. Most positions are based in the US, with offices in New Jersey, New York, Austin, and San Francisco. Remote-friendly roles do exist, and a small number of international positions appear from time to time. If you are applying from India, filter specifically for roles tagged remote or global.

This is not a slow-moving big-tech environment. CoreWeave is in full scale-up mode, which means fast decisions, high ownership expectations, and a preference for people who can build and ship without needing a lot of structure. If you are an infrastructure engineer, systems programmer, networking specialist, Kubernetes expert, or cloud sales professional, this is one of the more compelling companies to target right now.

With 299 active openings, hiring is clearly moving fast. Getting your application seen quickly is half the battle.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

CoreWeave does not publish its full interview process publicly, so the stages below reflect what candidates have commonly reported on Glassdoor and Blind. Treat this as a realistic guide, not a guaranteed sequence.

Stage 1: Application and Resume Screen
Your resume goes through an initial filter, either automated or by a recruiter. Tailoring it to match the specific role (GPU infrastructure, Kubernetes, networking, or AI platform) gives you a real advantage over generic applications.

Stage 2: Recruiter Call
A short introductory call to confirm your background, location, availability, and salary expectations. Be ready to explain why CoreWeave interests you specifically, not just 'I want to work in AI.'

Stage 3: Technical Phone Screen
For engineering roles, candidates commonly report a coding problem or a deep conversation about past projects. Infrastructure and platform roles tend to focus on systems thinking, Linux internals, or networking concepts rather than purely algorithmic puzzles.

Stage 4: Technical Interview Loop
Candidates typically describe a few rounds covering coding, system design, and domain knowledge (GPU architecture, distributed systems, Kubernetes at scale). Senior roles get heavier system design questions. Some candidates report a take-home assignment replacing one of the live rounds.

Stage 5: Hiring Manager and Culture Conversation
A discussion with the hiring manager and sometimes a cross-functional partner. Expect questions about how you handle ambiguity, ownership, and working in a fast-moving environment without clear playbooks.

Stage 6: Offer
Once the loop closes, offers tend to move quickly. Total compensation at CoreWeave, based on publicly reported data on levels.fyi and Glassdoor, is competitive for the US market.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

CoreWeave is an infrastructure company at heart, so technical depth matters more than breadth. Here is what consistently shows up across their roles:

Deep systems knowledge
Whether it is Linux internals, GPU architecture, Kubernetes at scale, or high-performance networking (InfiniBand, RDMA), CoreWeave wants people who understand what happens below the abstraction layer. Surface-level cloud certifications alone are not enough.

Hands-on experience with GPU or AI infrastructure
Roles related to NVIDIA GPUs, CUDA, distributed AI training, or ML platform work are a natural fit. Even without an AI company on your resume, experience optimizing compute-heavy workloads is genuinely valued.

Kubernetes and cloud-native expertise
CoreWeave operates one of the largest Kubernetes deployments in the world. Experience with Kubernetes operators, cluster management at scale, or building services on top of Kubernetes is a real differentiator.

Ownership mentality
The company grew very fast with a lean team. They look for people who pick up problems without being told, ship things, and do not wait for a formal process to guide every decision.

Clear communication
For both customer-facing and technical roles, being able to explain complex infrastructure trade-offs clearly is valued. Written communication matters too, since the team is distributed across locations.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Research the company seriously
CoreWeave's engineering blog and NVIDIA partnership announcements give you real insight into what they are building. Be ready to speak to specific products and technical decisions rather than just saying 'AI cloud.'

Brush up on systems fundamentals
For engineering roles: Linux process model, memory management, TCP/IP networking, RDMA and InfiniBand basics, and how GPUs communicate across nodes. You do not need to memorize hardware specs, but you should be able to reason through trade-offs clearly.

Practice system design for infrastructure scale
Design questions at CoreWeave tend toward infrastructure scenarios: scheduling GPU jobs across large clusters, designing low-latency storage systems, or handling multi-tenant networking. Practice thinking these through out loud with explicit trade-off reasoning.

Know Kubernetes well
At minimum: how pods are scheduled, how operators work, how persistent volumes and CNI plugins behave. If you have operated large Kubernetes clusters, prepare concrete examples from your own experience.

Prepare your 'why CoreWeave' answer
Recruiter calls almost always include this. A strong answer connects the company's position in the AI infrastructure market to your own technical interests, not just compensation or brand recognition.

Look up recent interview reports on Glassdoor and Blind
Candidates who interviewed recently have shared question styles and topic areas. These are not guarantees, but they give you a real feel for the depth expected.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

CoreWeave has the energy of a startup that suddenly became critical infrastructure. A few things stand out about how they operate:

Speed over process
Decisions happen fast. There is not much committee approval culture. People are expected to make calls with the information available and course-correct as they go. If you prefer structured environments with clear approval chains and detailed playbooks, this may feel uncomfortable at first.

Engineering-led
The founding team comes from a deeply technical background. Engineering opinions carry real weight, and there is genuine respect for people who can go deep on a problem rather than just coordinate a team around it.

High ownership, high accountability
People tend to own entire domains, not small slices of a ticket queue. This is rewarding if you want to see the direct impact of your work. It also means there is less of a safety net when things go wrong.

Mission alignment around AI
A lot of people at CoreWeave are genuinely excited about being at the center of the AI infrastructure wave. The product does not feel like an afterthought in daily work. If you care about what large-scale compute enables, that energy shows up in how people talk about their roles.

Remote and distributed reality
CoreWeave has US offices, but remote roles exist. For candidates in India, most opportunities will be either fully remote or will require relocation. Be transparent with the recruiter about your situation early so you are not both wasting time.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does CoreWeave hire candidates based in India?

CoreWeave is primarily a US-based company and most of its 299 open roles are in US locations. Roles specifically listed for India are rare. However, some remote engineering positions open up from time to time that are location-flexible. Check their careers page regularly and filter for remote-tagged roles. If you are open to relocating to the US, your options widen considerably.

What salary can I expect at CoreWeave?

For US-based roles, levels.fyi and Glassdoor publicly report competitive total compensation packages that typically include a base salary, equity, and a performance bonus. For India-based or fully remote roles, publicly reported data is very thin given how few such positions have been posted historically. Discuss your expectations directly with the recruiter in the first call rather than assuming US market figures apply to your situation.

How long does the CoreWeave hiring process typically take?

Candidates on Glassdoor and Blind commonly report that CoreWeave moves faster than large established tech companies. The full loop from recruiter call to offer is often described as taking a few weeks rather than months. Having 299 active openings as of July 2026 points to strong hiring momentum, which typically means faster decisions. That said, timelines vary by team and role urgency, so follow up proactively after each stage.

Do I need a GPU or AI background to get hired at CoreWeave?

Not for every role. CoreWeave also hires for networking engineering, storage infrastructure, Kubernetes platform work, sales, and operations. That said, familiarity with GPU computing concepts and the AI infrastructure landscape is a plus even in non-GPU roles, because it helps you understand the customer and the product context. For core infrastructure engineering positions, GPU and high-performance computing knowledge is often expected rather than optional.

Is CoreWeave a good fit for early-career engineers?

CoreWeave's open roles skew toward mid-level and senior experience based on what is publicly visible in their listings. If you are a few years into an infrastructure, cloud, or systems engineering career with real hands-on experience in Kubernetes, Linux internals, or GPU workloads, you are in a realistic position to apply. Very junior candidates may find the technical bar high without a strong foundation in systems fundamentals already in place.

How do I stand out when applying to CoreWeave?

Tailor your resume to highlight infrastructure depth, not just familiarity with cloud services. Specific experience with Kubernetes at scale, NVIDIA GPU deployments, InfiniBand networking, or distributed AI training will get attention. In interviews, show that you can reason from first principles and discuss trade-offs clearly rather than just naming tools. A genuine answer to 'why CoreWeave' that ties to the AI compute market also helps you stand out from applicants who are simply applying broadly across the industry.

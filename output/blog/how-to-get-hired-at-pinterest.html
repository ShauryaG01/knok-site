01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Pinterest is the visual discovery platform used by hundreds of millions of people worldwide to find ideas for home decor, fashion, food, and travel. As of July 2026, Pinterest has 212 open roles spanning engineering, product, design, data science, marketing, and operations.

Pinterest hires primarily across its offices in San Francisco and New York, with some remote-friendly roles depending on the team. The company continues to invest in advertising technology, creator tools, and recommendation systems.

Getting hired at Pinterest is competitive, but the process is structured and transparent. Pinterest values people who are curious, collaborative, and genuinely excited about helping users discover things they love. A focused preparation plan gives you a real shot at landing a role here.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Pinterest's hiring process generally follows these stages, though the structure can vary by role and seniority level.

Stage 1: Application and Resume Screen
Apply through Pinterest's careers page and tailor your resume to the specific role. Recruiters look for relevant experience, clarity of communication, and evidence of working on products or systems at scale.

Stage 2: Recruiter Call
If your profile stands out, a recruiter will reach out for a short introductory call. This covers your background, what attracts you to Pinterest, and logistics like timeline and location. Be ready to explain your most recent role clearly.

Stage 3: Hiring Manager or Technical Screen
Depending on the role, this could be a coding screen for engineers, a portfolio review for designers, or a case discussion for product or marketing candidates. Engineers typically face algorithmic or system design questions. Product managers may be asked to walk through a product decision they made.

Stage 4: Virtual Onsite or Panel Interviews
This is the main evaluation stage, usually consisting of several rounds conducted virtually or in person. Rounds typically cover:
- Technical depth (engineering, data, or domain knowledge)
- Behavioural and situational questions
- Cross-functional collaboration scenarios
- A culture or 'life story' conversation with a panel member

Stage 5: Reference Checks and Offer
Pinterest typically checks references before extending an offer. Have former managers or colleagues ready to speak to your work. If selected, the recruiter walks you through the offer, covering compensation, equity, and benefits.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Pinterest looks for a blend of technical skill, user empathy, and a collaborative mindset. Here is what matters most across roles.

Technical or domain expertise: Engineers are expected to write clean, scalable code and think clearly about system design. Data roles require strong SQL and analytical thinking. Product managers need a track record of shipping features that users actually use.

User obsession: Pinterest is a consumer product. Interviewers often ask how you have made decisions grounded in user needs. Bring examples where you used user feedback or data to solve a real problem.

Collaboration and inclusion: Pinterest values diverse perspectives and looks for people who work well across functions. Describe how you have worked with people who think differently from you, and avoid framing past conflicts as entirely the other person's fault.

Curiosity and learning: Pinterest wants people who keep developing their skills. Be ready to talk about something new you taught yourself recently, whether technical or otherwise.

Impact and ownership: Show that you own outcomes, not just tasks. Quantify results from your own work using numbers you can speak to confidently.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Use Pinterest as a real user first: Spend genuine time on the platform before your interviews. Understand how the feed works, what makes content discoverable, and how advertisers fit into the experience. Interview panels can tell when a candidate has actually used the product.

Prepare your story bank: Pinterest interviews rely heavily on behavioural questions. Prepare clear examples for collaboration, conflict, failure, and impact using the STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result). Practice saying them out loud.

Read Pinterest's engineering blog: For technical roles, Pinterest's public engineering content covers how the company has approached recommendation systems, ad infrastructure, and image understanding. Referencing real problems Pinterest has solved makes you stand out.

Know every line of your resume: Interviewers often dig into projects you list. Be ready to explain each bullet point in detail, including the decisions you made and what you would do differently today.

Practice system design: For senior engineering roles, system design rounds carry significant weight. Practice designing systems relevant to Pinterest's domain, such as image search, content recommendation, or notification pipelines.

Prepare thoughtful questions: Each round usually ends with time for your questions. Ask about team roadmap, how success is measured in the role, or what the biggest technical challenge is right now. It signals genuine interest.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Pinterest's culture centres on a few themes that show up consistently in interviews and in how teams operate day to day.

Putting Pinners first: Decisions at Pinterest tend to circle back to what is best for the end user, called a 'Pinner'. If you can show that instinct in your work history, it resonates strongly with interviewers.

Authenticity: Pinterest encourages employees to bring their real selves to work. Interviews here tend to feel like honest conversations rather than high-pressure interrogations. You do not need to perform a 'polished candidate' persona.

Integrity: Pinterest places real importance on doing the right thing even when it is hard. Expect questions about ethical trade-offs or moments when you had to push back on a decision you disagreed with.

Continuous learning: The company invests in employee development and values people who are curious about their craft and adjacent fields. Talk about how you keep growing professionally.

Inclusion in practice: Pinterest has publicly committed to diversity and inclusion goals. Bringing specific examples of how you have actively made teams more inclusive, not just in words but in concrete actions, lands well with interviewers.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does Pinterest hire candidates from India?

Pinterest hires internationally and welcomes global talent, but most roles listed as of mid-2026 are based in the United States. If you are in India and targeting Pinterest, look specifically for roles tagged as remote-friendly. For most US-based roles, you would need to explore work authorisation options or relocation. Some contract or vendor roles through Pinterest's partners may be available in India.

How long does Pinterest's hiring process take from application to offer?

The process typically takes a few weeks from the time a recruiter reaches out to when an offer is extended, though this can vary by team, role level, and how quickly both sides move. The initial resume screen can take longer if the role has a high volume of applicants. Staying in contact with your recruiter and being responsive helps keep things moving.

What kinds of roles are currently open at Pinterest?

As of July 2026, Pinterest has 212 open roles. These span software engineering, machine learning, product management, data science, design, marketing, sales, and operations. The mix changes frequently, so it is worth checking regularly if a specific function interests you.

How important is the coding round for engineering roles at Pinterest?

The coding round is important, but it is not the only thing that matters. Pinterest engineering interviews are publicly reported to include both algorithmic problem solving and system design, with behavioural rounds weighted alongside technical performance. Strong problem-solving skills combined with clear communication and good instincts about user impact tend to produce the best outcomes.

Does Pinterest value side projects or open-source contributions?

Side projects and open-source work can strengthen your application, especially if they demonstrate skills directly relevant to the role. However, Pinterest's interviewers are generally more interested in the depth of your thinking and the impact of your work than in the number of projects you list. Quality and clarity of explanation matter more than volume.

What salary range can I expect at Pinterest?

Compensation at Pinterest varies widely by role, level, and location. Levels.fyi and Glassdoor show publicly reported figures that can give you a useful benchmark, but individual offers depend on your experience and the specific team. Pinterest compensation typically includes base salary, equity in the form of RSUs, and benefits. Research the level that matches your experience before your offer conversation.

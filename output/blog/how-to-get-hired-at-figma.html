01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Figma is the design collaboration platform that product teams use to build everything from early wireframes to final developer handoff, all in one shared space. As of July 2026, Figma has 179 open roles across engineering, design, product management, sales, and operations. Roles span experience levels, and Figma does hire internationally, so India-based candidates can apply for both remote-eligible positions and office-based roles.

Figma's hiring bar is high because the company builds tools that professionals rely on every day. They want people who bring craft, clear thinking, and genuine interest in how products feel to use. The process is structured and typically takes several weeks from first contact to offer.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

The stages below are drawn from publicly reported candidate experiences and may vary by team and role.

Stage 1: Recruiter Screen
A recruiter reviews your application and reaches out for a short call. They will ask about your background, what draws you to Figma, and your current situation. This is also a good time to ask about the role scope and team structure.

Stage 2: Hiring Manager Call
If the recruiter moves you forward, you will speak with the hiring manager. This conversation goes deeper into your past work, how you approach problems, and whether your goals match what the team needs. For engineers, expect questions about technical decisions you have made and how you handled trade-offs.

Stage 3: Technical or Portfolio Round
Engineers typically complete a coding or system design exercise, either take-home or live. Designers present a portfolio and walk through their process for a specific project. Product managers discuss case studies or work through a product exercise. Figma is known for valuing the thinking behind your work as much as the final output.

Stage 4: Interview Loop
This is a series of conversations with team members and cross-functional partners. Topics cover past experience, how you collaborate and handle disagreement, product sense, and role-specific depth. For technical roles, at least one session will focus on system design or domain knowledge.

Stage 5: Reference Check and Offer
Figma checks references before extending an offer. Have a handful of references ready who can speak to your specific contributions on real projects, not just your general character.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Figma hires across a wide range of functions, but a few qualities come up consistently in what they value.

Craft and attention to detail. Figma's product is a precision tool for people who care about how things look and behave. They want team members who hold the same standard in their own work, whether that is clean code, a clear decision document, or a tight visual design.

Clear communication. Because Figma teams are collaborative and sometimes distributed, the ability to write clearly and explain your thinking is highly valued. Candidates who can walk through their reasoning step by step tend to stand out over those who jump straight to conclusions.

Product curiosity. Even for engineering, sales, or operations roles, Figma values people who use design tools, think about user experience, and understand how product decisions affect real users. Using Figma before you interview is not optional.

Ownership and initiative. They want people who move projects forward without waiting to be told. Past examples of spotting a problem and driving a solution, even outside your formal job scope, will resonate well in interviews.

Collaboration without ego. Figma was built on the idea of working together in a shared space. They look for the same quality in their people: the ability to give and receive honest feedback, share credit, and work smoothly across disciplines.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Use the product before you apply. Create a free Figma account and spend real time in it. Build a simple frame, try developer mode, explore FigJam. Interviewers will ask what you think of the product and you want a genuine, specific answer rather than a vague compliment.

Study their public writing. Figma publishes design system talks, engineering blog posts, and conference videos. Reading these tells you how the team thinks and gives you language to use naturally in interviews.

Prepare specific project stories. Use a clear structure: what the project was, what your role was, what decision you made and why, what happened, and what you would do differently now. Vague answers like 'I helped a team ship a feature' do not land well.

For engineers: think about collaborative systems. Publicly reported candidate accounts suggest Figma engineering interviews touch on themes like real-time collaboration, browser rendering, and performance. Familiarity with these areas helps even if your role does not directly involve them.

For designers: document process, not just output. Interviewers reportedly want to hear what you tried and discarded, how you involved stakeholders, and how you handled conflicting feedback. Bring a project where you can speak to the full arc from problem to shipped solution.

Research your interviewers. Look up the people you will speak with on LinkedIn. Read anything they have published. Asking a specific question about their work signals genuine interest rather than last-minute preparation.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Figma describes itself as a company that believes design should be accessible and collaborative, not locked behind expensive software or siloed inside one person's machine. That philosophy shapes how the company runs internally as well.

Transparency. Figma shares context widely across teams. Employees report that decisions come with explanations and that leadership is accessible. If you do well in environments where information is open and goals are clearly stated, this culture will suit you.

Direct feedback. Giving and receiving honest feedback is a core part of how Figma teams operate. Employees are expected to ask for feedback often and deliver it constructively. If you come from an environment where feedback is rare or indirect, this will be an adjustment worth preparing for before joining.

High expectations with genuine support. Publicly reported employee accounts describe Figma as a high-performance workplace that also invests in people growing within the company. The pace is fast, but the expectation is sustainable output, not short sprints followed by burnout.

Inclusion as a practice. Figma has made public commitments to building a diverse team and conducts pay equity reviews, as described in their public diversity reports. For India-based applicants, the international hiring process follows the same structure as for candidates in other regions.

User obsession. Because Figma's customers are designers who have strong opinions about every interaction, the internal culture holds itself to the same high product standard. Teams are expected to know their users, read feedback carefully, and care about the details that most people would overlook.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does Figma hire people based in India?

Yes, Figma hires internationally and India-based candidates can apply for remote-eligible roles as well as office-based roles in locations where Figma operates. As of July 2026, there are 179 open roles at Figma across a range of functions. Check each job listing for location requirements before applying, as some roles are office-specific and not open to remote applicants.

What does Figma pay for engineering roles?

Figma does not publish salary bands for individual markets publicly. Glassdoor and levels.fyi carry compensation data for Figma engineering roles, but sample sizes for India-specific positions are small, so treat those figures as rough signals rather than reliable benchmarks. For remote roles tied to US-based pay structures, compensation is commonly cited as competitive with other top-tier product companies.

How long does the Figma hiring process take?

Based on publicly reported candidate experiences, the full process from recruiter screen to offer typically spans several weeks. The interview loop and reference check stage tend to be the longest part. If you have not heard back after a stage, a polite follow-up email to your recruiter is appropriate and expected.

Is there a coding test for engineering roles at Figma?

Publicly reported candidate accounts describe Figma engineering interviews as including both a coding exercise and a system design round. The format can vary by team and seniority level, with some candidates describing pair-programming style sessions. Practice writing clean, readable code and be ready to explain your approach and trade-offs clearly while working through the problem.

Do I need a design background to work at Figma in a non-design role?

You do not need a design degree or portfolio for engineering, sales, or operations roles. However, Figma expects all employees to use the product and care about how it feels to use. Coming into interviews with specific thoughts on what Figma does well or where it could improve will always give you an edge over candidates who have not engaged with the product.

How competitive is it to get hired at Figma?

Figma is a widely known and respected product, so competition for open roles is high. Industry surveys and publicly reported data suggest that acceptance rates at top-tier product companies are a small fraction of total applicants. The clearest way to stand out is a specific, well-documented track record of impact in your area, backed by concrete examples you can discuss in depth during the interview loop.

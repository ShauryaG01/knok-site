01 Overview

Overview

Braze is a B2B customer engagement platform trusted by enterprise brands to run personalised cross-channel campaigns across push notifications, email, SMS, in-app messages, and content cards. Their PM team owns the tools that marketing and growth teams rely on daily, so the interview process is designed to find candidates who can think across both the technical and business dimensions of a product.

As of mid-2026, knok's job radar tracks 252 open roles at Braze globally. The PM interview process typically spans multiple rounds. Candidates report being assessed on product sense, analytical reasoning, cross-functional collaboration, and deep familiarity with Braze's actual product surface. Preparation over several weeks is commonly recommended by candidates who have cleared the process.

02 Most Asked Questions

Most Asked Questions

Braze interviews go deep on their actual product. The following questions reflect patterns candidates report across recent interview cycles.

  1. How would you improve Braze's Canvas feature to drive adoption among enterprise customers?
  2. A client reports that push notification open rates dropped significantly after a Braze platform update. How do you diagnose and resolve this?
  3. How would you prioritise building new features for SMS versus email channels at Braze?
  4. Design a success metrics framework for a marketing team launching their first Braze campaign.
  5. Braze is considering expanding into a new vertical such as healthcare or fintech. Walk me through how you would evaluate the opportunity.
  6. How would you reduce churn among Braze's mid-market customers?
  7. A competitor releases a simpler, cheaper campaign tool targeted at Braze's customers. How should Braze respond?
  8. Tell me about a time you made a product decision with incomplete data. What did you do?
  9. How would you redesign Braze's onboarding flow to reduce time-to-value for new enterprise clients?
  10. Define what success looks like for a new AI-driven personalisation capability inside the Braze platform.
  11. How do you balance the needs of technical users (developers, data engineers) against non-technical marketing teams within the same product?
  12. Describe a time you had to align multiple stakeholders with conflicting priorities to ship a feature. What was your approach?

03 Sample Answers (STAR Format)

Sample Answers (STAR Format)

Use the STAR structure for all behavioural and situational questions: Situation, Task, Action, Result. Keep the first two parts brief and spend most of your time on what you personally did.

---

Q: Tell me about a time you made a product decision with incomplete data.

*Situation:* I was PM for a B2B SaaS analytics product. Our sales team was pushing to add a custom reporting feature, but we had almost no usage data to support the ask.

*Task:* I had to decide whether to add it to the next quarter's roadmap or defer it, while keeping the sales team aligned.

*Action:* I ran a quick discovery sprint. I reviewed support tickets mentioning reporting gaps, interviewed several churned accounts, and set up calls with enterprise prospects who had cited reporting as a concern. I also looked at publicly reported competitor benchmarks to understand what table-stakes reporting looked like in our segment.

*Result:* Discovery showed only a small subset of customers needed the full custom feature. We shipped a lighter-weight scheduled export first, which satisfied the majority of requests. The full custom builder was scoped for a later quarter with a clearer business case attached.

---

Q: How would you reduce churn among Braze's mid-market customers?

*Situation:* At a previous company, I owned retention for a mid-market SaaS segment that was seeing higher cancellation rates than the enterprise tier.

*Task:* My goal was to identify root causes and build a concrete retention improvement plan.

*Action:* I pulled exit survey data and mapped the customer journey. Most cancellations were happening early in the lifecycle, before customers had run their first successful campaign. I partnered with customer success and engineering to build an in-product onboarding checklist with progress tracking. I also introduced a health score for accounts that triggered automated check-ins when engagement dropped.

*Result:* Early-stage cancellations in that cohort dropped meaningfully in subsequent quarters. The health score model became a standard tool across all segments. Applying this at Braze, I would first map where mid-market customers stall, then focus product and CS investment on that specific friction point.

---

Q: How would you prioritise features for a new AI-driven personalisation capability?

*Situation:* At a previous role, I was building a personalisation engine for a messaging platform, similar in concept to what Braze has been investing in.

*Task:* I had to prioritise a backlog of possible AI features, balancing customer value, engineering complexity, and time-to-market.

*Action:* I used an impact-versus-effort matrix to narrow the list. I ran a customer advisory session with top accounts to validate which personalisation use cases would change their workflows most. I then sequenced the roadmap to ship a 'next best send time' recommendation first, since it had broad applicability and reused existing ML infrastructure.

*Result:* The first feature shipped on time and was adopted quickly by accounts that had participated in the advisory session. The key lesson: anchoring AI prioritisation to a specific customer workflow, rather than a general capability, drives adoption much faster.

04 Answer Frameworks

Answer Frameworks

For product design questions: Start with the customer. Who are they, and what are they trying to accomplish? Define the problem clearly before jumping to solutions. Finish by proposing metrics that tell you whether your solution worked. Braze interviewers value candidates who connect feature ideas to campaign outcomes that a marketer cares about, not just product-side engagement numbers.

For metric drop questions: Work through a structured root cause hierarchy. First rule out data or instrumentation issues. Then check for external changes, such as platform updates or policy changes by Apple or Google affecting push notification delivery. Then look at product-side changes like SDK updates or UI shifts. Braze interview scenarios in this area often involve mobile push or email deliverability, so knowing the basics of those channels gives you a real edge.

For prioritisation questions: Be explicit about the framework you are using. RICE (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort) is commonly cited in B2B SaaS contexts. Braze interviewers want to see that you weigh customer value against engineering cost and strategic fit, not just respond to whoever is loudest in the room.

For behavioural questions: Use STAR throughout. Keep Situation and Task to a few sentences each. Spend the majority of your answer on Action, describing what you specifically did rather than what the team did. End Result with a measurable or observable outcome and, where possible, a lesson you carried forward into future decisions.

05 What Interviewers Want

What Interviewers Want

Braze PMs work at the intersection of marketing technology, enterprise sales, and developer tooling. Candidates report that interviewers look for a consistent set of qualities.

Deep product knowledge of Braze. Interviewers notice quickly if you have not used the product or read their documentation. Know what Canvas, Content Blocks, Liquid personalisation, and Currents are before your first conversation. Surface-level familiarity will not hold up past the first question.

B2B and enterprise instincts. Braze's customers are marketing and growth teams at large companies. Interviewers want to see that you understand enterprise buying cycles, multi-stakeholder decisions, and the difference between the end user (a marketer) and the economic buyer (a VP or CMO).

Data fluency without data obsession. Braze runs on data, but interviewers also want PMs who can move forward with a clear hypothesis when data is thin. Triangulating from qualitative signals such as support tickets, customer calls, and churn interviews is as valued as quantitative analysis.

Cross-functional collaboration. The PM role at Braze involves close partnership with engineering, design, data science, and customer success. Interviewers look for specific stories about navigating disagreement and aligning teams, not generic claims about being 'a team player.'

Comfort with both the marketer and the developer persona. Braze's platform has a significant technical layer with SDKs and APIs. Candidates who only think about marketing users miss half the people that Braze PMs need to serve.

06 Preparation Plan

Preparation Plan

Week 1: Get inside the Braze product. Sign up for a Braze trial or watch their official product walkthrough videos. Read developer documentation for Canvas, Segments, and Currents. Look at published customer case studies to understand how real marketing teams use the platform day to day.

Week 2: Build your story bank. Write out several STAR stories from your past experience. Map each story to a PM competency: prioritisation, data-driven decisions, cross-functional conflict, customer discovery, and shipping under constraints. Practice telling each story concisely without rambling.

Week 3: Practice Braze-specific scenarios. Run mock interviews using the questions listed in this guide. For each product design question, practice writing a one-page brief (problem statement, customer, success metrics, top solutions) within a set time limit. Timed practice matters because Braze interviews move at pace.

Week 4: Research Braze's strategic context. Read their publicly reported product announcements and any analyst commentary on the martech landscape. Braze's annual customer conference (Forge) is a useful source of roadmap signals. Being able to say 'I noticed Braze recently launched X and I have a view on where that goes next' signals genuine interest rather than surface-level prep.

07 Common Mistakes

Common Mistakes

  1. Treating Braze like a consumer product company. Braze is enterprise B2B. Answers about 'delighting users' need to translate into business outcomes for the marketer's team and their end customers.
  1. Not knowing the product before the interview. Candidates report being asked about specific Braze features such as Canvas and Currents early in the process. Go in with hands-on familiarity, not just a high-level understanding.
  1. Skipping the 'why' in prioritisation answers. Listing features you would build is not enough. Interviewers want your reasoning: why this feature, why now, and why over something else on the backlog.
  1. Giving vague behavioural answers. Saying 'we improved metrics' is too generic. Describe the specific metric, your specific action, and be honest about the constraints and trade-offs you navigated.
  1. Ignoring the developer and data engineer persona. Braze's platform has SDKs, APIs, and data pipeline integrations. Candidates who only think about the marketer persona miss half the user base Braze PMs must serve.
  1. Not preparing questions for the interviewer. Braze interviewers commonly treat candidate curiosity as a signal of genuine interest. Prepare a few specific questions about their roadmap direction, team structure, or how they define PM success internally.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

How many rounds does the Braze PM interview typically have?

Candidates report the process typically includes a recruiter screen, one or more hiring manager conversations, a product sense or case exercise, and a final panel. The exact number varies by level and team. Confirm the full structure with your recruiter at the start so you can pace your preparation accordingly.

Is there a take-home assignment in the Braze PM interview process?

Candidates at some levels report receiving a written case or product brief exercise before the final panel. Typically this involves defining a product strategy, proposing metrics, or evaluating a feature trade-off. You are usually given a few days to complete it. Ask your recruiter early whether to expect one so you can set aside the time.

What salary can I expect for a PM role at Braze in India?

Based on knok's job radar data, PM salaries in India broadly range from 24-40 LPA for mid-level roles (3-6 years of experience) and 40-60 LPA for Senior PM positions. Group or Principal PM roles can reach 55-90+ LPA. Actual offers from Braze depend on your level, specific location, and how you negotiate, so treat these as a reference range rather than a guarantee.

Does Braze hire PMs in India?

Braze has a growing presence in India. As of mid-2026, knok's job radar shows 252 open roles at Braze globally. For India-specific PM openings, check Braze's careers page directly. Bangalore and Delhi are the two largest PM hiring markets in India overall, with 271 and 177 PM openings respectively tracked across all companies on knok's radar.

What background do Braze PMs typically come from?

Candidates report that Braze values PMs from martech, B2B SaaS, enterprise software, and developer tools backgrounds. A technical background in engineering or data is commonly cited as a strong advantage, given the platform's API-first architecture and the technical sophistication of Braze's customers. An MBA is not a strict requirement but is common among Senior PM candidates and above.

How should I follow up after a Braze PM interview?

Send a brief thank-you note to your recruiter promptly after each round. Reference a specific point from the conversation to make it personal rather than a generic message. If you have not heard back within the timeframe your recruiter mentioned, a single polite follow-up is appropriate. Candidates report that Braze recruiters are generally responsive to direct, professional communication.

01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Goldman Sachs is one of the most sought-after employers in global finance and technology, with a strong and growing presence in India. The firm's Bengaluru and Hyderabad offices handle everything from investment banking analytics and risk to core engineering and data platforms.

As of early July 2026, knok's job radar shows 287 open roles at Goldman Sachs across functions. These span technology, operations, finance, risk, and compliance, meaning there is real hiring momentum right now, not just a handful of niche openings.

Goldman Sachs hires at multiple levels: campus (analyst), experienced (associate and above), and specialist tracks in engineering, risk, and compliance. The process is rigorous but structured, so knowing what to expect at each stage gives you a genuine edge.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

The Goldman Sachs hiring process typically runs across four to six stages, though the exact sequence varies by function.

Step 1: Application and resume screen. You apply through the Goldman Sachs careers portal or via a referral. Recruiters screen for relevant experience, academic background, and skills that match the role. Roles in technology often include a short online coding assessment at this stage.

Step 2: HireVue or phone screen. Many candidates, especially for campus and early-career roles, receive a HireVue video interview. You answer pre-recorded questions about your background, motivation, and situational judgment. For experienced hires, this is often a brief recruiter call instead.

Step 3: Technical or skills round. For engineering roles, expect a coding round focusing on data structures, algorithms, and system design. For finance and operations roles, expect case-based questions, Excel or data exercises, and market knowledge checks.

Step 4: Superday (final rounds). This is the most intensive part: a series of back-to-back interviews, usually three to five panels, in a single day. Panels include senior employees, cross-functional leads, and sometimes a managing director. Expect a mix of technical, behavioural, and market questions.

Step 5: Offer and background check. Successful candidates receive a verbal offer followed by a formal letter. Background verification covering education, past employment, and references follows before joining.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Goldman Sachs looks for people who combine sharp analytical thinking with strong communication and genuine curiosity about markets or technology.

For technology roles, the firm wants candidates who can write clean, efficient code, think about systems at scale, and take ownership of problems end to end. Proficiency in Python, Java, or Go is commonly expected, and familiarity with distributed systems or cloud platforms strengthens your application.

For finance, risk, and operations roles, they look for quantitative rigour, comfort with ambiguity, and the ability to communicate complex findings to non-technical stakeholders. An understanding of financial instruments, risk frameworks, or regulatory environments is a strong differentiator.

Across all roles, Goldman Sachs values people who demonstrate initiative, handle pressure professionally, and work well inside large cross-functional teams. Behavioural questions often probe how you have navigated difficult situations, managed competing priorities, or influenced outcomes without direct authority.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Know the firm's business. Goldman Sachs is divided into three broad divisions: Global Banking and Markets, Asset and Wealth Management, and Platform Solutions. Understanding which division your role sits in, and what that division does commercially, impresses interviewers far more than generic finance knowledge.

Sharpen your technical skills early. For engineering candidates, practise data structures and algorithms on platforms like LeetCode, focusing on medium and hard problems. For finance roles, be ready to walk through a DCF or explain how interest rate changes affect bond prices.

Prepare stories using the STAR method. Goldman's behavioural questions follow predictable themes: leadership, resilience, teamwork, and ethics. Prepare four to five strong stories that you can adapt across these themes. Keep answers concise but specific.

Research recent news. Interviewers often ask about market events or recent developments in Goldman's areas. Follow financial news regularly in the weeks before your interview. Being able to reference a real event shows you are genuinely engaged with the industry.

Practise under pressure. The Superday format is designed to test how you perform across multiple rounds in quick succession. Do mock interviews with peers and simulate back-to-back sessions to build stamina and consistency.

Network before you apply. A referral from a Goldman employee significantly increases the chance that your resume gets reviewed. Reach out to alumni on LinkedIn, attend company events, and engage meaningfully rather than just asking for a referral outright.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Goldman Sachs operates with a 'client first' philosophy that shapes how teams prioritise work and make decisions. The firm is known for a performance-driven environment where results, speed, and precision are expected at every level.

Hierarchy and meritocracy coexist. The firm has a clear hierarchy from analyst to managing director to partner, but strong ideas get heard regardless of level, provided they are backed by data and delivered with confidence. Junior employees who show ownership early tend to move quickly.

Collaboration is real, not just a value statement. Because Goldman's deals and platforms span multiple teams and geographies, working across functions is a daily reality. Being a strong team player, especially across time zones, matters a great deal.

The pace is demanding. Publicly reported accounts from current and former employees consistently describe long working hours, especially in investment banking and trading roles. Technology and operations roles tend to offer more predictable hours, though crunch periods exist there too.

Diversity and inclusion are actively invested in. Goldman has launched publicly reported programmes focused on gender diversity and socioeconomic inclusion in India, with campus partnerships across engineering and management institutions in the country.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does Goldman Sachs hire freshers from Indian colleges?

Yes. Goldman Sachs recruits from IITs, IIMs, NITs, and a range of other engineering and management institutions across India for its analyst programmes. The campus process usually runs between August and December for the following year's joining batch. Off-campus applications for freshers are harder but possible if you have a strong profile and a referral.

How long does the Goldman Sachs hiring process take?

For campus roles, the process from test to offer typically runs two to four weeks. For experienced hires, it commonly takes four to eight weeks from first recruiter contact to offer letter, though roles in specialised areas like risk or compliance can take longer. Background verification adds another two to four weeks before a joining date is confirmed.

What salary can I expect at Goldman Sachs India?

Goldman Sachs does not publish salary bands publicly. Based on Glassdoor and levels.fyi data, compensation at the analyst level in technology roles in India is commonly cited as competitive within the financial services sector. Total compensation including bonuses varies significantly by division and individual performance, with experienced hires at associate level and above seeing a meaningful variable pay component tied to firm results.

Is a referral necessary to get into Goldman Sachs?

A referral is not required, but it helps. Many Goldman employees report that referred candidates receive faster recruiter attention, especially for experienced roles where hundreds of applications arrive for a single opening. That said, strong applications through the careers portal do get picked up. A referral improves your odds of being seen, not your odds of clearing the interview itself.

What is the Goldman Sachs Superday and how should I prepare for it?

The Superday is a full-day interview where you meet three to five Goldman interviewers back to back, usually in a single session. Interviewers come from different seniority levels and sometimes different teams. Prepare consistent, specific answers to behavioural questions and solid technical fundamentals, since you cannot predict which panel will ask what. Rest well the night before, because stamina and composure across all rounds matter as much as the content of any single answer.

Which Goldman Sachs offices in India are the largest?

Goldman Sachs has major operations in Bengaluru and Hyderabad, which together house the bulk of the firm's India-based technology and operations workforce. The Mumbai office focuses more on client-facing investment banking and markets roles. Most open technology and back-office roles currently visible on job portals are based in Bengaluru or Hyderabad.

01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Adyen is a global payments technology company that processes transactions for large retailers, airlines, and digital platforms. Headquartered in Amsterdam, it has offices across Europe, Asia-Pacific, and the Americas, with hiring open to candidates worldwide.

As of July 2026, Adyen has 253 open roles across software engineering, data, sales, account management, risk and compliance, and corporate operations. Indian candidates with strong technical or commercial backgrounds regularly compete for both local and international positions.

Adyen is publicly listed but keeps its teams deliberately lean, so each hire carries real weight. The bar is consistently high across all functions. Those who clear it join a company known for genuine ownership and direct impact on payment infrastructure used by some of the world's largest businesses.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

The Adyen hiring process typically runs across four to five stages. The exact sequence varies by role, function, and region, but here is what candidates commonly report:

StageWhat to expect
Application reviewRecruiters screen for relevant experience and skills. A tailored resume that closely matches the job description improves your callback chances.
Recruiter callA short video or phone conversation covering your background, interest in Adyen, notice period, and location preferences.
Technical or skills assessmentEngineering roles typically involve a coding challenge or take-home problem. Commercial roles may include a case study or written exercise.
Panel interviewsTwo to four rounds with team members, hiring managers, and sometimes cross-functional colleagues. Expect behavioural questions, role-specific deep dives, and questions on how you think through problems.
Offer and reference checkA verbal offer from the recruiter, followed by a formal offer letter and reference verification.

The overall timeline from application to offer commonly takes three to six weeks, though senior or specialist roles can move faster when the pipeline is smaller.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Adyen hires for a specific profile. Understanding it before you apply makes a real difference.

Ownership mindset. Adyen's teams are intentionally small, so they look for people who will take a problem from start to finish without waiting to be told what to do next. They want candidates who have actually done this before, not just claimed it on a resume.

Technical depth. For engineering and data roles, Adyen expects strong fundamentals: data structures, system design, and clean code. For commercial roles, they look for analytical rigour and the ability to explain complex products to clients in plain language.

Genuine curiosity about payments. You do not need a payments background to join, but you need to show that you understand why payments infrastructure matters and have taken the time to learn about it. Candidates who treat this as 'just another tech job' rarely progress far.

Direct communication. Adyen values straight talk. They look for people who can clearly explain their thinking, disagree respectfully, and avoid vague corporate-speak.

Independent collaboration. You are expected to work well with others across functions and also to operate without constant hand-holding. Adyen requires both, not one or the other.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Start with the Adyen Formula. Adyen publishes a document called the 'Adyen Formula' that explains their values and how they think about building a company. Read it carefully. Interviewers will probe whether you genuinely fit those principles, so prepare specific examples from your past that match them.

Know their product. Understand what Adyen actually builds: a single payments platform covering online, in-store, and app-based transactions. Know who their major merchant clients are and how their technology differs from traditional payment gateways. Candidates who can talk concretely about the problem Adyen solves consistently stand out.

Prepare your stories. For behavioural interviews, prepare three to five examples from your work history covering ownership, conflict resolution, working under pressure, and cross-team collaboration. Keep each answer clear: what the situation was, what you specifically did, and what the result was. Vague or generic answers get probed hard.

Practice technical fundamentals. If you are applying to engineering or data roles, practice problems covering arrays, graphs, dynamic programming, and system design. The Adyen bar is high, and interviewers care as much about how you think through trade-offs as whether you reach the correct answer.

Ask specific questions. Adyen interviewers appreciate candidates who ask thoughtful questions about the team's current challenges, how they measure success, or where the role is headed. Generic questions about culture or growth signal low preparation.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Adyen's culture is deliberately different from most tech companies, and they are open about that.

The Adyen Formula. This written document outlines how Adyen thinks about its culture, its people, and how it builds products. Interviewers actively reference it during hiring, and many behavioural questions are designed to test whether you naturally fit the values it describes. Reading it before your first interview is not optional preparation; it is essential.

Lean over bureaucratic. Adyen tries to avoid the kind of process and hierarchy that slows down large companies. Teams are small, meetings are kept to a minimum, and people are expected to get things done. If you need a highly structured environment with clear playbooks at every step, Adyen's style may feel uncomfortable.

Direct feedback. People are expected to say what they mean, give honest feedback, and receive it without becoming defensive. This shows up in interviews too: vague or evasive answers tend to attract follow-up questions until the interviewer gets a straight answer.

Long-term thinking. Adyen is not optimising for next quarter. They build for the long run and expect their people to think the same way. Quick fixes and short-term hacks are not celebrated here.

Genuinely global teams. Adyen's footprint spans many countries, and teams reflect that diversity. Indian candidates who have worked across cultures, or who are open to global collaboration and potential relocation, will find that experience valued.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does Adyen hire freshers or entry-level candidates?

Adyen does hire at entry level for some roles, particularly in engineering and business operations, but the majority of openings target candidates with a few years of experience. Entry-level positions tend to be more common out of the Amsterdam headquarters than from regional offices. If you are early in your career, focus on building strong fundamentals and look for Adyen's graduate or early-career programmes when they are listed.

What salary can I expect at Adyen in India?

Adyen does not publicly publish pay bands for India-based roles. Glassdoor and levels.fyi carry some self-reported figures for Adyen positions, though sample sizes for India-specific roles are small, so treat those numbers as directional rather than definitive. Your best approach is to research role-specific figures on those platforms and come into the offer stage with a clear number in mind based on your experience and current market data.

How long does the Adyen hiring process take from application to offer?

Most candidates report a total timeline of three to six weeks from first contact to offer, though this varies by role and how quickly both sides can schedule rounds. Technical roles with coding assessments can run slightly longer if scheduling takes time. If you have not heard back within two weeks of completing an interview round, following up once with your recruiter is perfectly reasonable.

What is the Adyen Formula and why does it matter for interviews?

The Adyen Formula is a written document explaining how Adyen thinks about culture, hiring, and building products. Interviewers at Adyen actively reference it, and many behavioural questions test whether you naturally fit the values it describes. Reading it before your first interview is considered baseline preparation. Candidates who reference it naturally during conversations tend to leave a stronger impression than those who have never heard of it.

Are Adyen roles open to remote work?

Adyen generally prefers in-person or hybrid working and is known for a strong in-office culture, particularly at its Amsterdam headquarters. Fully remote positions are not common at Adyen compared to other global tech employers. Check individual job listings for location requirements, as policies can vary by team and region. If relocation is not possible for you, filter your search accordingly before investing time in the interview process.

Do I need payments or fintech experience to join Adyen?

You do not need prior payments experience to get hired at Adyen, but you do need to show genuine curiosity about the space and a basic understanding of how payment infrastructure works. Candidates who treat the role as interchangeable with any other tech or sales job and have done no research on payments typically do not get past the first interview. A few focused hours of preparation on payments basics will meaningfully improve your chances.

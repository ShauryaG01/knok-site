04 Negotiation Tips

Negotiation Tips

  1. Anchor with data from 3+ offers or trusted peers at the same level.
  2. Negotiate fixed first; treat variable and ESOP separately.
  3. Ask for signing bonus if base is capped.
  4. Get the offer letter with break-up (basic, HRA, PF) before resigning.
  5. If competing offers exist, share timelines, not numbers, to accelerate decisions.
05 Total Comp Breakdown

Total Comp Breakdown

CTC typically includes: Basic, HRA, special allowance, PF employer contribution, gratuity provision, and projected variable. Ask for in-hand estimate after tax. ESOPs at startups may not appear in CTC, model dilution and vesting cliff separately.

06 Market Snapshot

Market Snapshot

Delhi NCR has 58 active QA Engineer openings as of July 2026, making it the second-busiest city in India for this role, behind Bangalore. What makes this list stand out is the range of employer types: a large German telecom GCC anchors the top, followed by a global robotics product company, a testing-tools startup, an IT services firm, and a cluster of early-stage companies in fintech, logistics, hospitality, and e-commerce. This mix is actually good news for QA professionals. You can choose between the structured career path of a GCC, the technical depth of a product company, or the fast-paced generalist work of a startup, all without leaving NCR. The market is active, but concentrated: the top two employers alone account for nearly half of all open roles, so knowing where to focus your energy matters.

09 How To Stand Out

How To Stand Out

Given the employer mix in Delhi NCR, a few skills show up consistently across these job descriptions: - Automation over manual: Every major employer here, GCC, product company, or startup, wants engineers who can write test scripts, not just run manual cases. Selenium, Cypress, Playwright, and Appium are the most commonly requested tools.
- API testing: Postman and RestAssured are close to table stakes at this point. If you cannot test REST APIs confidently, that is the first gap to close.
- CI/CD integration: Knowing how to plug your test suite into Jenkins or GitHub Actions puts you clearly ahead of candidates who stop at writing tests.
- Domain awareness: For Deutsche Telekom, telecom protocol familiarity helps. For GreyOrange, understanding warehouse and logistics workflows is a plus. For fintech startups like vegapay, payment flows and compliance edge cases matter a lot.
- ISTQB certification: Not mandatory everywhere, but GCCs often shortlist certified candidates faster, especially for senior and lead roles where process credibility is part of what they are hiring for.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Which company has the most QA openings in Delhi NCR right now?

Deutsche Telekom Digital Labs leads with 19 open roles, nearly a third of all QA positions in Delhi NCR. It is a GCC for the German telecom giant, which means stable hiring volumes and structured roles rather than one-off contract positions. GreyOrange is second with 10 openings, focused on robotics and warehouse automation software, a very different environment but an equally active one.

Is a GCC like Deutsche Telekom Digital Labs better than a startup for a QA career?

It depends on what you optimise for. GCCs offer structured growth paths, better job security, and exposure to large enterprise systems, ideal if you want to build deep process expertise or are earlier in your career. Startups give you broader ownership and faster promotions, but the role can be loosely defined and the position less stable. Many engineers spend time in a GCC first to build a strong foundation, then move to a product company or startup once they have the credibility and the skills.

Do I need ISTQB certification to get a QA job at these companies?

ISTQB is not a hard requirement at most of these employers, but it helps at GCCs like Deutsche Telekom Digital Labs, which tend to shortlist certified candidates faster for senior and lead roles. For product companies like GreyOrange and lambdatest, automation skills and a strong portfolio matter more than certifications. If you are targeting GCC roles and have the bandwidth, getting certified is a relatively low-effort way to stand out in a competitive shortlist.

Are there manual QA roles in Delhi NCR, or is everything automation now?

Some manual testing roles still exist, especially at startups that are early in building their QA practice, but the trend across all employer types is toward automation. Even roles listed as 'manual QA' often include automation skills in the growth-expectations section of the job description. If you are currently manual-only, learning one automation framework (Selenium or Cypress are the safest starting points) significantly expands the number of roles you can seriously apply for.

How does Delhi NCR compare to Bangalore for QA job opportunities?

Bangalore has 80 active QA openings versus Delhi NCR's 58, so Bangalore is larger, but the gap is not enormous. Bangalore skews more toward pure product companies and SaaS firms, while Delhi NCR has a stronger GCC presence thanks to employers like Deutsche Telekom Digital Labs. If you are specifically targeting GCC roles, prefer to stay in NCR, or want exposure to robotics and warehouse-tech domains, Delhi NCR is a strong market in its own right.

What should I include on my QA resume to get shortlisted faster?

Lead with your automation stack, name the specific tools and frameworks you have actually used (Selenium, Cypress, Playwright, Appium, Postman, RestAssured). Add a line about CI/CD integration if you have it, since that is increasingly expected even at mid levels. For senior roles, include bug-impact statements: not just 'found bugs' but 'caught a payment flow defect before release that would have affected checkout.' Tailor the framing to the company type, process and scale language for GCCs, ownership and speed language for startups.

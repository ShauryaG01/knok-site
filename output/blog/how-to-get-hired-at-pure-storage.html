01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Pure Storage is a US-headquartered flash storage and cloud data services company with a growing India footprint, primarily in Bengaluru and Pune. The company builds and sells enterprise storage hardware and software, and its India teams span software engineering, technical support, sales engineering, and customer success.

As of July 2026, knok jobradar is tracking 361 open roles at Pure Storage globally. That is a solid pipeline across multiple functions, giving serious candidates real options to find a role that matches their background, whether you are a fresher targeting support roles or a senior engineer with storage or cloud infrastructure experience.

Pure Storage is known for a structured, high-bar interview process. Glassdoor reviews commonly cite strong compensation, good career development, and a collaborative work environment as standout positives. The flip side is that the process demands thorough preparation, so knowing what to expect stage by stage gives you a real edge.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Pure Storage's hiring process typically runs across five or six stages. The exact sequence can vary by role and team, but candidates generally report the following flow.

Stage 1: Application and Resume Screen
Your resume goes through an ATS filter before a recruiter sees it. Tailor your resume to the job description, using the same keywords the posting uses. For engineering roles, highlight storage, cloud, networking, or relevant infrastructure experience. For sales or support roles, emphasise customer-facing outcomes and domain knowledge.

Stage 2: Recruiter Call
A short call where HR checks your basic fit: current location, notice period, compensation expectations, and interest in the role. Be clear and direct. Have a one-minute pitch on your background ready.

Stage 3: Technical or Functional Phone Screen
For engineering roles, this is usually a coding or system-level discussion with a hiring manager or senior engineer. For sales or support roles, expect scenario-based questions about handling customer problems or complex technical situations. Candidates publicly report this round as thorough and substantive, so treat it as seriously as the panel.

Stage 4: Technical Assessment
Some engineering tracks include an online coding test or a take-home problem at this stage. Difficulty is typically at the medium-to-hard level, sometimes with a system design component depending on seniority.

Stage 5: Panel Interviews (Virtual or On-site)
This is the main loop. Candidates commonly report a series of interviews covering deep technical questions (coding, system design, storage concepts), cross-functional collaboration scenarios, and behavioural questions using the STAR format. Interviewers at Pure Storage are known to dig deep, so surface-level answers rarely pass.

Stage 6: Offer and Background Check
If you clear the panel, the recruiter shares a verbal offer, followed by a formal letter after background verification. Negotiation is possible at this stage.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Pure Storage looks for candidates who combine strong technical fundamentals with a genuine customer mindset. Here is what matters most by function.

For Software Engineering Roles
- Strong fundamentals in data structures, algorithms, and system design
- Experience with storage systems, distributed systems, networking, or cloud infrastructure (preferred, not always required)
- Proficiency in C, C++, Python, Go, or Java depending on the team
- Ability to debug complex systems and communicate technical trade-offs clearly

For Sales and Sales Engineering Roles
- Deep understanding of enterprise IT infrastructure, ideally storage or cloud
- A track record of meeting or exceeding targets. Glassdoor and publicly reported profiles commonly cite quota performance as a key screening filter.
- Ability to run technical demos and explain complex products in simple terms
- Comfort working in a consultative, solution-selling model

For Technical Support and Customer Success Roles
- Strong troubleshooting skills, ideally with storage, SAN, NAS, or cloud environments
- Clear written and spoken communication, because you will be working with enterprise customers directly
- Patience and an ownership mindset when handling escalations

Across All Roles
Pure Storage consistently values intellectual curiosity, the ability to learn fast, and a collaborative approach. The company moves quickly, and interviewers specifically look for people who can handle ambiguity without losing momentum.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Understand the Product First
Spend time on Pure Storage's public resources: product pages, conference talks, and analyst commentary from publicly available sources. Knowing the difference between FlashArray, FlashBlade, and Portworx shows genuine interest and comes up in interviews.

Sharpen the Fundamentals
For engineering roles, revise data structures, algorithms, and operating system concepts. System design is a major component, especially for mid-to-senior roles. Practice designing distributed storage systems, caching layers, and file systems. Medium-to-hard difficulty problems on coding platforms are a reasonable benchmark, based on what candidates publicly report.

Prepare Strong Behavioural Stories
Pure Storage uses structured behavioural interviews. Prepare several stories from your past work using the STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result). Cover: handling a difficult customer, disagreeing with a colleague and resolving it, a time you drove a project with incomplete information, and a technical failure and what you learned from it.

Research Compensation Ranges
Levels.fyi and Glassdoor commonly cite compensation data for Pure Storage roles in India. Check these before your recruiter call so you enter salary discussions informed, not reactive.

Apply to Multiple Relevant Roles
With 361 open roles tracked by knok jobradar as of July 2026, there is a good chance more than one opening fits your profile. Apply to a few positions rather than just one, especially if you are open to different functions or cities. Optimise Your Resume
Keep it concise and lead with impact, not duties. Quantify outcomes wherever you can, and mirror the language used in the job description.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Pure Storage describes its culture around a few core ideas: be a disruptor, keep things simple, and put the customer at the centre. In practice, Glassdoor reviews and publicly reported employee accounts commonly describe the internal culture as fast-paced, collaborative, and merit-driven.

What stands out in India-based teams:
- Engineers in Bengaluru and Pune work on core product development, not just maintenance or localised support, which is a meaningful difference from traditional outsourced IT setups
- The company runs a relatively flat structure for engineering, meaning individual contributors get visibility and ownership early in their tenure
- Internal mobility is encouraged, and publicly reported profiles often show people moving between sales engineering, product, and support tracks over time

Diversity and Inclusion
Pure Storage has publicly committed to inclusion programmes and participates in diversity hiring initiatives. Glassdoor reviews commonly note that the environment is respectful and non-hierarchical by the standards of large enterprise tech companies.

Growth and Learning
The company invests in certifications, internal learning platforms, and conference exposure. For engineers especially, working on flash storage and cloud-native data infrastructure builds a niche skill set that industry surveys consistently rate as high-value in the broader job market.

Pace and Expectations
This is not a slow-moving company. Deadlines are real, and customers are enterprise-grade, which means quality standards are high. Candidates who thrive at Pure Storage tend to be self-driven, comfortable with ambiguity, and genuinely curious about infrastructure technology.

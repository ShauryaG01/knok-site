01 Salary Overview

Salary Overview

Frontend Engineer Salary in India (2026) Start with the uncomfortable reality: most Frontend Engineer job listings in India do not show a salary. Of the 384 openings tracked by knok jobradar as of 8 July 2026, pay figures appeared in only a small fraction of postings, so treat every band in this guide as a direction drawn from disclosed listings, not a guarantee. That said, the data tells a clear story. Frontend Engineer pay in India in 2026 spans from 5 LPA at the entry level to 58+ LPA for lead and staff-level engineers at well-funded product companies. The gap between a large IT services company and a funded startup for the same title can be large, which is why the bands for each level are deliberately wide. Stack matters too. Engineers strong in React, TypeScript, and performance tooling tend to sit at the higher end of each band. Owning features end-to-end, writing tests, and contributing to architectural decisions moves you further still.

Entry (0-2 years), ₹5-11 LPA
Fresh graduates and early-career engineers land here. Employers care most about fundamentals: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and at least one framework like React or Vue. A strong GitHub portfolio can push your offer toward 11 LPA even with limited full-time experience. Mid (3-5 years), ₹12-22 LPA
This is where pay accelerates sharply. Engineers at this stage are expected to own features end-to-end, write unit and integration tests, and mentor juniors. Moving from a services company to a product company is one of the fastest ways to reach the upper end of this band. Senior (6-9 years), ₹24-40 LPA
Senior engineers are expected to make and defend technical decisions, not just implement them. Performance optimisation, accessibility, and system-level thinking become real differentiators. The spread is wide because company type, funded startup vs. MNC vs. services firm, makes a significant difference. Lead / Staff, ₹38-58+ LPA
At this level, engineers who think like architects command the top of the market. Equity (ESOPs) and performance bonuses begin to outweigh base salary as a component of total pay. The '58+' ceiling is real for principal engineers at listed or late-stage product companies.

04 Negotiation Tips

Negotiation Tips

How to Negotiate Your Frontend Engineer Salary 1 Know your floor before the call.
Use the bands above to set your minimum, the number below which you walk away. Your target should sit higher than your floor. Walking in without a clear number puts you at a disadvantage from the start. 2. Let them name a number first.
If asked for expected salary early in the process, it is fair to say you would like to understand the full role before naming a figure. Many recruiters will share a band at that point, which is genuinely useful information. 3. Anchor on market data, not personal need.
'Based on market data for this level, I am targeting the upper end of the mid-band (12-22 LPA)' lands better than 'I need X because of my EMI.' Glassdoor and levels.fyi are good references to cite in the conversation alongside knok jobradar data. 4. Push on everything beyond base.
Variable pay, joining bonuses, stock options (ESOPs), and remote-work allowances are all negotiable, especially at senior and lead level. A joining bonus can bridge the gap when a company cannot move on the fixed component. 5. A professional counter rarely costs you the offer.
Industry surveys consistently show that most first offers have some flexibility. A single, evidence-backed counter, delivered with genuine appreciation, is standard practice and rarely ends a process.

05 Total Comp Breakdown

Total Comp Breakdown

Total Compensation: What to Look at Beyond CTC In India, CTC (Cost to Company) bundles several components that never hit your bank account. Here is what to scrutinise before you sign. Base Salary
The fixed monthly component. This is what most people mean when they say 'salary.' Maximise this relative to total CTC, especially early in your career, PF contributions and leave encashment are typically calculated on basic pay. Variable Pay
Also called 'performance bonus' or 'annual incentive.' Paid once or twice a year, conditional on your rating and on the company hitting its targets. At mid and senior levels, variable pay is commonly cited as a meaningful portion of total CTC at product companies, ask whether the full variable has actually been paid out in recent years, not just what is promised on paper. ESOPs / RSUs
At funded startups and listed tech companies, stock grants can add meaningful value over a multi-year vesting period. Ask about the vesting schedule, the cliff, and, for unlisted companies, the last known valuation before you factor ESOPs into your comparison. Joining Bonus
A one-time payment used when a company cannot match your current fixed salary. Usually subject to a claw-back clause if you leave within a year, read those terms carefully before accepting. Allowances and Benefits
Food coupons, leave travel allowance (LTA), health insurance for self and family, and internet or equipment reimbursements are standard. Remote-first companies sometimes add a home-office setup allowance. These are tax-efficient but should not distract you from negotiating the base. The real question: ask HR for the 'net take-home' or 'in-hand' figure so you know exactly what reaches your account each month.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Why do most Frontend Engineer job listings in India not show a salary?

Indian labour law does not require employers to publish pay ranges, unlike regulations in some other countries. Employers keep this information off listings to preserve negotiating flexibility and avoid anchoring candidates from different salary brackets. The practical effect is that you often walk into a negotiation without knowing the budget, which is why benchmark data like the bands in this guide matter.

Is there a real pay difference between product companies and IT services companies?

Yes, and publicly reported data and Glassdoor reviews consistently show it. Product companies, especially funded startups and global tech firms, tend to offer higher fixed salaries, meaningful equity, and faster annual pay growth than IT services companies for the same title and experience level. The trade-off is often job security: services companies tend to be more stable employers with steadier project pipelines.

Does the tech stack I know, React vs. Angular vs. Vue, affect my salary?

React dominates hiring volume in India, and engineers who combine it with TypeScript tend to see more interview calls and stronger offers. Angular retains a solid base in large enterprises and BFSI firms. Vue and other frameworks are niche but not penalised, depth of understanding and the ability to explain architectural trade-offs matter more than any specific framework name on your resume.

How fast does a Frontend Engineer's salary grow year over year?

Industry surveys suggest that mid-career engineers who move companies every few years tend to see faster pay growth than those who stay in the same role. Internal annual increments at most companies are modest by comparison. The jump from mid (12-22 LPA) to senior (24-40 LPA) typically happens either through a title change at the same company or a well-timed external move.

Should I count ESOPs when comparing two job offers?

For listed companies, ESOPs and RSUs are relatively straightforward to value using the current share price and vesting schedule. For unlisted startups, treat ESOPs as potential upside rather than guaranteed income, the shares only have value if the company reaches a liquidity event like an IPO or acquisition. Ask for the last known valuation, the vesting cliff, and how many shares are outstanding before factoring stock into your comparison.

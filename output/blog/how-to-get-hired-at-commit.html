01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

CommIT currently has 172 open roles as of July 2026, making this one of the stronger hiring windows to target if you have been watching the company. Openings span technology, consulting, operations, and support functions, so there is likely a fit whether you are an engineer, a business analyst, or a project coordinator.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

CommIT's hiring process typically runs across a few rounds, though the exact number varies by role and seniority. Here is what most candidates report going through:

StageFormatWhat to Expect
Application screeningResume review by HR or ATSA few days to two weeks
Initial HR callShort phone screenRole fit, notice period, salary expectations
Technical round(s)One or two rounds, online or in personDomain knowledge, problem-solving, coding or case work
Managerial roundConversation with a team leadOwnership mindset, past projects, cultural fit
Offer and verificationBackground check, document submissionOne to two weeks after verbal offer

For technical roles, expect coding problems or system design questions depending on the level. For non-technical roles, situational and behavioural questions are the norm. Interviews tend to be conversational rather than strictly formal, so being comfortable explaining your reasoning out loud matters as much as having the right answer.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Across most roles, CommIT's interviewers pay attention to a consistent set of qualities rather than just your resume line items.

Technical fundamentals. For engineering and IT roles, a solid grasp of core concepts matters more than knowing a specific tool. Interviewers want to see that you understand the 'why' behind your choices, not just that you can recall syntax or memorise steps.

Problem-solving approach. How you break down a problem is watched closely, not just whether you reach the right answer. Thinking out loud, naming trade-offs, and handling ambiguity without freezing are all signals interviewers look for.

Communication and ownership. CommIT looks for people who can drive their work forward and explain it clearly to both technical and non-technical stakeholders. Past projects where you owned an outcome end-to-end make strong interview material.

Collaborative mindset. Team fit carries real weight in the managerial round. Be ready to share examples of working through a disagreement or supporting a colleague, not just individual wins.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Start by mapping the 172 open roles against your skills. Filter by function, experience level, and city before applying broadly. A targeted application with a resume tailored to the job description will almost always do better than a spray-and-pray approach.

Resume tips. Lead with outcomes wherever possible. Use specifics from your own work such as project scope, team size, or timelines. Match your language to the words in the job description so your resume clears the initial screening.

Technical preparation. For software or data roles, brush up on data structures, algorithms, and any domain-specific frameworks the job description names. For consulting or operations roles, practice structured thinking and clear written or verbal communication.

Research the company. Read CommIT's recent announcements, understand their core business, and form a view on where things are headed. Candidates who ask sharp, informed questions in the managerial round leave a much stronger impression.

Mock interviews. Do at least one or two practice runs before your technical or HR round. Recording yourself surfaces filler words and unclear explanations that you would not notice otherwise.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

CommIT's work culture is broadly described by current and former employees as structured but approachable. Teams tend to be mid-sized, which means you get real ownership of your work without disappearing into a very large hierarchy.

Collaboration over competition. The internal environment leans toward knowledge-sharing rather than internal rivalry. Cross-team projects and peer learning are common, which suits professionals who enjoy working with a variety of people.

Ownership mindset. Employees are expected to move their work forward without needing constant direction. This suits self-starters and people who prefer autonomy. If you are early in your career and rely on close mentorship, ask specifically about how your team handles onboarding.

Growth and learning. Upskilling is encouraged, and many roles come with access to learning platforms or internal training. Certifications relevant to your domain are viewed positively during performance conversations.

Work-life expectations. Like most professional services environments, project deadlines can mean longer hours during crunch periods. Outside those peaks, the pace is more manageable. Ask about team rhythms in your managerial round to get a realistic day-to-day picture before you sign.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does CommIT hire freshers or only experienced professionals?

CommIT hires both. Entry-level openings typically appear under trainee, associate, or graduate labels. Campus recruitment is one channel, but off-campus applications through portals work too. Check the current 172 open roles and filter by experience level to see where you fit. Applying early in a hiring cycle tends to give freshers a better shot before the pipeline fills up with experienced candidates.

How long does the CommIT hiring process take from application to offer?

Most candidates complete the process in two to four weeks, though senior or specialised roles can stretch longer. The initial screening stage alone can take one to two weeks depending on volume. Following up politely with HR after each stage is acceptable and sometimes speeds things along without being pushy.

Is it better to apply directly on the CommIT website or through a portal like Naukri or LinkedIn?

Applying directly on the company website sends your application straight into their ATS without a middleman layer. That said, CommIT recruiters actively monitor portals like Naukri, LinkedIn, and Indeed as well. If the role matters to you, applying through both channels at the same time is a reasonable approach to maximise visibility.

What salary should I expect at CommIT?

Compensation varies widely by role, city, and experience level. For reliable benchmarks, check Glassdoor or AmbitionBox where CommIT employees have publicly reported their pay. Go into salary discussions armed with those numbers rather than accepting the first figure offered. Negotiating is normal and expected, especially if you have a competing offer.

Does employee referral actually make a difference at CommIT?

Yes, referrals are commonly cited as a faster path to getting screened at most mid-to-large companies, and CommIT follows the same pattern. If you know someone inside, ask them to submit a referral before or at the same time as your direct application. Even a brief LinkedIn message to a current employee introducing yourself and your background can help your resume get a second look.

What questions should I ask in the final interview round?

Ask about team structure, what the team is actively working on right now, and how success is measured in the first few months on the job. These questions signal genuine interest and give you real information to decide if the role is the right fit. Save questions about leave policies and perks for the HR conversation rather than the managerial round, where the focus should stay on work and growth.

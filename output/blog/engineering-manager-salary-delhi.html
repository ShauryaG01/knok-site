01 Salary Overview

Salary Overview

Most Engineering Manager listings in Delhi NCR arrive without a salary figure. Recruiters rely on 'discuss CTC later' conversations, so walking in without a benchmark hands them a clear advantage.

Knok's jobradar tracked 53 active EM openings in Delhi NCR as of 8 July 2026. The indicative salary bands across levels are:

These bands reflect what companies are actively hiring for right now, not survey averages from a year ago. There is real overlap between levels because different companies title the same role differently. A product startup may call someone a 'Manager' while a large GCC gives the equivalent scope a 'Senior Manager' title. Always benchmark against the job scope (team size, ownership, stakeholder visibility) rather than the title alone.

02 By Experience Level

By Experience Level

Manager (35-60 LPA) covers professionals who have moved from an individual contributor role and are directly managing an engineering team. Responsibilities at this level typically include sprint planning, hiring, performance reviews, and close collaboration with product managers. Pay within this band varies based on the company type (product startup versus GCC versus IT services), the domain, and the number of direct reports.

Senior Manager (55-90 LPA) is for leaders who manage other managers or lead a large cross-functional engineering organisation. At this level, the expectation shifts toward org design, resolving cross-team dependencies, and contributing to roadmap and hiring strategy. The bottom of this band overlaps with the top of the Manager band, which is why how you frame your current scope in interviews directly affects where an offer lands.

Director (90-150+ LPA) involves leading multiple engineering teams, owning a significant portion of the product or platform, and reporting into VP or CXO-level stakeholders. Roles at this level are fewer in number and frequently filled through referrals or direct outreach rather than job board applications alone.

04 Negotiation Tips

Negotiation Tips

Know the band before the first call. Recruiters often ask for your expected CTC in the screening round. If the role is at Manager level, the band is 35-60 LPA. Anchor toward the upper portion of that range if you have a clear track record of shipping products and growing teams.

Separate fixed from total comp. Many Delhi NCR companies structure packages with a variable component. Ask the recruiter to share the fixed component separately. A headline number that includes variable pay you may not always receive is not the same as a guaranteed base.

A competing offer is stronger than market data. Quoting publicly reported salary surveys helps frame the conversation, but a real offer in hand gives you a concrete lever. If you are actively interviewing, timing your offers to arrive close together is worth the effort.

Negotiate the title alongside the pay. Moving from Manager to Senior Manager in an offer costs the company nothing extra in headcount, but it shifts your next negotiation to the 55-90 LPA band. If the scope of the role justifies it, ask for the title change explicitly during the offer stage.

05 Total Comp Breakdown

Total Comp Breakdown

Engineering Manager compensation in Delhi NCR typically has several components beyond base salary.

Fixed base salary is what the salary bands (35-60, 55-90, 90-150+ LPA) primarily refer to. This is the number that forms the floor for all future negotiations and should be your first focus in any offer discussion.

Performance bonus is commonly cited at Manager level and above, linked to individual performance ratings and overall company results. The payout timing (quarterly versus annual) and the size of the variable component vary by company, so confirm both during the offer conversation.

ESOPs or RSUs are increasingly common at product startups and GCCs. Vesting periods are commonly structured over four years with a one-year cliff. Treat equity as upside rather than guaranteed income, since the final value depends on the company reaching a liquidity event like an IPO or acquisition.

Joining bonus is negotiable and is often used to offset unvested stock you leave behind at your current employer. If you have unvested ESOPs, raise this point explicitly before you sign.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Is the 35 LPA starting point of the Manager band realistic for a first-time Engineering Manager?

Yes, 35 LPA is the entry point for the Manager band based on current hiring data. A first-time EM transitioning from a senior individual contributor role at a product startup or GCC can realistically land within the 35-60 LPA range. The exact number depends on team size, domain (fintech and SaaS roles tend to pay more, based on publicly reported data), and the company type.

Do Delhi NCR Engineering Manager salaries match Bangalore levels?

The salary bands are broadly similar across both cities. The key difference is opportunity volume: Bangalore has 182 active EM openings versus 53 in Delhi NCR right now. Delhi NCR's market skews toward GCCs and enterprise tech, while Bangalore has a higher share of product companies that commonly offer more aggressive equity alongside the base.

Should I share my current CTC with the recruiter?

You are not required to share your current CTC, and in many cases doing so works against you. Instead, state your expectations based on the role scope and the relevant market band. Saying 'I am expecting a package in the upper portion of the Manager range for this role' anchors the conversation around the market, not around a percentage hike over your past salary.

What is the practical difference between a Manager and Senior Manager title for negotiation?

The Manager band runs 35-60 LPA and the Senior Manager band runs 55-90 LPA, with an overlap between 55 and 60 LPA. If the scope of a role matches Senior Manager level (managing other managers, org-level decisions, large team), negotiating for that title shifts the ceiling of your comp conversation from 60 LPA to 90 LPA. It costs the company nothing extra in headcount but meaningfully changes your long-term trajectory.

How much weight should I give to ESOPs in an Engineering Manager offer?

ESOPs are worth understanding carefully but should not replace a competitive base salary. At the Manager and Senior Manager level, ESOP grants can be meaningful over a vesting period, but the final value depends entirely on the company reaching an IPO or acquisition. Treat equity as upside on top of a base you are already comfortable with, and always confirm the vesting schedule and exercise price before signing.

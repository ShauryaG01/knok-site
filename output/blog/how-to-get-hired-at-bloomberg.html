01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Bloomberg is a global financial technology and media company best known for the Bloomberg Terminal, used by traders, analysts, and portfolio managers worldwide. The engineering and technology teams in India play a central role in building and maintaining the products that power global finance.

As of July 2026, Bloomberg has 141 open roles listed, spanning software engineering, data, product, and infrastructure. The company hires across experience levels, from fresh graduates to senior engineers, with a strong focus on problem-solving ability and genuine curiosity about financial markets.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Bloomberg's hiring process typically has several stages. The exact flow can vary by team and role, so treat this as a general guide.

Stage 1: Application and Resume Screen
Submit your application through Bloomberg's careers page. A recruiter reviews your resume for relevant experience, particularly in C++, Python, or financial systems. If shortlisted, a recruiter usually reaches out within a couple of weeks.

Stage 2: Recruiter Call
A short conversation with HR to discuss your background, interest in Bloomberg, and logistics like notice period and location. This is also your chance to ask about the role and the team you would be joining.

Stage 3: Online Assessment or Phone Screen
For engineering roles, you may receive a coding assessment covering data structures, algorithms, and sometimes a system design question. Difficulty is commonly reported as medium to hard on candidate review platforms.

Stage 4: Technical Interview Rounds
These are one-on-one sessions with Bloomberg engineers covering coding, problem-solving, and system design. Interviewers may also ask domain questions specific to financial systems or the product area you are applying to. Bloomberg typically runs two to three technical rounds at this stage, though some teams vary.

Stage 5: Hiring Manager or Final Round
A conversation focused on your experience, how you approach building software at scale, and your interest in Bloomberg's mission. Some teams include a behavioural component here as well.

The full process from application to offer can span a few weeks to a couple of months, depending on the team's hiring pace.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Bloomberg values engineers who write clean, efficient code and think clearly about system design. C++ is central to many of Bloomberg's core products, so strong C++ skills are a real advantage. Python skills are valued for data and analytics roles.

Beyond technical ability, Bloomberg looks for people who are genuinely curious about financial markets and understand why accuracy and reliability matter so much in this domain. A trading system cannot have bugs that go unnoticed, so attention to detail and rigorous thinking are qualities interviewers specifically assess.

Teamwork and clear communication matter too. Bloomberg's teams are collaborative, and interviewers pay close attention to whether you can explain your thought process while working through a problem, not just whether you arrive at the right answer.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Build your fundamentals first. Review data structures (arrays, trees, graphs, hashmaps) and common algorithms (sorting, searching, dynamic programming). Practise medium to hard problems on competitive coding platforms until you can solve them while talking through your approach out loud.

Know your language well. For engineering roles, brush up on C++ or Python depending on the team. Bloomberg's core codebase is heavily C++, so even working familiarity helps. For system design rounds, practise designing low-latency, high-availability systems, since Bloomberg operates in environments where speed and correctness are both critical.

Learn the domain basics. Study the fundamentals of financial markets: what a bond is, how equities are traded, what market data means. You do not need to be a trader, but showing genuine curiosity about the domain goes a long way in Bloomberg interviews.

Research Bloomberg's products. Understand what the Terminal does and who uses it. During your interviews, connect your technical answers back to real-world impact in financial systems wherever you can. This signals that you have thought seriously about why the work matters.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Bloomberg's culture is commonly described as high-standards and mission-driven. The company takes the reliability of its data seriously because financial decisions worth large sums depend on Bloomberg's accuracy every single day. This creates a culture where quality is non-negotiable and engineers are expected to take real ownership of what they build.

The work environment is structured but not rigid. Teams have autonomy to make technical decisions, and there is a strong emphasis on getting things right rather than shipping fast. Bloomberg also has a well-known philanthropic arm and a public commitment to diversity and inclusion, which shapes how teams are built and how people are supported day to day.

For Indian professionals, Bloomberg's offices offer exposure to global financial technology projects, often working directly with counterparts in New York and London. The scale and complexity of the systems you build here are genuinely hard to find at many other employers in the Indian market.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does Bloomberg hire freshers or only experienced professionals?

Bloomberg does hire freshers, primarily through campus recruitment and internship programmes. Entry-level roles typically require strong fundamentals in data structures and algorithms. Experienced hires are also common, especially for roles requiring financial domain knowledge or specialised systems experience.

How important is C++ for getting hired at Bloomberg?

C++ is central to Bloomberg's core engineering work, so it is a genuine advantage for many roles, particularly in terminal and data infrastructure teams. That said, Bloomberg also hires engineers skilled in Python, Java, and other languages for analytics, machine learning, and web-facing products. Check the specific role requirements before you apply.

What salary can I expect at Bloomberg in India?

Bloomberg does not publish salary bands publicly. Commonly cited figures on Glassdoor and levels.fyi suggest compensation is competitive within the financial technology sector in India. Salary varies significantly by role, experience, and location, so discuss the specifics directly with your recruiter early in the process.

How long does Bloomberg's hiring process take?

The process typically spans several weeks from application to offer, though it can extend to a couple of months depending on the team and role. Staying in regular contact with your recruiter is the best way to get timely updates and keep things moving. Having your documents and references ready in advance also helps avoid delays.

Do I need a finance background to work at Bloomberg?

A finance background is not required for most technology roles, but genuine curiosity about financial markets is a plus. Bloomberg looks for engineers who want to understand the domain they are building for. Basic familiarity with concepts like market data, trading, or financial instruments can set you apart from candidates who have not done this homework.

What are the most common reasons candidates do not get through Bloomberg interviews?

Weak fundamentals in data structures and algorithms are the most common stumbling block at the technical stage. Candidates who struggle to explain their thought process clearly also find it harder to pass. Bloomberg interviewers value how you think as much as the final answer, so practising talking through your approach matters as much as getting the right solution.

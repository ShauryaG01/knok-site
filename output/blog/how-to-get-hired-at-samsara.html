01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Samsara builds technology for physical operations, fleet management, driver safety, and industrial IoT. Now publicly listed, they serve industries that keep goods moving: logistics, construction, food and beverage, and field services. Their platform combines hardware (cameras, sensors, GPS devices) with cloud software that gives fleet managers real-time visibility over their operations. As of July 2026, Samsara has 251 open roles globally. For Indian professionals, the primary hiring hub is Hyderabad, with a growing number of remote engineering positions. Roles span software engineering, data science, customer success, sales engineering, and product management. Samsara is a high-growth US product company, the hiring bar is competitive, the process is structured, and they move relatively fast once you are in the pipeline. If you can show ownership, technical depth, and genuine interest in the problem they are solving, you have a strong shot.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

The process unfolds in stages and varies by role and level. Here is how it typically looks, based on publicly reported candidate accounts: Stage 1, Application and Resume Screen
Your application goes through an initial filter. Make sure your resume clearly shows outcomes rather than just responsibilities, and mirrors the language in the job description. Stage 2, Recruiter Call
A recruiter walks you through the role, asks about your background, notice period, and location preferences. This is a two-way conversation, ask about team structure, collaboration model, and what success looks like in the role. Stage 3, Technical or Role-Specific Screen
For engineering roles, expect a coding exercise, either asynchronous via an online assessment platform or a live session with an interviewer. For sales and customer success roles, this is often a scenario walkthrough or short written exercise. The difficulty is moderate; fundamentals and clear thinking carry more weight than algorithmic tricks. Stage 4, Panel Interviews
This is the core of the process. You will speak with multiple interviewers covering technical depth, system design (for senior engineering roles), and behavioural questions. Interviewers typically include your potential manager and cross-functional peers. Stage 5, Leadership or Final Round
Senior hires often finish with a conversation with a department leader. The focus is on culture fit, long-term thinking, and how you handle ambiguity under pressure. Offer and Background Check
Decisions typically come within days of the final round. A verbal offer is followed by written documentation and a background verification check before you officially join.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Samsara looks for a specific combination of traits regardless of which team you are joining: - Ownership and follow-through, they want people who take responsibility for outcomes, not just tasks. Expect interview questions that dig into what happened when things went wrong and what you personally did about it.
- Data-driven decision making, whether you are in engineering, sales, or operations, they expect decisions to be grounded in evidence. Answers that rely on 'gut feel' without any supporting data do not land well.
- Customer empathy, Samsara's end users are truck drivers, fleet managers, and field workers, not tech enthusiasts. Showing that you can think from a non-technical user's perspective is a genuine differentiator.
- Cross-functional collaboration, teams here are lean, so you will regularly work outside your immediate function. Stories showing you navigated across product, engineering, and operations together carry real weight.
- Systems thinking, especially for engineering roles, they value candidates who can reason about trade-offs in large systems, not just write clean code for isolated functions. For India-based candidates specifically, strong written English, experience working with globally distributed teams, and a record of shipping in fast-moving environments consistently stand out.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Know the product before your first call.
Samsara's platform is publicly documented and demoed. Understand what fleet visibility means in practice, what does a dispatcher see on their dashboard? What problem does a safety manager solve with the camera system? Interviewers quickly notice the difference between candidates who researched deeply and those who skimmed the homepage. Build strong STAR stories.
Behavioural interviews at Samsara focus on ownership, handling failure, and cross-team impact. Prepare specific stories where you can cleanly separate what *you* did from what the team did. Answers that credit 'we' without showing your individual contribution rarely satisfy interviewers here. Practise system design with real constraints.
For senior engineering roles, system design carries significant weight. Focus on problems relevant to Samsara's domain: real-time telemetry ingestion, GPS data processing at scale, reliable pipelines from edge devices to the cloud. Explain trade-offs clearly, there are no perfect answers, only well-reasoned ones. Understand the competitive landscape.
Samsara competes with companies like Geotab and Verizon Connect. Knowing why a fleet company would choose Samsara, and where Samsara's product is still evolving, signals genuine preparation and intellectual honesty that interviewers notice. Prepare sharp questions for each stage.
Ask how success is measured in the first few months, how India-based teams collaborate day-to-day with counterparts in other time zones, and what the biggest unsolved problem on the team is right now. Avoid questions you could answer by reading the website.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Samsara's publicly stated values centre on doing right by customers, moving with urgency, and thinking long term. In practice, publicly reported employee experiences describe a high-autonomy environment where you are expected to identify what needs doing and act without waiting for permission at every step. Pace and expectations
This is not a slow-moving company. The culture rewards people who take action, learn quickly from outcomes, and iterate. Glassdoor reviews commonly cite a high performance bar and meaningful, real-world impact as defining features of working here. Distributed and remote-friendly
Samsara has teams across San Francisco, Atlanta, London, and Hyderabad. The India team works in a distributed model with significant async communication. If you are used to centralised, office-first decision-making, expect a shift, autonomy is high and written documentation is valued over verbal instructions. Empathy for the end user
Despite being a technology company, Samsara's products ultimately serve people doing physical jobs, drivers, operators, and field teams. There is a consistent internal emphasis on empathy for end users, and this shapes how decisions get made across every function. Growth from within
For engineering roles especially, Samsara has a reputation for promoting people who consistently deliver. If you want a company where output matters more than tenure, this culture fits well. The full process from recruiter call to offer typically spans a few weeks, though it can move faster for urgent roles or extend for senior positions involving more decision-makers. If you have a competing offer or a hard deadline, tell the recruiter early, Samsara is known to accommodate reasonable timelines without penalising candidates for being upfront about it.

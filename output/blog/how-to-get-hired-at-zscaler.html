01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Zscaler is a cloud-native cybersecurity company built around zero-trust security. Its platform protects users, workloads, and devices without relying on traditional firewalls or VPNs. The company has a significant India footprint, with engineering and go-to-market teams spread across Bengaluru and Hyderabad.

As of July 2026, Zscaler has 358 open roles, spanning software engineering, security engineering, sales, customer success, technical support, and professional services. If you work in cybersecurity or cloud infrastructure, this is one of the more active hiring windows to watch.

Roles in India tend to cluster around product engineering, cloud operations, and pre-sales or post-sales technical positions. The company hires on a rolling basis, so applications submitted early in a cycle often move faster.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

The Zscaler hiring process usually runs across four to six rounds, though the exact number varies by role and team.

Stage 1: Application and resume screen
Your resume goes through an internal recruiter review. Engineering roles look closely at cloud platforms, networking knowledge, and security tools. Tailor your resume to match the job description before applying.

Stage 2: Recruiter call
A short call to check your background, salary expectations, and availability. Be ready to talk about your current role and why you are interested in cybersecurity or this specific position.

Stage 3: Technical screen
For engineering roles, this is commonly a coding round or a technical discussion covering networking concepts (TCP/IP, DNS, HTTP), security fundamentals (firewalls, proxies, PKI), or cloud architecture. Some teams use a take-home assignment instead.

Stage 4: Technical interviews (one to two rounds)
Deeper technical rounds with engineers or architects on the team. Expect system design questions, scenario-based security questions, or deep dives into past projects. For sales or customer success roles, this round is typically a presentation or a mock customer call.

Stage 5: Hiring manager interview
A conversation focused on your experience, problem-solving approach, and team fit. Behavioural questions based on the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) come up frequently here.

Stage 6: HR and offer
Final check on compensation expectations, joining timelines, and background verification. Background checks cover employment history and education and are standard across all roles.

The full cycle commonly takes two to four weeks from application to offer, though senior roles can run longer.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Zscaler hires people who understand the shift from perimeter-based security to cloud-native, identity-first security. Here is what consistently matters across roles:

Technical depth (engineering roles)
- Solid networking knowledge: TCP/IP, DNS, TLS, proxies, and firewalls
- Cloud platform experience: AWS, Azure, or GCP
- Programming in Python, Go, Java, or C++ depending on the team
- Familiarity with zero-trust concepts, SASE, or cloud security posture management (CSPM)

Security mindset
Zscaler looks for people who think adversarially, not just defensively. Whether you are an engineer or in a customer-facing role, showing that you understand attacker techniques and how to counter them stands out.

Customer orientation (non-engineering roles)
Sales engineers, customer success managers, and support engineers are expected to understand customer environments and translate technical concepts into business outcomes. Prior B2B SaaS or enterprise IT experience is a strong plus.

Communication
Clear written and spoken English matters across all roles, especially for positions that interact with global customers or cross-functional teams.

Ownership and initiative
Interviewers often probe for examples where you spotted a problem and drove it to resolution without being asked. Self-starters tend to do well here.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Know the product
Read Zscaler's documentation on ZIA (Zscaler Internet Access) and ZPA (Zscaler Private Access). Understand what problems they solve and how they differ from traditional VPNs. If you can explain zero-trust in plain language, you are ahead of most candidates.

Brush up on networking and security basics
For engineering and technical roles, expect questions on how HTTPS inspection works, how DNS filtering is applied, and how proxies intercept traffic. These topics come up frequently in Zscaler interviews based on publicly shared candidate experiences.

Prepare STAR answers
Choose three to five projects from your career that show clear impact. Structure each answer: what was the situation, what did you do, and what was the result. Include numbers where possible.

Research the team and role
Zscaler's LinkedIn page and engineering blog share details on what teams are working on. Look up your interviewers if you can and note their areas of focus.

Practice system design (senior roles)
For senior engineering positions, practice designing scalable, secure cloud systems. Focus on availability, fault tolerance, and least-privilege access.

Salary research
For compensation benchmarks, check Glassdoor or levels.fyi for Zscaler India roles. These platforms aggregate self-reported data and give a reasonable range for your level and function.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Zscaler operates with a stated belief that security should be invisible to end users but impenetrable to attackers. This shapes how teams work: speed of execution matters, but not at the cost of security or reliability.

Fast-moving and global
Teams in India often work closely with counterparts in the US and Europe. Comfort with async communication and occasional time-zone overlap is part of the job.

Learning-oriented
Cybersecurity changes quickly and Zscaler invests in certifications and internal training. Candidates who show genuine curiosity about new threat vectors or cloud-native architectures tend to fit well.

Performance-driven
Results are tracked, especially in go-to-market roles. The culture rewards people who set clear goals and hit them. If you thrive with clear accountability, this environment suits you.

Structured hiring process
Zscaler involves multiple interviewers in hiring decisions, which means the process takes longer than at smaller companies but tends to be more consistent and fair.

Stability with scale
Zscaler is a public company with a large enterprise customer base. For candidates moving from early-stage startups, the trade-off is more process in exchange for stability and strong brand recognition.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

How many jobs is Zscaler hiring for right now?

As of July 2026, Zscaler has 358 open roles globally, with a strong India presence across Bengaluru and Hyderabad. Roles span engineering, sales, customer success, and support. The count changes regularly as positions are filled and new ones open, so check the Zscaler careers page frequently if you are actively searching.

Do I need a cybersecurity background to get a job at Zscaler?

Not for every role. Engineering positions benefit from security knowledge, but strong networking or cloud infrastructure skills can get you in the door. Customer success and sales roles value enterprise IT experience and communication skills over deep security expertise. Zscaler also has internal training programs, and many employees come from adjacent fields and grow into security roles over time.

Which certifications help when applying to Zscaler?

Certifications that appear frequently in Zscaler job descriptions include CISSP, CompTIA Security+, AWS/Azure/GCP certifications, and CCNA or CCNP for networking-heavy roles. Zscaler also has its own administrator certifications for ZIA and ZPA, which signal product-specific knowledge. These are rarely mandatory but strengthen your profile, especially if you are moving into cybersecurity from another domain.

How long does the Zscaler interview process take?

The process commonly takes two to four weeks from first contact to offer for most roles. Senior or specialist positions can take longer, especially if multiple stakeholders need to be involved. Following up with the recruiter after each round is normal and expected. If you have a competing offer, let the recruiter know early so they can try to align timelines.

What salary can I expect at Zscaler India?

Zscaler does not publicly list salary bands for India roles. For current data, check Glassdoor and levels.fyi, both of which carry self-reported figures for Zscaler India across different levels and functions. Compensation typically includes base pay, a variable component (especially for sales roles), and RSUs for mid-to-senior positions, though the exact structure varies by role.

Does Zscaler offer remote work for India-based roles?

Most Zscaler India roles follow a hybrid model, with offices in Bengaluru and Hyderabad. Some customer-facing roles allow more flexibility depending on the territory covered. Fully remote arrangements exist for certain specialist or senior positions but are less common. Check the individual job listing for the work-location requirement, as policies vary by team and function.

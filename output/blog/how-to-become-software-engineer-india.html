01 Role Overview

Role Overview

Software engineering is one of the most sought-after careers in India today. Companies across Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Pune, Chennai, and the NCR region hire engineers at every level, from fresh graduates to senior specialists.

The role involves writing and reviewing code, solving technical problems, and collaborating with product and design teams to ship features. The field covers several sub-areas:

  • Frontend: building the interfaces users interact with
  • Backend: servers, APIs, and databases
  • Full-stack: both frontend and backend
  • Mobile: Android and iOS apps
  • DevOps: infrastructure, CI/CD, and deployments
  • Data engineering and ML: pipelines, models, and analytics

Most engineers start as generalists and find their niche within the first couple of years. Entry-level roles are commonly labelled Software Development Engineer (SDE-1), Associate Software Engineer, or Junior Developer.

02 Skills You Need

Skills You Need

Core programming skills

  • One primary language: Python, Java, C++, or JavaScript are the most common entry points in India
  • Data structures and algorithms (DSA), the gatekeeper for most tech interviews
  • Version control with Git
  • Basic SQL and relational databases

Area-specific skills

  • Frontend: HTML, CSS, React or Angular
  • Backend: REST APIs, Node.js or Spring Boot or Django, one SQL database
  • DevOps: Linux, Docker, basic cloud (AWS, GCP, or Azure)

Soft skills that Indian interviewers notice

  • Clear written and spoken communication in English
  • Ability to explain your thought process while solving a DSA problem out loud
  • Comfort reading and discussing someone else's code

Tools most teams expect

  • Git and GitHub
  • VS Code or IntelliJ
  • Basic terminal commands on Linux or macOS

03 Step By Step Path

Step By Step Path

  1. Pick one language and go deep. Python is the fastest for beginners. Java and C++ are common in service companies. JavaScript opens frontend and backend doors. Commit to one language for the first few months before branching out.
  1. Learn DSA systematically. Arrays, strings, hashmaps, trees, graphs, dynamic programming. This is non-negotiable for most product company interviews in India. Use LeetCode or similar platforms, working through problems by topic rather than randomly.
  1. Build a portfolio of real projects. Three to four projects on GitHub matter more than certificates. Each project should solve a real problem, use at least one external API or database, and have a clear README. Recruiters on LinkedIn and Naukri look at GitHub links.
  1. Learn one framework end-to-end. Pick React for frontend or Spring Boot or Django for backend. Build a complete project with it, not just follow-along tutorials.
  1. Apply for internships early. If you are in college, start applying from your second year onward. Off-campus routes through LinkedIn, Naukri, AngelList, and direct company portals work well for students from tier-2 and tier-3 colleges.
  1. Prepare specifically for Indian tech interviews. Product companies (Razorpay, Zepto, Meesho, and similar) and FAANG India offices use structured rounds: DSA coding rounds, low-level design (LLD), and system design at senior levels. Start LLD prep from your third year if you are targeting these companies.
  1. Network actively. Attend college hackathons, join communities like GDSC, participate in open-source via GSSoC or GSoC, and engage on LinkedIn. Many off-campus offers come through referrals.

05 India Specific Tips

India Specific Tips

The college tier reality

IITs, NITs, IIITs, and a few well-known private colleges (BITS, VIT, Manipal) attract on-campus placements from top companies. If you are from a tier-2 or tier-3 college, on-campus placements may be limited. The off-campus route is just as valid: build a strong GitHub profile, get active on LinkedIn, and apply directly. Many engineers at top companies came through the off-campus path.

Naukri vs LinkedIn

Naukri is still the dominant job board for Indian companies, especially service companies and mid-market firms. LinkedIn is increasingly important for product companies and startups. Set up profiles on both. On Naukri, keyword-optimise your headline and skills section. On LinkedIn, engage with posts from engineers at companies you want to join.

Service companies vs product companies

TCS, Infosys, Wipro, HCL, and Cognizant hire in bulk and offer stability, but growth can be slower and pay tends to be lower according to publicly reported industry surveys. Product companies (startups, FAANG India, unicorns) pay significantly more according to Glassdoor data, but their interviews are harder. Many engineers start at a service company, build skills for a couple of years, then move to product companies.

Communities worth joining

  • Google Developer Student Clubs (GDSC) on your campus
  • GirlScript Summer of Code (GSSoC) and Google Summer of Code (GSoC) for open source
  • HasGeek meetups in Bengaluru, Pune, and Hyderabad
  • LinkedIn communities around specific stacks (React India, PyDelhi, etc.)

Certifications that carry weight

Cloud certifications (AWS Solutions Architect Associate, Google Cloud Associate) are valued by Indian recruiters, especially for DevOps and backend roles. Keep certificates relevant and recent.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Do I need a CS degree to become a software engineer in India?

No, but it helps, especially for on-campus placements at large companies. Many engineers without a CS degree have broken in through self-study, bootcamps, and strong portfolios. Companies care about what you can build and how you perform in coding interviews. A non-CS degree combined with solid DSA skills and three to four good projects can get you interviews at startups and mid-size product companies.

How important is DSA, and when should I start?

DSA is the single biggest factor in most Indian tech interviews, including at service companies, product startups, and FAANG India offices. Start as early as possible, ideally from your first year of college or the first month of self-study if you are switching careers. Focus on understanding patterns rather than memorising solutions. Consistent daily practice over several months beats last-minute cramming.

What is the typical starting salary for a software engineer in India?

Starting packages vary widely by company type and city. Glassdoor and publicly reported industry surveys show that service companies typically offer lower packages compared to product companies and startups. FAANG India offices and funded unicorns offer the highest entry-level packages, with figures commonly cited in industry surveys. Always verify offers against current data on Glassdoor or levels.fyi before deciding.

Is it better to start at a service company like TCS or try directly for a product company?

Both are valid paths. Service companies offer volume hiring, stability, and a structured environment to learn the basics. Product companies offer better pay and faster growth, but their interviews are harder. If you are not yet ready for product company interviews, joining a service company for a year or two and then moving is a common and effective strategy in India.

How do I stand out when applying off-campus in India?

A strong GitHub with real projects, an optimised LinkedIn and Naukri profile, and referrals from people inside the company are the three biggest levers. Applying directly through company career pages works better than relying on job portals alone. Cold-messaging engineers and recruiters on LinkedIn with a short, specific note (not a generic 'please refer me') also gets responses more often than most people expect.

What are the best cities in India for software engineering jobs?

Bengaluru has the largest concentration of tech companies, from early-stage startups to large MNCs. Hyderabad and Pune are strong alternatives with a lower cost of living. Chennai has a solid base of MNC engineering centres. Delhi NCR (Gurugram and Noida) is growing fast, especially in fintech and e-commerce. Remote work has opened options beyond these cities, though many companies still prefer hybrid or in-office arrangements.

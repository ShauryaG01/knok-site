01 Salary Overview

Salary Overview

Most Engineering Manager job postings in India do not show the salary. Companies treat pay as something to reveal deep in the process, often only after a technical round and a hiring manager call. You are essentially applying blind. Knok's jobradar found 898 active Engineering Manager openings as of 2026-07-08. Among listings that share a range, pay varies enormously: a first-time Manager role can start around 35 LPA, while a Director-level role can go above 150 LPA. The title 'Engineering Manager' means very different things at different companies, which explains the wide spread. If you are currently at the Manager level and eyeing a move to Senior Manager, the salary bands show that a real step-up is within reach. But only if you negotiate from a position of market knowledge, not guesswork.

04 Negotiation Tips

Negotiation Tips

1. Know the band before the first call. Most listings hide pay, but some do not. Build your market picture using every data point available so you are not guessing when a recruiter asks for your number. 2. Anchor toward the top of the band. If you are targeting Senior Manager, the range is 55-90 LPA. Opening at the lower end signals you do not know your value. Start near the top of what is credible for the scope you will own. 3. Negotiate total comp, not just base. Variable pay, ESOPs, joining bonus, and notice-period buyout are all negotiable. If the base is stuck, push on these levers before walking away. 4. Use competing offers. A real offer from another company is your strongest tool. Be transparent about it, most hiring managers will either match or explain clearly why they cannot. 5. Understand the scope before accepting. The same LPA figure means very different things depending on whether you own one team or five, and whether you have real authority to hire and set direction.

05 Total Comp Breakdown

Total Comp Breakdown

Base salary is what the LPA figure typically refers to, but for an Engineering Manager, it is rarely the whole story. Base Salary, The salary bands (35-60 for Manager, 55-90 for Senior Manager, 90-150+ for Director) reflect base pay. This is your guaranteed annual income and the anchor for every other calculation. Performance Bonus / Variable Pay, Paid annually or quarterly, tied to individual and company performance. Industry surveys commonly cite variable components that range from a modest fraction to a significant share of base at managerial levels. Always clarify whether the payout is target-based or fully at management's discretion. ESOPs / RSUs, At product companies, funded startups, and listed tech firms, stock can be a major part of the package. Glassdoor and levels.fyi data suggests that at senior levels in top-tier companies, stock grants over a full vesting cycle can rival or exceed base salary, though this varies sharply by company stage and market conditions. Joining Bonus, Used to offset unvested stock or bonuses you leave behind. Fully negotiable for lateral hires, and often overlooked. Benefits, PF, gratuity, family health insurance, meal allowances, and learning budgets are standard at larger companies. Worth factoring in, but rarely the deciding variable when comparing offers.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Is 35 LPA a good salary for an Engineering Manager in India in 2026?

Based on knok jobradar data, 35 LPA sits at the lower end of the Manager band (35-60 LPA). For a first-time EM role at a mid-size company or early-stage startup, it is a reasonable entry point. If you are already managing multiple teams or have spent time in an EM role, you have solid grounds to negotiate higher within the band, and the data shows room to do so.

What is the pay difference between a Manager and a Senior Manager?

Knok's salary band data puts Managers at 35-60 LPA and Senior Managers at 55-90 LPA. The bands overlap between 55 and 60 LPA, which means a title change alone will not automatically move the needle, scope, team size, and negotiation matter just as much. The biggest step-up comes when moving from Senior Manager to Director, where the band starts at 90 LPA and goes above 150 LPA.

Why are there so few Engineering Manager openings in Mumbai compared to Bangalore?

Knok's data shows 10 EM openings in Mumbai versus 166 in Bangalore, a direct reflection of the two cities' tech ecosystems. Mumbai's market leans heavily toward BFSI and financial services, where the EM title is less common than in product or SaaS companies. Bangalore's density of product companies, GCCs, and funded startups is what drives the volume. Roles that do exist in Mumbai at fintech or large banks can still be competitive on pay.

Should I take a Director title at 90 LPA or a Senior Manager title at 90 LPA?

Take the Director title if the scope is real. The Director band starts at 90 LPA and goes above 150 LPA, putting you in a stronger position for future negotiations from day one. A Director title also carries more external signal when you next move. Before accepting either offer, verify the actual team structure, your authority to hire and set direction, and whether the title is used consistently across the organisation.

How much can ESOPs and RSUs realistically add to an Engineering Manager's total comp?

It depends heavily on company stage. Glassdoor and levels.fyi data suggests that at top-tier product companies and well-funded startups, stock grants at senior levels can be substantial, sometimes rivalling base salary over a full vesting cycle. At GCCs and service companies, stock is often absent or minimal. Always ask for the vesting schedule, the cliff period, and the last known valuation or share price before assigning any value to a stock component.

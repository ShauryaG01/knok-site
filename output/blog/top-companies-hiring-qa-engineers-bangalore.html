01 Salary Overview

Salary Overview

QA Engineer compensation in India varies by city, company stage, and whether the role is product, platform, or growth-focused. Fixed pay is quoted in LPA (lakhs per annum); variable pay and ESOPs are common at startups. Always clarify fixed vs CTC before negotiating.

04 Negotiation Tips

Negotiation Tips

  1. Anchor with data from 3+ offers or trusted peers at the same level.
  2. Negotiate fixed first; treat variable and ESOP separately.
  3. Ask for signing bonus if base is capped.
  4. Get the offer letter with break-up (basic, HRA, PF) before resigning.
  5. If competing offers exist, share timelines, not numbers, to accelerate decisions.
05 Total Comp Breakdown

Total Comp Breakdown

CTC typically includes: Basic, HRA, special allowance, PF employer contribution, gratuity provision, and projected variable. Ask for in-hand estimate after tax. ESOPs at startups may not appear in CTC, model dilution and vesting cliff separately.

06 Market Snapshot

Market Snapshot

Bangalore leads India's QA hiring market by a wide margin. As of 8 July 2026, knok jobradar tracked 80 open QA Engineer roles in Bangalore, ahead of Delhi (58), Chennai (13), Pune (12), Hyderabad (6), and Mumbai (4). What makes Bangalore's market distinctive is the employer mix. You have Global Capability Centres (GCCs) from European giants like Bosch and Thales alongside US tech firms like IBM and HPE, Indian IT services majors like Wipro, and product companies and startups like gleanwork and workindia. Each segment moves at a different hiring speed and rewards different skill sets. GCCs typically run structured, multi-round interviews; startups often hire faster but expect you to own test strategy from day one. Knowing who you are applying to shapes how you prepare, and how you write your CV.

09 How To Stand Out

How To Stand Out

QA roles in Bangalore, especially at GCCs and product companies, have shifted heavily toward automation and engineering depth. Here is what hiring managers are actively looking for: 1. Automation-first CV
Selenium and Cypress are table stakes. Show hands-on experience with API testing (Postman, RestAssured), performance testing (JMeter, k6), and CI/CD integration (Jenkins, GitHub Actions). A CV that only lists manual testing skills will struggle at product companies and mid-to-senior GCC roles. 2. Domain depth for GCC roles
Bosch hires for automotive and embedded QA; Thales for safety-critical systems; Mufgub for financial services testing. Tailor your resume to show relevant domain exposure, a generic QA CV will lose to a targeted one every single time. 3. SDET skills unlock the product tier
Companies like gleanwork want engineers who can write test code, not just test cases. If you are comfortable in Python or Java for test automation frameworks, lead with that. 4. ISTQB helps at GCCs
ISTQB Foundation or Advanced is frequently listed as 'preferred' in GCC job descriptions. It signals process maturity to teams running structured QA programmes, worth completing if you are specifically targeting GCCs. 5. Put real metrics in your resume
Use numbers from your own work: 'cut regression cycle from three days to four hours by building a Selenium suite' is far stronger than 'improved testing efficiency.' Concrete outcomes are what get CVs shortlisted.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Which company has the most QA Engineer openings in Bangalore right now?

BoschGroup leads with 5 open QA roles in Bangalore as of 8 July 2026, followed by Wipro and Thales Group with 3 each. Bosch's openings are concentrated in automotive and embedded QA, so tailor your application if you have that background. The full table above covers every active employer with 2 or more open roles.

Is Bangalore still the best city for QA jobs in India?

Based on knok jobradar data from 8 July 2026, Bangalore has 80 open QA roles, more than any other Indian city tracked. Delhi follows with 58. If you are open to relocating, Delhi is a strong second option, particularly for IT services and BFSI-adjacent QA roles.

Do QA Engineers at GCCs earn more than at IT services companies in Bangalore?

Generally yes, according to publicly reported data on Glassdoor and levels.fyi. GCCs like IBM, HPE, and Bosch tend to pay toward the higher end of each experience band and often include variable pay or stock on top of base. IT services roles typically sit closer to the lower end of the band with predictable annual increments. Industry surveys suggest the gap between the two segments widens significantly at senior and lead levels.

Do I need ISTQB certification to get hired at a GCC in Bangalore?

ISTQB Foundation is listed as 'preferred' rather than mandatory in most GCC job descriptions, but it does help, particularly at Bosch and Thales, which run structured, process-heavy QA teams. For product companies and startups, automation skills and tool experience carry far more weight. If GCCs are your primary target, completing the Foundation exam is a worthwhile step before applying.

What does the interview process look like for QA roles at these companies?

Most Bangalore QA interviews run 3-4 rounds: an online test covering testing concepts and basic coding, an automation round where you write or walk through test scripts, a test strategy or system design discussion, and an HR round. GCCs like IBM and HPE tend to run more structured, longer processes. Startups like gleanwork and Arintra typically move faster, often completing all rounds within a couple of weeks.

I am a manual QA engineer. Should I still apply to these openings?

Yes, but position yourself carefully. The Bangalore market has shifted toward automation, so even if your background is primarily manual, highlight any scripting work, API testing, or tool experience you have picked up. IT services companies and some GCCs still value strong manual and domain expertise for specific project needs. For product companies and senior roles, showing that you are actively upskilling in automation frameworks makes a meaningful difference to shortlisting.

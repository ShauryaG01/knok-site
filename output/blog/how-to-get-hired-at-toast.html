01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Toast builds restaurant technology, from point-of-sale systems and online ordering to payroll and marketing tools, all designed specifically for the food and beverage industry. The company is publicly traded (NYSE: TOST) and continues to expand its product suite and global team.

As of July 2026, Toast has 375 open roles spanning software engineering, sales, customer success, product management, finance, and operations. Competition varies by function: engineering and product roles tend to attract strong candidate pools, while customer success and implementation roles may move faster.

For Indian professionals, Toast's hiring is most accessible in software engineering and product. The company also has operations in India, so both India-based and US-based roles (for those with work authorisation) are worth exploring on the careers page.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Toast's hiring process typically runs through several stages. The exact number of rounds varies by team and seniority, so treat this as a general map rather than a guaranteed checklist.

Application and Resume Screen
Your resume goes through an initial review by a recruiter or an applicant tracking system. Tailor your resume to highlight any experience with SaaS products, customer-facing tools, or restaurant and hospitality technology.

Recruiter Call
If shortlisted, a recruiter reaches out for a brief phone or video call. Expect questions about your background, current situation, role interest, and salary expectations. This is also your chance to ask about team size, location, and timelines.

Hiring Manager Interview
The next stage is usually a conversation with the hiring manager. For engineering roles, this may include a light technical discussion. For go-to-market roles, expect questions about past results and how you handle customer situations.

Skills Assessment or Take-Home
Many roles include a structured assessment, which could be a coding challenge, a case study, or a presentation depending on the function. Formats vary by team, so ask the recruiter what to expect.

Final Panel or Loop
The final stage often involves a series of interviews with cross-functional stakeholders, including potential peers, team leads, or senior leaders. Behavioural questions using the STAR method are common here.

Offer and Background Check
Successful candidates receive a verbal offer followed by a formal written offer. A background check is standard before onboarding begins.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Toast is a mission-driven company focused on helping restaurants succeed. Across all roles, a few themes come through consistently in how they hire.

Customer obsession and restaurant empathy
Toast's products serve restaurant owners and staff under real operational pressure. Candidates who can show they understand the customer's world, whether from direct hospitality experience, customer success work, or building products for frontline users, stand out strongly.

Ownership and accountability
Toast values people who take initiative and follow through without needing close supervision. In interviews, expect questions about situations where you identified a problem and solved it without being asked.

Collaboration across functions
Restaurant technology is complex, combining hardware, software, payments, and services. Toast teams are cross-functional by necessity. Demonstrating that you work well across engineering, sales, and operations is a clear plus.

Technical depth (for engineering roles)
For software engineering, Toast works with a modern stack and ships product at scale. Strong fundamentals in data structures, system design, and clean code matter. Experience building reliable, high-throughput systems is valued.

Growth mindset
Toast operates in a competitive and fast-changing market. They look for people who are curious, who learn from setbacks, and who can adapt as the business evolves.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Research Toast's product before every round
Sign up for a Toast demo or watch product walkthroughs online. Understanding how the POS, online ordering, and payroll tools actually work will help you ask sharper questions and show genuine interest. Interviewers notice when candidates have actually studied the product.

Know the restaurant industry context
Toast serves an industry with thin margins, high staff turnover, and intense time pressure. Reading about challenges restaurants face, such as labour costs, delivery aggregators, and post-pandemic recovery, gives you useful talking points in every round.

Prepare STAR stories tailored to Toast's values
For behavioural questions, prepare specific examples from your past work around ownership, customer impact, and cross-team collaboration. Vague answers rarely land well; concrete stories with clear outcomes do.

For engineering candidates: practise system design
Toast's engineering interviews commonly include system design discussions. Focus on designing reliable, scalable systems and be ready to talk about trade-offs clearly. Coding rounds typically cover standard data structures and algorithms, so practise on platforms like LeetCode.

Prepare smart questions for each stage
Ask the recruiter about timelines and process. Ask the hiring manager what success looks like in the first few months. Ask the panel about team culture and how decisions get made. Thoughtful questions signal that you are evaluating them as much as they are evaluating you.

Apply to multiple relevant roles
With 375 open roles at Toast right now, there may be more than one position that fits your profile. Applying to a couple of well-matched roles increases your chances without spreading you too thin. 06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Toast describes its culture as one built around its mission: 'empowering the restaurant community to delight guests, do what they love, and thrive.' In practice, this translates to a team that takes the restaurant industry seriously and sees its software as essential infrastructure, not just a convenience tool.

Restaurants first
Decisions at Toast tend to be evaluated through the lens of what is best for restaurant operators. This shapes product priorities, support quality, and even internal processes. Coming in with genuine curiosity about the hospitality world signals cultural fit from the very first call.

Fast-paced and ambitious
Toast has scaled rapidly and continues to expand into new markets and product categories. The pace can be demanding, and teams are expected to move quickly. People who thrive here tend to be comfortable with ambiguity and changing priorities.

Team-oriented
Toast publicly emphasises collaboration and mutual respect. Cross-functional teamwork is not optional; it is baked into how the company ships product and serves customers.

Diversity and inclusion
Toast has publicly committed to building diverse teams and has employee resource groups supporting various communities. Candidates from diverse backgrounds are encouraged to apply.

If you want a focused, customer-driven environment where your work directly serves small business owners and their teams, Toast's culture is likely a strong fit.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

How long does the Toast hiring process take from application to offer?

The timeline varies by role and team. Based on publicly reported candidate experiences, the process commonly runs from a few weeks to a couple of months. Engineering roles with multiple technical rounds tend to run longer. After each stage, it is reasonable to follow up with your recruiter to ask about next steps and expected timelines.

Does Toast hire remotely, and can Indian candidates apply for US roles?

Toast has both remote and on-site roles. Many engineering and product positions allow remote work, especially for US-based candidates. Toast also has operations in India, so Indian professionals should check the careers page for India-specific listings. For US roles, you would need the appropriate work authorisation unless the specific role offers visa sponsorship.

What salary can I expect at Toast?

Compensation depends on the role, level, and location. For US-based software engineering roles, publicly reported figures on Glassdoor and levels.fyi show wide variation by seniority and function. For India-based roles, commonly cited LPA figures on Glassdoor India and similar platforms can give you a rough ballpark, though sample sizes are small so treat them as approximate guides. Always ask the recruiter for the salary band early so you are not caught off guard at the offer stage.

How competitive is it to get a job at Toast?

Toast is a well-known brand in the restaurant tech space and attracts a strong applicant pool, particularly for engineering and product roles. Customer success and implementation roles may have faster timelines and slightly less competition. With 375 open roles currently, the chances of finding a good match are reasonable if your profile aligns with what they are actively hiring for.

What is the coding interview like for software engineering roles?

Based on publicly reported interview experiences, Toast's coding interviews typically cover data structures, algorithms, and system design. The difficulty is commonly cited as moderate to challenging, in line with other mid-to-large SaaS companies. Preparing on platforms like LeetCode and practising system design with real-world scenarios is a solid approach before your first technical round.

Does Toast offer H-1B or other visa sponsorship?

Toast's sponsorship policy can change and the company does not always publish a blanket policy covering all roles. The safest approach is to ask your recruiter directly during the first call. Some teams are more open to sponsorship than others, so having an honest conversation early saves time for both sides and avoids a mismatch at the offer stage.

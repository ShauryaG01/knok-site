01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Gusto is a US-based company that builds payroll, HR, and benefits software for small and mid-sized businesses. The company is well-established in the American SMB market and is publicly reported to serve a large number of small businesses, giving its teams meaningful technical and operational challenges to solve.

As of early July 2026, Gusto has 92 open roles spanning engineering, product, design, customer experience, finance, and go-to-market functions. Most positions are based in the US, but some engineering and customer support roles do allow remote or international candidates, so it is important to check each job listing carefully before applying.

For Indian professionals, Gusto is a strong target if you have experience in fintech, payroll systems, developer tooling, or HR tech. The company competes in a space where reliability and compliance matter deeply, so backgrounds in high-stakes software, regulated industries, or customer-centric product work translate well.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Gusto's hiring process is structured and moves in clear stages, though exact steps vary by team and level. Here is what candidates commonly report:

Application and resume screen: Recruiters review applications and look for relevant experience aligned to the job description. A tailored resume moves through faster than a generic one.

Recruiter call: A recruiter introduces the role, asks about your background and motivations, and discusses compensation expectations. This is a good time to ask about visa sponsorship if it is relevant to you.

Technical or skills screen: For engineering roles, this is typically a live coding or take-home problem. For product and design roles, expect a case study or a brief work sample exercise.

Virtual onsite (several rounds): This is the main evaluation stage. Engineering candidates commonly face coding, system design, and a behavioural session. Product candidates often present a case study and field cross-functional questions. Design candidates typically share portfolio work and walk through their process.

Hiring manager or leadership conversation: Some pipelines include a final conversation with a senior leader before an offer is extended.

The full process from application to offer commonly takes several weeks. Follow up with your recruiter after a reasonable wait if you have not heard back.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Gusto looks for people who combine technical or functional craft with genuine care for the people using their product. A few qualities they consistently emphasise:

Ownership: They value candidates who identify problems and act on them without waiting for permission. Strong interview stories show initiative, not just execution.

Cross-functional collaboration: Gusto's teams work closely across engineering, product, design, and customer success. Examples of working well with people outside your immediate function stand out in interviews.

Customer empathy: Gusto's users are small business owners managing payroll and benefits, often without dedicated HR staff. Connecting your work to real user impact, whether in a design critique or a system design discussion, signals a strong fit.

Clear communication: Gusto values people who can explain complex ideas simply. This shows up in written take-homes, design reviews, and verbal system design discussions alike.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Know the product before your first call: Gusto offers public-facing payroll and HR tools. Reading their blog, help centre, and recent press before any interview gives you genuine context that stands out.

Prepare strong behavioural stories: Gusto interviewers ask about collaboration, handling failure, and working through ambiguity. Prepare a few clear examples using a situation, action, and result structure.

For engineering candidates: Practice problem-solving with clear verbal reasoning alongside your code. Study design concepts relevant to fintech, such as data consistency, reliability, and access controls.

For product and design candidates: Prepare a structured critique of Gusto's own product or a close competitor. Think through user segments, trade-offs, and how you would measure success.

Ask thoughtful questions: Gusto interviewers often assess curiosity and preparation based on the questions you bring. Ask about team dynamics, how priorities are set, and what success looks like in your first few months.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Gusto's stated mission is to create a world where work empowers a better life. In practice, this shapes how the company approaches both its product and its internal culture.

People first: Gusto applies this idea to its customers and to its own team. Employees are expected to take the mission seriously, not just treat it as a tagline. In interviews, they notice whether candidates show genuine interest in the problem space.

Ownership and accountability: Teams operate with significant autonomy. This is attractive if you like moving fast without heavy process, and it means the company expects you to self-direct. Candidates who can show examples of owning outcomes, not just tasks, tend to fit well.

Care and craft: Because payroll errors have real financial consequences for workers, Gusto values people who slow down to get things right. Speed matters, but not at the cost of correctness.

Glassdoor reviews commonly cite strong management and a genuine sense of mission as highlights. The trade-offs mentioned most often involve fast-changing priorities and the challenges that come with a growing organisation.

If you are coming from an Indian product or fintech company, your experience with scale, compliance complexity, and serving diverse user bases is directly relevant. Frame your background in terms of the problems you solved and the impact you had.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does Gusto hire remotely from India?

Most of Gusto's 92 open roles as of July 2026 are based in the US. Some engineering and customer support positions do allow remote or international candidates. Check each job listing carefully for the location and work authorisation requirement before you apply, as this varies by role and team.

What salary can I expect at Gusto?

Gusto does not publish a single universal salary band publicly. Glassdoor and levels.fyi list commonly cited ranges by role and seniority for US-based positions. For roles open to candidates outside the US, compensation structures can differ significantly, so it is best to ask your recruiter directly during the initial call.

How hard is the Gusto coding interview?

Engineering candidates commonly report that Gusto's coding rounds focus on clear problem-solving and communication rather than extreme difficulty. Solid preparation at a medium difficulty level, combined with explaining your reasoning as you code, is typically sufficient. System design questions tend to focus on reliability and data integrity, which maps well to fintech and payroll work.

Does Gusto sponsor visas for Indian candidates applying to US roles?

Gusto has publicly sponsored work visas for some US-based roles in the past. Whether a specific role qualifies for sponsorship depends on the team's needs and current policy, which can change year to year. Ask your recruiter about sponsorship eligibility during the first call so you do not invest time in a process that will not work out.

How long does the Gusto hiring process take?

The process from application to offer commonly takes several weeks. Response times after the virtual onsite can vary by team and hiring urgency. It is reasonable to send a polite follow-up to your recruiter after a few days of silence if you are waiting on feedback from a completed round.

What is the best way to stand out in a Gusto application?

A tailored resume that mirrors the language in the job description and shows clear impact tends to move through the initial screen faster than a generic one. A referral from a current employee also improves your chances, so checking your network before applying is worth the effort. Demonstrating genuine familiarity with Gusto's product and mission during interviews consistently gets positive feedback from hiring teams.

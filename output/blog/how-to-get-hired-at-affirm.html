01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Getting Hired at Affirm

Affirm is a US-based fintech company best known for its buy-now-pay-later (BNPL) platform, which lets consumers split purchases into fixed instalments with clear, upfront pricing. The company works with major retailers and has expanded into products like the Affirm Card. After going public, it continued to scale its engineering, data, and business teams significantly.

As of early July 2026, Affirm has 186 open roles spanning software engineering, data science, product management, finance, risk, and operations. Most roles are US-based, but a number are remote-friendly. Indian professionals most commonly apply when they hold a valid US work visa (H-1B or OPT), are planning to relocate, or are targeting roles explicitly open to international remote candidates.

Hiring at Affirm is structured and moves at a steady pace. The process rewards candidates who can show technical depth and a genuine connection to the company's mission of building honest, transparent financial products.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

The Affirm Interview Process, Stage by Stage

Note that exact stages vary by role and team. The outline below reflects what candidates commonly report.

Stage 1: Application and Resume Screen
Your resume is reviewed by the recruiting team. For technical roles, a strong GitHub profile or relevant portfolio helps. Tailoring your resume to match the specific job description's language improves your chances of clearing this stage.

Stage 2: Recruiter Call
If shortlisted, you will typically get a short call with a recruiter (commonly around half an hour, per candidate reports). This covers your background, current situation, interest in Affirm, and logistics like visa status and availability. Be honest and concise. Recruiters are also assessing communication skills, so speak clearly about your past work.

Stage 3: Technical or Skills Screen
For engineering roles, this is usually a live coding round on a standard platform or a take-home assignment, depending on the team. Data roles may involve a SQL or Python problem. Product and business roles may receive a short written case or a problem-solving exercise.

Stage 4: Virtual Onsite (Panel Interviews)
This is the main evaluation. Candidates commonly report several rounds, often including:
- Coding rounds (for engineers): data structures, algorithms, and sometimes a design-flavoured problem
- System design: designing a payments or financial data system is a common topic at Affirm
- Behavioural rounds: using the STAR method to talk about ownership, collaboration, and handling ambiguity
- Cross-functional round: for senior roles, you may speak with a product manager or data lead from a partner team

Stage 5: Offer and Background Check
If the panel approves, the recruiter reaches out with a verbal offer. A formal offer letter follows after a background check. For roles requiring US work authorisation, Affirm's HR team will guide you on next steps.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

What Affirm Looks for in Candidates

Technical Depth Without Over-Engineering
Affirm's engineering teams work on real-money transactions, so they value engineers who write clean, reliable code over clever but brittle solutions. In interviews, explain your trade-offs out loud. Show that you choose simplicity when it works.

Ownership Mindset
Affirm places a strong emphasis on taking end-to-end responsibility for your work. They want to see that you do not just hand a problem off to another team. Come ready with stories where you saw something through from identification to resolution.

Mission Alignment
Affirm's stated mission is to deliver honest financial products. They avoid hidden fees and confusing terms. Candidates who genuinely care about consumer finance or financial inclusion tend to stand out. Interviewers will probe whether you understand why the company exists, not just what it does.

Data-Driven Thinking
Whether you are in engineering, product, or operations, Affirm expects you to use data to support decisions. For non-technical roles, being comfortable reading dashboards and asking sharp questions about metrics matters. For technical roles, understanding how your code affects downstream data pipelines is a clear plus.

Clear Communication
Affirm is a distributed company with teams across time zones. Written and verbal communication skills matter more here than at a typical office-first company. Candidates who give structured, precise answers in interviews consistently do well.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

How to Prepare for Affirm Interviews

Understand the Product
Spend time with Affirm's product before your interview. Go through the Affirm app, read about the Affirm Card, and understand how the BNPL model differs from a standard credit card. Interviewers notice when candidates have actually used the product versus read a quick summary.

For Engineers: Focus on Fundamentals and System Design
Coding questions at Affirm are commonly in the medium-to-hard range, per candidate reports. Practise data structures such as trees, graphs, and hash maps, along with standard algorithm patterns. For system design, focus on distributed systems topics relevant to payments: idempotency, consistency, and fault tolerance come up frequently. Practise designing systems out loud, not just in your head.

For Data Roles: SQL and Experimentation
SQL fluency is important. Practise window functions, aggregations, and joining large tables efficiently. Be ready to talk about A/B testing: how you would set up an experiment, how long you would run it, and how to interpret the results.

Prepare Your Behavioural Stories
Write down a handful of stories from your past work covering: a time you disagreed with a decision and what happened, a project you owned end-to-end, a time you failed and what you learnt, and a situation where you used data to change direction. Format each using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result).

Research the Team
When you receive your interview schedule, look up the interviewers on LinkedIn. Understand which team they are on and which products they support. This helps you ask sharp questions and tailor your examples to work they actually care about.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Culture and Values at Affirm

Honest Financial Products
Affirm's core idea is that consumer credit does not need to be confusing or exploitative. The company publishes the total cost of a purchase upfront and does not charge late fees on most products. This ethos carries into the work culture: straight talk is valued, and people are expected to flag problems early rather than bury them.

People-First but High-Expectation
Affirm positions itself as a company that cares about employee wellbeing. At the same time, it operates in a competitive fintech space and expects strong performance. Former employees commonly describe it as a place with smart, motivated colleagues and a fast pace, particularly in product and engineering.

Distributed and Async-Friendly
A large portion of Affirm's workforce is remote. Internal communication tends to be written-first: detailed documents, Slack threads, and async reviews rather than back-to-back meetings. If you thrive in an environment where you can do deep work and communicate clearly through writing, this culture suits you well.

Inclusion and Diversity
Affirm has publicly reported diversity and inclusion programmes and employee resource groups for various communities. The company recruits from a wide range of backgrounds and has spoken openly about pay equity as part of its stated values.

Growth Opportunities
Because Affirm is growing its product surface (international markets, merchant tools, the Affirm Card), there are opportunities to move across teams or take on broader scope over time. Employees who demonstrate strong ownership tend to get stretched into larger roles.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does Affirm hire candidates from India for India-based remote roles?

Affirm's open roles are primarily US-based, though some are open to remote workers in certain time zones. Indian professionals most often enter via a US work visa (H-1B or OPT) or through roles that explicitly allow international remote work. Check each job description carefully for location requirements. If a role says 'US only,' that typically means you need existing US work authorisation before applying.

How long does the full Affirm hiring process take?

The process is commonly reported to take a few weeks to over a month from application to offer, though it can move faster for senior or urgent roles. The recruiter call and technical screen usually happen early in the process, with the virtual onsite scheduled a week or two after. If you have not heard back after any stage, a polite follow-up to your recruiter is perfectly reasonable.

What salary can I expect at Affirm?

Affirm does not publish salary bands publicly for all roles. Publicly reported data on Glassdoor and levels.fyi suggests compensation is competitive with other US fintech and tech companies, including base salary, equity (RSUs), and a performance bonus. The mix of base versus equity varies by level and role. Ask your recruiter for the band early in the process so there are no surprises at offer stage.

What programming languages and tech stack does Affirm use?

Affirm engineering teams are known to use Python and Kotlin as primary backend languages, with React on the frontend, running on cloud infrastructure. Coding interviews allow most common languages, so you can code in Python, Java, or C++ if those are your strengths. It helps to tell your recruiter your preferred language before the interview so the team can come prepared.

Does Affirm sponsor H-1B visas?

Based on publicly reported information, Affirm has sponsored H-1B visas in past years, as is common for US tech and fintech companies. Sponsorship is not guaranteed for every role and depends on budget, headcount, and the specific team's situation. Always confirm sponsorship availability with your recruiter during the initial call, before investing time in technical rounds.

What are the most common reasons candidates do not get an offer from Affirm?

Candidates commonly report not clearing the system design round (especially when designing for unnecessary complexity rather than correctness), giving vague behavioural answers without measurable outcomes, or failing to connect their experience to Affirm's mission. Weak written or verbal communication during the onsite is also frequently cited as a factor. Preparing specific STAR stories and practising system design out loud beforehand addresses most of these gaps.

01 Salary Overview

Salary Overview

Finding accurate salary data for DevOps Engineers in Hyderabad is harder than it should be. Most Indian job listings do not show a pay range, which means you spend hours applying, clearing rounds, and only learning the offer number at the final HR stage. Of the 28 active DevOps Engineer openings in Hyderabad tracked by knok's jobradar (as of 2026-07-08), a significant portion carried no disclosed salary at all.

What the listings that do share pay reveal is a wide market: entry roles start at 6 LPA for a fresher, and lead or staff-level engineers can reach 70+ LPA. The biggest jump in DevOps pay happens around the 3-year mark, when engineers move from executing tasks to owning infrastructure decisions. That transition from entry to mid-level is visible in the salary bands, and it explains why mid-level DevOps talent is the most competed-for segment in Hyderabad's market.

Hyderabad has 28 active openings in this snapshot. It sits behind Bangalore (187) but is comparable to Delhi (40) and just ahead of Pune (37). The city's mix of IT services companies, product startups, and Global Capability Centres (GCCs) keeps DevOps demand consistent across the year.

02 By Experience Level

By Experience Level

Your years of hands-on experience are the biggest driver of your DevOps salary in Hyderabad. Here is how the market currently breaks down:

Entry (0-2 years, 6-12 LPA). Freshers and early-career engineers who know the basics of Linux, Git, and at least one CI/CD tool fall in this band. Reaching the higher end of 12 LPA usually requires a strong internship, a relevant cloud certification (AWS, Azure, or GCP), or demonstrable project work on GitHub.

Mid (3-5 years, 15-28 LPA). This is where the salary curve turns sharply upward. Engineers who can independently manage cloud infrastructure, write Infrastructure as Code using Terraform or Ansible, and handle on-call responsibilities command this range. The gap between 15 and 28 LPA within this band is wide because skill depth varies a great deal at this level.

Senior (6-9 years, 30-50 LPA). Companies at this level expect you to design systems, mentor juniors, and own reliability for production workloads. Kubernetes expertise, security-aware infrastructure design, and multi-cloud experience push engineers toward the 50 LPA end of this band.

Lead/Staff (45-70+ LPA). These roles involve setting the DevOps strategy for an entire engineering organisation, not just maintaining pipelines. The 70+ figure applies to staff engineers at product companies or GCCs with a strong track record and typically some org-wide influence.

04 Negotiation Tips

Negotiation Tips

Salary negotiation for DevOps Engineers in Hyderabad follows a few patterns worth understanding before you talk to an HR team.

Name a number, not a range. When HR asks your expectation, giving a range signals they can anchor to the bottom. State your target based on the upper portion of the salary band for your experience level.

Use the skills gap as leverage. DevOps talent with strong Kubernetes, Terraform, or observability experience is genuinely scarce in the market. If the job description lists these skills and you have them, say so explicitly. Scarcity is your strongest negotiating position.

Clarify what the CTC actually includes. Some companies bundle performance-linked variable pay, allowances, and employer PF contributions into the CTC number. Ask the recruiter clearly: what is fixed, what is variable, and what is the guaranteed take-home? Publicly reported structures vary significantly across companies, so do not assume.

Counter the first offer at least once. A first offer in tech hiring is rarely the final offer. Asking for a revision is standard practice. The worst outcome is the recruiter says the number is firm, and you are back where you started.

A competing offer is your cleanest leverage. If you have another offer in hand, share the range (not necessarily the exact figure) with the recruiter. This shifts the conversation from what you expect to what they need to match.

05 Total Comp Breakdown

Total Comp Breakdown

In most Indian DevOps roles, the figure in the offer letter is the CTC (Cost to Company), not the amount you take home. Understanding what CTC includes helps you compare offers properly.

Base salary is the core component and the most predictable part of any offer. It is paid monthly and does not depend on performance results.

Variable or performance pay is common at product companies and GCCs. It is paid annually or quarterly, and tied to individual and company targets. Industry surveys note that variable pay is commonly cited as a percentage of base, but the exact split varies by employer. Always ask HR for the fixed-to-variable ratio before signing.

Allowances (house rent, transport, food) are included in CTC at many IT services companies. They are part of the salary structure, not a bonus. They inflate the CTC number but are not discretionary upside.

Stock or ESOPs appear at product startups and some GCCs. Their value depends entirely on whether a liquidity event happens. At the lead and staff level (45-70+ LPA), ESOPs can be a meaningful part of the package, but treat them as a potential bonus, not guaranteed income.

Provident Fund (PF) is deducted from your salary, and the employer also contributes. Some companies include the employer PF contribution inside the CTC figure, which reduces your effective take-home relative to the headline number.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Is the 15-28 LPA mid-level band realistic for a 3-year DevOps engineer in Hyderabad?

Yes, 15-28 LPA is what active listings show for mid-level DevOps roles (3-5 years) in Hyderabad. Where you land within that band depends on your skill depth. Engineers with strong cloud certifications, hands-on Kubernetes experience, and production infrastructure ownership tend to land in the upper portion of the range. Those transitioning from a closely related role such as sysadmin or cloud support may start at the lower end and move up within a year or two as they build DevOps-specific depth.

Which skills push a DevOps engineer toward the higher end of the salary band?

Kubernetes administration, Terraform for Infrastructure as Code, and platform engineering (designing internal developer platforms) are the most commonly cited high-value skills in senior DevOps job descriptions. Cloud security, FinOps (managing cloud spend), and observability tooling (Grafana, Datadog, OpenTelemetry) also appear frequently in roles at the 30-50 LPA level. Depth in one cloud provider combined with a second cloud certification tends to stand out more than surface-level familiarity across all three at the mid-level.

How does Hyderabad compare to Bangalore for DevOps salaries?

Bangalore has 187 active DevOps openings compared to 28 in Hyderabad in this snapshot, so there is significantly more absolute opportunity there. Salary bands at senior and lead levels in Bangalore are publicly reported to run higher, reflecting the higher cost of living and stronger competition for talent. However, Hyderabad's GCC sector has grown between 2024 and 2026, and many of those roles are competitive with Bangalore bands, especially at the senior level. Remote-first Bangalore roles are also worth applying to from Hyderabad without relocating.

What is the difference between a DevOps Engineer and a Site Reliability Engineer (SRE) salary?

SRE roles typically sit at the higher end of the DevOps range or slightly above it, because the role usually requires stronger software development skills alongside infrastructure expertise. In practice, many Indian job listings use 'DevOps Engineer' and 'SRE' interchangeably, especially at the mid-level. At the senior and lead level, SRE roles at product companies tend to align with the 30-70+ LPA bands in this guide, and equity tends to be a larger part of the package at that level.

How long does it typically take to cross from entry-level pay to mid-level pay?

The data shows entry-level at 6-12 LPA (0-2 years) and mid-level starting at 15 LPA (3-5 years), so the crossover typically happens around the 3-year mark. Engineers who earn a cloud certification early, contribute to production infrastructure rather than only internal tools, and take on on-call responsibilities tend to make the jump faster. Job-hopping at the 2-year mark is commonly cited as a way to accelerate the salary jump, since internal increments at IT services companies are often smaller than what the open market offers for the same skills.

01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

GreyOrange is a global robotics and AI company that builds warehouse automation solutions used by retailers, e-commerce players, and logistics providers around the world. With its engineering base in India and a growing presence across the US, Europe, and Asia, it occupies a rare space: deep technical complexity paired with real, large-scale commercial deployment.

Right now, GreyOrange has 63 open roles spanning engineering, product, sales, operations, and support functions. That is an active pipeline, which means the hiring team is genuinely looking. Whether you are a software engineer interested in robotics or AI, a supply chain specialist, or someone in enterprise sales, there is likely a relevant opening worth targeting.

The company builds systems that blend hardware, software, and AI into end-to-end fulfilment solutions. Even roles that are not purely technical tend to require comfort with complex, multi-layer products and fast-moving environments. If you thrive on hard problems and want your work to have tangible, real-world impact, GreyOrange is worth a serious look.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

The process at GreyOrange typically runs across four to five stages, though this varies by role, seniority, and team. Based on commonly reported candidate experiences, here is what to expect.

Screening and application review. Recruiters scan for relevance to the role's core skills. A tailored resume that clearly maps your background to the job requirements, whether in robotics software, enterprise sales, or operations, tends to move faster than a generic one.

Recruiter call. A brief introductory conversation covering your background, salary expectations, and availability. Come prepared with your current notice period and a clear sense of your compensation expectations. Use this call to ask about the team and what success looks like in the role.

Technical or functional assessment. For engineering roles, expect a coding round or system design exercise. For non-technical roles, a written case study or domain-specific assignment is common. This stage filters for core competence in the role's primary skill area.

Panel interviews. One or more rounds with the hiring manager and potential teammates. For engineering, this goes deep into problem-solving, system design, and technical reasoning. For business and product roles, expect situational questions around stakeholder management, cross-functional work, and decision-making under uncertainty.

Leadership or senior review. For mid-to-senior positions, a final round with a senior leader or founder is common. This tends to focus on long-term thinking, ambition, and alignment with the company's direction rather than technical specifics.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Software and robotics engineers. Strong fundamentals in your core area, whether that is backend systems, computer vision, robotics middleware, or AI/ML. Comfort with distributed systems and real-time software is a clear advantage. Communication matters too, since engineers here work closely with cross-functional and global teams.

Product managers. A systems thinking mindset is essential. GreyOrange builds complex, multi-layer products, so PMs who can hold both technical and business context simultaneously tend to stand out. Prior experience with hardware-software products or B2B enterprise tools is a meaningful differentiator.

Sales and business development. Enterprise sales experience, particularly in SaaS or hardware solutions sold to large retailers or logistics companies, is highly valued. Comfort with long sales cycles and multi-stakeholder buying decisions is important. Knowledge of the logistics and supply chain space helps.

Across all roles. Genuine curiosity about automation and the future of supply chains comes up consistently in hiring conversations at GreyOrange. This is not a company where you can fake enthusiasm for the domain. If you find warehouse robotics and AI genuinely interesting, that comes through and it matters.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Know the product. GreyOrange's publicly available case studies, demos, and press coverage give you a solid foundation. Understand what their core robotic systems do, how they fit into a fulfilment centre, and what business problems they solve for clients. Being able to speak to this in your own words signals you have done the work.

For engineering roles. Brush up on system design, particularly distributed systems, low-latency software, and real-time decision engines. If you have direct robotics experience, prepare to discuss it in concrete terms. If you do not, focus on transferable skills and articulate clearly how your background applies.

For non-technical roles. Come with a point of view on the future of warehouse automation. Think about how e-commerce growth, labour market changes, and AI are reshaping logistics operations. Having an informed perspective shows you are already thinking like someone who belongs on the team.

Prepare strong stories. Behavioural questions at GreyOrange tend to probe for ownership, cross-functional collaboration, and comfort with ambiguity. Prepare specific examples from past roles. Avoid vague statements; concrete outcomes land better.

Ask sharp questions. At every stage, ask about team dynamics, current challenges, and what the roadmap looks like. Candidates who ask thoughtful questions signal genuine interest and tend to leave a stronger impression.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

GreyOrange operates at the intersection of hardware, software, and real-world operations. This creates a culture that is more demanding and more dynamic than a typical software company. Teams work on problems where mistakes have physical and commercial consequences, which builds a culture that values precision, ownership, and direct communication.

The company has a global footprint with a strong India engineering core. If you are based in Gurugram or another India office, you will regularly collaborate with teams across multiple time zones. Comfort with async communication and working with global stakeholders is practically a requirement for mid-to-senior roles.

Employees commonly describe an environment where individual contributors can have real impact without layers of process slowing them down. At the same time, the culture is collaborative, with peer reviews and cross-team design discussions being routine. This balance of autonomy and collaboration suits people who are self-directed but value working alongside sharp colleagues.

For candidates who want to work on genuinely hard problems in AI and robotics, with the scale that comes from serving large global clients, GreyOrange offers a compelling environment. It rewards people who bring initiative, think clearly under pressure, and care about getting things right.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does GreyOrange hire freshers or only experienced professionals?

GreyOrange does hire for entry-level and campus roles, particularly in engineering. However, the majority of their 63 current open roles target professionals with some prior experience. If you are a fresher, look specifically for associate or trainee-level postings and make sure your academic projects clearly demonstrate relevance to robotics, AI, or software systems.

How long does the GreyOrange hiring process typically take?

Timelines vary by role and team, but candidates commonly report a process spanning a few weeks from the recruiter call to an offer. Senior or specialised roles can take longer. If you have not heard back within a week of completing any round, a polite follow-up to your recruiter is completely appropriate and usually welcomed.

Is GreyOrange open to remote work or is it fully office-based?

GreyOrange's core work, especially in engineering and operations, is closely tied to physical systems and office-based teams. Many roles require in-person presence at a specific location. Some product and business roles may offer more flexibility, but check each job posting carefully and clarify remote work expectations during your recruiter call.

What salary can I expect at GreyOrange?

Publicly reported compensation varies widely by role, level, and function. Glassdoor and levels.fyi list ranges for specific roles at GreyOrange in India, so check there for the most current data. Come prepared with a specific expectation during your recruiter call, anchored to market benchmarks for your level and domain, rather than waiting to be quoted a number first.

How important is domain knowledge in warehouse automation or logistics?

It depends on the role. For core engineering positions, strong technical fundamentals matter more than domain knowledge, which can be learned on the job. For sales, product, and operations roles, prior exposure to logistics, supply chain, or warehouse management systems is a real differentiator. Highlight any relevant domain experience clearly in your resume and during interviews.

Should I apply if I do not meet every requirement listed in the job description?

Yes, if you meet the core requirements. Job descriptions at most technology companies, including GreyOrange, often list a comprehensive wishlist rather than a strict checklist. If you have the fundamental skills and genuine interest in the domain, apply and use your cover note to briefly explain your fit and address any obvious gaps directly.

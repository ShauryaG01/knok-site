01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Nokia is one of the world's leading telecom and networking companies, and its India operations form a major part of its global R&D and delivery engine. Cities like Bengaluru, Chennai, and Gurgaon house large Nokia teams working on 5G, IP networking, cloud software, and enterprise solutions.

As of July 2026, Nokia has 222 open roles tracked by knok jobradar. These span network engineers, software developers, system architects, sales professionals, and support specialists. Whether you are a fresher targeting a graduate engineer trainee role or an experienced professional looking at senior IC or leadership positions, Nokia hires across levels year-round.

Candidates commonly report that the hiring process runs across several structured stages before an offer lands. Nokia recruits through its own careers portal, LinkedIn, and major job aggregators. Referrals carry weight here: employees commonly report that an internal referral moves a resume to the top of the review queue noticeably faster than a cold application.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Nokia's hiring process has several stages, though the exact sequence varies by role and team. The stages commonly reported by candidates are:

Application and resume screen. A recruiter or HR team member reviews your resume against the job requirements. Matching your resume to keywords in the job description, especially around 5G, LTE, MPLS, cloud, or the specific product line, improves your chances of clearing this gate.

Online assessment. For engineering roles, Nokia commonly uses online coding or aptitude tests. Candidates report platforms like HackerRank or similar tools being used. Tests typically cover data structures, algorithms, and sometimes domain-specific networking concepts. For non-tech roles, the assessment may focus on logical reasoning or situational judgment.

Technical interview, round one. A hiring manager or senior engineer conducts this call. Expect questions on your resume projects, core CS fundamentals, and role-specific skills. Networking engineers may get questions on protocols such as BGP, OSPF, or MPLS. Software engineers are commonly asked to solve coding problems live.

Technical interview, round two. Some teams run a second technical round that goes deeper into system design, architecture trade-offs, or domain knowledge. This is more common for senior roles.

HR and behavioural round. An HR partner discusses your background, salary expectations, notice period, and cultural fit. Nokia interviewers are reported to ask competency-based questions ('tell me about a time you...') aligned to the company's values.

Offer and background check. If all rounds go well, HR extends a verbal offer followed by a formal letter. Background verification covering education and previous employment is standard before joining.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Nokia looks for a combination of technical depth, a collaborative mindset, and the ability to work in a global matrix organisation.

Technical skills that stand out. For network engineering roles, hands-on knowledge of 5G, 4G LTE, IP routing protocols, and Nokia-specific platforms such as SROS or AirScale is a strong advantage. For software roles, proficiency in C, C++, Python, or Java, combined with experience in cloud-native development or DevOps, features prominently in job descriptions.

Problem-solving and ownership. Interviewers look for candidates who can break down complex problems and drive solutions to completion without constant supervision. Sharing specific examples from past work, with clear outcomes, makes your answers stand out.

Communication across cultures. Nokia's India teams collaborate daily with colleagues and customers across Europe, North America, and Southeast Asia. Clear written and spoken English, and the ability to explain technical concepts to non-technical stakeholders, are valued across all levels.

Certifications that help. Industry surveys suggest that certifications like Nokia NRS (Network Routing Specialist), CCNA, CCNP, AWS or Azure cloud credentials, and PMP for project managers are commonly cited in Nokia job descriptions and can improve your match to open roles.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Tailor your resume to the role. Nokia job descriptions are detailed. Mirror the language used in the posting, especially product names, protocol names, and tools. A generic resume gets filtered out early.

Practise coding or networking fundamentals. For software roles, practise data structures and algorithms on platforms like LeetCode. For network roles, revise protocol concepts and be ready to explain packet flows or draw network topologies.

Study Nokia's product portfolio. Nokia makes equipment and software across mobile networks, fixed networks, IP routing, and optical networks. Knowing which business line you are applying to, and being able to speak to their products, signals genuine interest. Nokia's public product pages and press releases are a good starting point.

Prepare STAR-format answers. Nokia's HR and behavioural rounds follow a structured format. Prepare a handful of stories from your career using the Situation, Task, Action, Result structure. Common themes include handling conflict, delivering under pressure, and cross-team collaboration.

Research salary benchmarks before the HR round. Glassdoor and levels.fyi have publicly reported Nokia India compensation data. Knowing the range for your role and city helps you negotiate confidently without underselling yourself.

Use your network. LinkedIn is useful for finding Nokia employees in your target team. A warm referral or even a brief informational conversation can give you insider context on the team culture and what interviewers focus on.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Nokia describes its culture around the idea of creating technology that helps the world act together. In practice, this translates to a few observable traits that candidates and employees commonly discuss.

Collaborative and process-driven. Nokia is a large organisation with decades of telecom heritage. Teams follow structured processes, and decisions often involve multiple stakeholders across geographies. If you prefer moving fast with full autonomy, expect to adjust to a more consensus-driven pace.

Learning and development focus. Nokia publicly emphasises continuous learning. The company offers internal training programmes, access to online learning platforms, and rotational opportunities across teams. Employees in Bengaluru and Chennai commonly cite learning opportunities as a highlight of working there.

Diversity and inclusion. Nokia has publicly stated targets around gender diversity and runs programmes to support women in tech. India offices have employee resource groups and active D&I initiatives.

Work-life balance. Publicly reported employee reviews suggest that work-life balance at Nokia India is generally reasonable compared to product startups, though project deadlines in customer-facing roles can create crunch periods.

Global exposure. One commonly cited reason people join Nokia is the chance to work on global-scale infrastructure and interact with teams across Europe and the Americas. For engineers, this is a significant draw and a career differentiator.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

How long does Nokia's hiring process take from application to offer?

Candidates commonly report timelines running across several weeks from application to offer, though this varies by role, team, and location. Campus and bulk hiring drives can move faster, while senior or specialist roles sometimes take longer. Staying in touch with the recruiter once a week is a reasonable way to get updates without being seen as pushy.

Does Nokia hire freshers directly from college in India?

Yes, Nokia runs campus recruitment at several engineering institutions in India, focusing on B.E./B.Tech graduates in electronics, computer science, and telecommunications. Nokia also hires freshers through its careers portal for entry-level roles. Publicly reported accounts from campus candidates suggest the selection typically includes an online test followed by one or two interview rounds.

What salary can I expect at Nokia India?

Compensation varies by role, city, and experience level, and Nokia does not publish pay bands publicly. Glassdoor and levels.fyi have publicly reported Nokia India salary data that gives a reasonable range for software engineers and network engineers at different seniority levels. Check those platforms for the most recent figures before your HR round so you can negotiate from an informed position.

Is a Nokia NRS certification necessary to apply?

It is not strictly required for most roles, but industry surveys commonly cite Nokia NRS and similar networking certifications as a differentiator for network engineering positions. For software and corporate roles, Nokia-specific certifications matter less. If you are targeting a network or routing-focused role, having or actively working toward an NRS certification signals genuine domain knowledge to interviewers.

How important are employee referrals at Nokia?

Referrals are commonly reported to carry real weight at Nokia. A referral from a current Nokia employee does not guarantee an interview, but it typically ensures your resume is reviewed by a human rather than filtered by an automated system. LinkedIn is a practical way to find Nokia employees in your target team and request a referral conversation before you apply.

Nokia has 222 open roles right now. How do I pick the right one to apply to?

Start by filtering on Nokia's careers portal by business group (mobile networks, IP networks, cloud software, and so on), location, and experience level. Read each job description carefully and shortlist roles where you match the majority of the must-have requirements. Applying to a small set of well-matched roles is more effective than mass-applying, because recruiters are more likely to call you when your profile clearly fits what the team needs.

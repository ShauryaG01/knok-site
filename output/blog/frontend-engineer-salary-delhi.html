01 Salary Overview

Salary Overview

The first thing to know about Frontend Engineer salaries in Delhi NCR is that most listings do not tell you what they pay. Of the 36 Frontend Engineer roles tracked by knok jobradar in Delhi NCR as of 8 July 2026, salary was disclosed in only a small fraction of postings. That gap makes it difficult to negotiate with confidence unless you have market data to back you up.

Across experience levels, Frontend Engineers in Delhi NCR earn anywhere from 5 LPA at entry level to 58+ LPA for lead and staff roles. The range is wide because it covers very different situations: a fresh graduate joining a Noida IT services firm sits at one end, while a Staff Engineer at a Gurugram product startup or global captive centre sits at the other.

The bands in this guide come from knok's jobradar data. Use them as honest reference points, not guarantees. Pay in Delhi NCR tracks closely with Bangalore for mid and senior roles, though entry-level offers vary by employer type, from product-first startups in Gurugram to large IT services campuses in Noida.

Entry (5-11 LPA): Most offers here go to fresh graduates or engineers with one internship. You will typically work on component libraries and feature screens under guidance. Companies in the Noida IT corridor often anchor toward the lower half of this band; product startups in Gurugram may push higher if your portfolio is strong.

The jump from entry to mid is the steepest in percentage terms. A strong GitHub profile, a live side project, or a measurable win on performance such as Core Web Vitals or load time helps you land toward the upper half of this band.

Senior (24-40 LPA): Employers expect you to lead code reviews, mentor junior engineers, and make architecture calls on state management, bundling, and testing strategy. Global captive centres and funded product companies in Gurugram frequently pay toward the top of this range.

Lead / Staff (38-58+ LPA): The overlap with Senior at 38 LPA reflects that this step-up is about scope, not just tenure. If you are designing systems across multiple teams, influencing hiring decisions, or translating product roadmaps into engineering plans, you are in this band. The ceiling is open for standout candidates at high-growth startups or FAANG-adjacent firms.

04 Negotiation Tips

Negotiation Tips

Most employers in India expect candidates to negotiate. Walking in without a counter is leaving money on the table. A few approaches that work well for Frontend Engineers in the Delhi NCR market:

Know your band before the call. Use the experience table in this guide as your anchor. If you have four years of experience and an offer sits toward the bottom of the mid band, a polite counter toward the middle or upper portion of that range is reasonable, not aggressive.

Lead with specifics, not just years. 'I cut the app First Contentful Paint score by half' lands better than 'I have four years of experience.' Measurable wins from your current role are your strongest negotiation currency.

Ask about variable pay separately. The base salary is not your full package. Ask explicitly: 'What is the variable component and how is it determined?' Some Delhi NCR employers load a large chunk of CTC into variable pay that only pays out if the company hits its annual targets.

Do not reveal your current CTC first. Many candidates successfully deflect this question by sharing their expected CTC instead. You are not legally required to disclose your current salary in most hiring contexts in India.

Equity matters at startups. If a startup offers below the band, ask whether there are ESOPs, how the vesting schedule works, and what the last funding valuation was. For early-stage companies, this can matter more than the base over a three to four year horizon.

Counter in writing. Once you have a verbal offer, ask for the written offer letter before deciding. If you counter, do it by email so there is a clear record of what was agreed.

05 Total Comp Breakdown

Total Comp Breakdown

Base salary is what most listings quote, but your actual annual earnings can look quite different once you add the other components.

Variable pay (performance bonus): Common at mid-to-large employers. Industry surveys commonly cite this at 10-20% of base for mid and senior engineering roles, though the actual payout depends on both company performance and your individual rating. Some early-stage startups skip the cash bonus entirely and compensate through equity instead.

ESOPs and RSUs: Product companies and funded startups often include employee stock options as part of the offer. These vary enormously in value depending on the company stage. Ask for the exercise price, the cliff, and the vesting schedule before placing too much weight on this part of the package.

Provident Fund (PF): By law, your employer contributes a statutory percentage of your basic salary to PF each month. This is deferred income, not take-home pay, but it is real value that forms part of your total compensation.

Allowances: Internet or work-from-home allowance, meal vouchers (Sodexo), and Leave Travel Allowance (LTA) are common additions at larger employers. When structured correctly, these reduce your taxable income.

Learning budget: Some product companies offer an annual learning or conference budget. Useful if you want to keep your skills current through courses, certifications, or events without paying out of pocket.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Why do most Frontend Engineer job listings in Delhi NCR not show the salary?

Indian employers, especially IT services companies and mid-size firms, have a long tradition of keeping compensation confidential until the offer stage. The logic is that revealing a band early constrains negotiation on both sides. This is slowly changing as product companies and startups post salary ranges to attract talent faster, but as of mid-2026 opaque listings remain the norm rather than the exception. Market data sources like knok jobradar are useful precisely because they help you benchmark without relying on what the listing discloses.

Which skills push a Frontend Engineer's salary toward the top of the band?

React expertise is the baseline now, not a differentiator. What moves the needle are deep TypeScript knowledge, performance optimization experience (Core Web Vitals, bundle size reduction), accessibility compliance (WCAG), and the ability to own and scale a design system. In Delhi NCR's Gurugram market specifically, fintech and e-commerce companies pay a premium for engineers who understand micro-frontend architecture and can work confidently with backend teams on API contracts.

Is Bangalore really better paying than Delhi NCR for Frontend Engineers?

Publicly reported salary surveys do show a small Bangalore premium, particularly at senior and staff levels, because the density of product companies there is higher. However, Delhi NCR has a growing cluster of global captive centres in Gurugram that pay at or near Bangalore rates. Once you adjust for the cost of living difference between the two cities, the real gap is smaller than the headline figures suggest.

What is the difference between a Senior and a Lead or Staff Frontend Engineer in pay terms?

A Senior Engineer is measured by the quality and impact of their own code. A Lead or Staff Engineer is measured by the output of the team around them, which means owning architecture decisions across multiple product areas, mentoring several engineers, and working directly with product and design on roadmap priorities. The pay bands overlap at the top of the Senior range and the bottom of the Lead range, but the Lead/Staff ceiling of 58+ LPA reflects that scope and influence, not just tenure, are being rewarded.

Do Frontend Engineers in IT services companies earn less than those in product companies?

Yes, and the gap is meaningful. Industry surveys commonly cite a difference of 20-40% between IT services and product company salaries at the same experience level, though the exact figure varies by company and role. IT services firms often offer more structured onboarding and job stability, which makes them a practical entry point for fresh graduates. The common career pattern in Delhi NCR is to start in services, build two to three years of experience, then move to a product company for the salary step-up.

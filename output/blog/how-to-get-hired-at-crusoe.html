01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Crusoe is an American AI cloud company building data centres powered by sustainable and stranded energy sources. Originally focused on reducing flared natural gas at oil fields, the company has expanded into a full AI cloud platform serving large model-training workloads. As of mid-2026, Crusoe has 381 open roles spanning engineering, infrastructure, energy operations, sales, and corporate functions.

Crusoe is headquartered in Denver, Colorado, with offices in San Francisco and data centre sites across the United States. Most roles are US-based, but the company does hire for remote and internationally located positions, particularly in engineering. Indian candidates should check the location tag on each listing carefully: roles marked remote-global or with visa sponsorship mentioned are the most accessible from India.

The company is in a high-growth phase, backed by publicly reported large funding rounds, which means hiring is active and the bar is high. If you are targeting Crusoe, treat it like a well-funded US tech startup: strong fundamentals matter, the pace is fast, and genuine interest in the energy-plus-AI intersection will set you apart from candidates who are just chasing the next cloud job.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Crusoe's interview process follows a structure typical of US tech startups, though exact stages vary by team and role. Here is the general flow based on publicly reported candidate experiences.

Stage 1: Application and resume screen
Your resume goes through an initial filter. Highlight relevant experience clearly, since early screening is often done quickly. Tailor your application to the specific role rather than sending a generic CV.

Stage 2: Recruiter call
A talent team member will reach out to verify your background, check your location and availability, and explain the role. Be ready to discuss your notice period and salary expectations openly. This call is also your chance to ask about team size and the tech stack before you invest more time.

Stage 3: Hiring manager or technical screen
This is usually a video call with the direct manager or a senior engineer. For engineering roles, expect one or two coding questions. LeetCode medium level is commonly cited by candidates who have gone through this stage. For non-engineering roles, this round is more of a structured conversation about past work and situational judgment.

Stage 4: Technical or domain deep dive
Deeper evaluation of your core skills. Engineers typically face system design questions and additional coding problems. Infrastructure and data candidates are assessed on domain knowledge specific to distributed systems, networking, or large-scale pipelines. Energy and operations candidates face questions about power systems and field logistics.

Stage 5: Final panel round
A set of back-to-back interviews, sometimes called a virtual on-site, covering technical depth, cross-functional collaboration, and culture fit. Expect 3-4 conversations in a single session. Behavioural questions follow the STAR format: Situation, Task, Action, Result.

Offer and next steps
Offers include base salary, equity, and benefits. For US-based roles, publicly reported offers on Glassdoor and levels.fyi suggest compensation is competitive with other well-funded AI infrastructure companies. For internationally based hires, terms vary by location and are best clarified early with the recruiter.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Crusoe sits at the intersection of AI infrastructure and sustainable energy, so the profile they value most combines strong technical depth with genuine curiosity about this specific space.

For software engineers: Proficiency in Python, Go, or C++ is valued depending on the team. Distributed systems experience, familiarity with Kubernetes, and cloud infrastructure knowledge (especially around GPU workloads) come up frequently in job descriptions. Experience building or optimising systems for large model-training jobs is a strong differentiator.

For data and ML roles: Strong SQL and Python skills matter, along with experience working with large-scale data pipelines. The ability to work close to production systems (not just research notebooks) is a clear plus.

For energy and operations roles: Understanding of power systems, oil and gas infrastructure, or data centre power management is relevant. Crusoe's roots are in deploying compute near flared gas sites, so people who understand energy logistics or can operate in field environments stand out from the crowd.

Across all roles: Strong written communication is important because Crusoe operates across multiple US time zones. Problem-solving under ambiguity, an ownership mindset, and the ability to move without heavy process are consistent themes in their job descriptions and in publicly reported interview feedback from candidates.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Research the company's mission before anything else. Crusoe's core argument is that AI compute and sustainable energy can work together, not at odds. Read their publicly available blog posts and news coverage so you can speak to this naturally in interviews. Candidates who treat Crusoe as just another cloud provider tend to miss the mark.

Practice coding problems at a medium difficulty level. If you are applying for a software engineering role, data structures and algorithms are tested. Crusoe is not known for being as extreme as the largest tech companies in this regard, but consistent and clear thinking matters more than raw speed.

Prepare for system design discussions. For senior engineering roles, be ready to design distributed systems that handle scale, failure tolerance, and GPU scheduling. Talking through your trade-offs clearly is as important as arriving at the right answer.

Build 4-5 strong STAR stories for behavioural rounds. Think of concrete examples from past work that show ownership, navigating ambiguity, and cross-functional collaboration. Stories where you built something from scratch or fixed something broken are especially valued at a company this size.

Ask specific, intelligent questions. Interviewers at growth-stage startups notice candidates who ask about the roadmap, team structure, and the technical challenges ahead. It signals genuine interest rather than a checkbox mentality.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Crusoe describes itself as mission-driven: reduce the environmental cost of AI by using energy that would otherwise go to waste. This shapes who they hire and how the team operates day to day. People who care about the problem itself, not just the compensation, tend to be a better cultural fit.

The culture is startup-fast. Teams are small relative to the scope of what Crusoe is building. That means more ownership per person, less hierarchy, and a higher tolerance for ambiguity. If you are used to a large established company where decisions pass through multiple layers of approval, expect a noticeably different rhythm here.

Publicly reported employee feedback on Glassdoor describes the environment as collaborative with smart colleagues, though the pace can be intense at times. Work happens across US time zones, and for remote employees, overlap hours and async communication skills are important to establish early.

Crusoe values people who are direct, take initiative, and can disagree respectfully. The company is at a stage (2025-2026) where the decisions being made now will shape the product for years. People who can think at both the detail level and the strategic level are the ones who tend to thrive here.

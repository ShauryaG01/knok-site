01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Confluent is the company behind Apache Kafka, the distributed event-streaming platform that powers real-time data pipelines at thousands of companies worldwide. Founded by the original creators of Kafka at LinkedIn, Confluent has grown into a publicly listed, cloud-native data streaming company with customers in fintech, e-commerce, logistics, and media.

As of July 2026, Confluent has 49 open roles listed globally, spanning engineering, product, sales, solutions architecture, and customer success. Indian professionals are increasingly competitive for these positions, especially in engineering and solutions roles where hands-on Kafka or cloud platform experience is valued.

Roles are a mix of remote, hybrid, and office-based. Confluent's India footprint has grown steadily, with Bengaluru being the primary hub for engineering talent. The 49 open positions cover a wide range of seniority levels, so there is likely something that fits your experience band whether you are a mid-level engineer or a seasoned solutions professional.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Confluent's hiring process typically runs across four to five stages, though the exact steps vary by role and team. The structure below is based on publicly reported candidate experiences.

Recruiter Screen
A thirty-minute call with a recruiter covers your background, motivations, and basic fit for the role. This is also a good time to ask about team structure, remote policy, and the compensation range.

Hiring Manager Interview
A conversation focused on your past work, how you approach problems, and your genuine interest in Confluent. For technical roles, expect some light technical discussion here as well.

Technical Rounds (engineering and solutions roles)
This is typically two to three rounds covering:
- Coding: data structures and algorithms on a shared editor
- System design: distributed systems, event-driven architecture, scalability trade-offs
- Domain knowledge: Kafka internals, cloud platforms, or the specific stack for the role

For solutions architects and customer-facing roles, this stage often includes a product demo or case-study presentation instead of coding.

Panel or Cross-functional Round
You meet two to four interviewers from different teams. Expect a mix of technical depth and behavioral questions using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result).

Final Decision and Offer
References may be checked at this point. The full process commonly takes three to six weeks from first contact to offer, though timelines vary by team and role.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Confluent hires for technical depth and genuine curiosity about data infrastructure. Priorities shift slightly by role, but a few things matter everywhere.

For engineers: Strong fundamentals in distributed systems, proficiency in Java or Python, and ideally hands-on Kafka experience. System design skills carry significant weight, especially at senior levels. Interviewers want to see how you reason about trade-offs at scale, not just whether you write clean code.

For solutions architects and sales engineers: The ability to translate complex streaming concepts into business outcomes. Customer-facing roles need clear communication, confidence running product demos, and familiarity with cloud platforms such as AWS, GCP, or Azure.

For all roles: How you think matters as much as what you know. Interviewers look for structured reasoning, intellectual honesty (being upfront about what you do not know), and a tendency to take ownership rather than waiting to be directed. Publicly reported feedback on Glassdoor suggests that passive or overly deferential responses tend not to stand out in Confluent interviews.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Get solid on Kafka fundamentals. Even for non-engineering roles, understanding what Confluent's product does is important. Focus on topics, partitions, consumer groups, offsets, and how Kafka fits into modern data architectures. The official Confluent documentation and 'Kafka: The Definitive Guide' (available free on Confluent's site) are good starting points.

For engineering interviews: Practice distributed systems design. Common themes based on publicly reported candidate experiences include designing a message queue, handling at-least-once versus exactly-once delivery guarantees, and scaling event-driven pipelines. For coding rounds, medium-to-hard difficulty algorithmic problems are a reasonable benchmark.

Prepare your STAR stories. Write out three to five examples from your past work covering: a technically complex problem you solved, a time you disagreed with a team decision, a project you led end to end, and a mistake you made and what you learned from it. Confluent interviewers use behavioral questions consistently across rounds.

Research the company. Read Confluent's engineering blog, recent product announcements around Confluent Cloud and Apache Flink on Confluent, and their publicly available investor materials. Knowing what they are building next signals genuine interest rather than a generic job search.

For solutions roles: Prepare a short, ten-minute mock demo of a Confluent or Kafka use case. Walking an interviewer through a real-world streaming scenario, such as a fraud detection pipeline or a ride-hailing event stream, shows hands-on readiness better than talking in abstract terms.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Confluent's culture has a few consistent themes: technical excellence, customer obsession, and a bias toward openness. The company has open-source roots (Kafka is Apache-licensed), and that ethos of sharing and building in the open still shows up in how teams operate internally.

Publicly reported employee feedback on Glassdoor and Blind through 2025 highlights a few patterns. Engineers appreciate the high technical bar and the calibre of their peers. The product roadmap moves fast, which means roles carry real scope but also require comfort with ambiguity and change.

Like many enterprise software companies, Confluent made headcount adjustments in 2024, so current hiring decisions tend to be deliberate. Each hire is expected to bring something the team does not already have, whether that is a specific domain skill, a customer segment, or a technical depth the team currently lacks.

For Indian professionals specifically: Confluent values directness. Interviews are collaborative, but you are expected to drive the conversation, ask clarifying questions, and respectfully push back when you see things differently. Showing intellectual initiative and a clear point of view will serve you better than waiting to be asked.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does Confluent hire freshers or only experienced candidates?

Confluent primarily hires experienced professionals, especially for India-based roles. Most open positions require a few years of hands-on experience with distributed systems, cloud platforms, or enterprise software. That said, they do run internship programs, and strong final-year students with Kafka knowledge or open-source contributions have landed entry-level roles based on publicly reported accounts.

What salary can I expect at Confluent India?

Confluent does not publish salary bands publicly for India. Based on Glassdoor data and levels.fyi reports from candidates through 2025, software engineering compensation is commonly cited as competitive with other US-listed enterprise software companies hiring in Bengaluru. Ask the recruiter for the band during your first call; they are generally straightforward about sharing it.

Is Confluent remote-friendly for Indian candidates?

Confluent offers a mix of remote and hybrid roles. Many engineering positions are listed as remote or remote-first, while some solutions and customer-facing roles may require travel or presence in a metro city. Check each job listing carefully, as the setup varies by team and function.

How long does the Confluent interview process take?

The process typically takes three to six weeks from recruiter screen to offer, based on commonly reported candidate experiences. Delays happen most often at the technical scheduling stage. Following up politely with your recruiter after each completed round is perfectly normal and often helps keep things moving.

What is the best way to get a referral at Confluent?

Referrals carry real weight at Confluent. LinkedIn is the most direct path: search for Confluent employees in the team or role you are targeting, send a concise note explaining your background and the specific position, and ask if they would be open to a short call. A referral from someone who can speak to your actual work, such as a former colleague or a Kafka community connection, is far more effective than a cold ask.

01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Zensar Technologies is a Pune-based IT services and digital solutions company, part of the RPG Group, with clients primarily in the US, UK, and Europe. Their work spans digital transformation, cloud services, data analytics, application management, and testing.

As of July 2026, knok jobradar tracks 255 open roles at Zensar, covering software engineering, cloud, QA, data, business analysis, and project management. Most openings are based in Pune or Hyderabad, with some roles allowing hybrid arrangements depending on the project.

Zensar sits in the mid-tier of Indian IT services, which has a practical benefit: hiring cycles tend to be shorter than at Tier-1 firms, and you are more likely to speak directly with the hiring manager early in the process. Both freshers and experienced professionals apply here, and the company runs dedicated campus hiring alongside lateral hiring throughout the year.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Most candidates go through 3 to 4 rounds, though the exact number depends on the role, seniority, and team.

Round 1: HR screening
A recruiter reaches out to confirm your profile, briefly discuss the role, and ask about notice period, location preference, and salary expectations. This is a short call, not a technical one.

Round 2: Technical interview
A senior engineer or tech lead covers your core domain. For developers, expect questions on data structures, algorithms, and the languages listed in the job description, sometimes with a small coding problem. For QA roles, manual and automation concepts are standard (Selenium and Cypress are commonly tested frameworks). For data roles, SQL and Python are the usual focus. System design may come up for mid to senior positions.

Round 3: Managerial round
The hiring manager or project lead goes deeper on your past work and assesses how quickly you can contribute to a live project. Behavioural questions like 'tell me about a time you handled a tight deadline' are standard here.

Round 4: Leadership round (senior roles only)
Some senior or client-facing positions include a final conversation with a delivery head or account manager, focused on communication, client handling, and big-picture thinking.

Offer letters typically follow within a few days of clearing the final round. Salary discussions happen during the HR stage, so have your expected CTC ready before round 1.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Know what Zensar does. Before any round, read about their service lines: digital transformation, cloud, data, application management, and testing. Knowing which area your role sits in helps you tailor your answers.

Prepare your technical basics. Go through the skills listed in the job description and revise core concepts. Do not skip fundamentals even if you are experienced: interviewers at Zensar commonly revisit basics.

Review your own resume carefully. Interviewers pick questions directly from what you have written. Be ready to walk through every project you have listed, including the challenges you faced and your specific contribution.

Prepare for the HR round. Have clarity on your notice period, your current CTC, and a realistic expected CTC. Check Glassdoor and levels.fyi for current market ranges before you give a number.

Practice behavioural questions. The managerial round leans on past-behaviour questions. Prepare 3 to 4 examples from your work history that show problem-solving, teamwork, and handling pressure.

Get your documents ready. Offer processing at Zensar requires educational certificates, past offer letters, and salary slips. Having these scanned and ready speeds things up.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Zensar describes its culture around small, focused teams working closely on client deliverables. In practice, this means project-driven work with defined delivery timelines, regular client calls for senior roles, and a structured appraisal cycle.

The company invests in learning programmes and covers the cost of some certifications, though how much time you actually get for upskilling depends on your project load at the time. Internal mobility is possible after you have settled into a project, and many employees move across service lines over a few years.

Freshers joining through campus hiring go through a structured training programme before being placed on projects. The duration and depth of this training varies by intake batch and business need.

The environment is generally described as professional and process-oriented rather than startup-style. If you prefer clear processes, defined roles, and a predictable structure, Zensar tends to suit that preference.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does Zensar hire freshers?

Yes, Zensar runs campus hiring drives at engineering colleges across India, typically targeting final-year students. Freshers go through a structured training programme before being placed on live projects. Lateral hiring for experienced professionals runs throughout the year via the careers portal and platforms like LinkedIn and Naukri.

How long does the Zensar hiring process take from application to offer?

For lateral hires, the process commonly takes a few weeks from the first HR call to a written offer, though urgent project requirements can speed this up. Fresher batch timelines are tied to the academic calendar and can extend longer. Following up with the recruiter after each round helps you stay on their radar.

What salary can I expect at Zensar?

Exact figures vary by role, experience, and location. For current benchmarks, check Glassdoor and levels.fyi, which carry publicly reported CTC data from Zensar employees across different levels. Going in with a researched range rather than a single number gives you more room to negotiate.

Is there a service bond for freshers at Zensar?

Publicly reported accounts from Zensar fresher batches mention a training bond of varying duration. Confirm the current terms directly during the offer stage, as bond conditions can change between hiring cycles. Read the offer letter carefully before signing.

Are Zensar interviews conducted online or in person?

Most initial rounds, including the HR call and the first technical interview, are conducted online via video. Later rounds, especially the managerial discussion, may be online or in person depending on the role and location. The recruiter will confirm the format when the interview is scheduled.

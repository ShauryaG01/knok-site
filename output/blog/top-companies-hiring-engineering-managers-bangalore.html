01 Salary Overview

Salary Overview

Engineering Manager compensation in India varies by city, company stage, and whether the role is product, platform, or growth-focused. Fixed pay is quoted in LPA (lakhs per annum); variable pay and ESOPs are common at startups. Always clarify fixed vs CTC before negotiating.

04 Negotiation Tips

Negotiation Tips

  1. Anchor with data from 3+ offers or trusted peers at the same level.
  2. Negotiate fixed first; treat variable and ESOP separately.
  3. Ask for signing bonus if base is capped.
  4. Get the offer letter with break-up (basic, HRA, PF) before resigning.
  5. If competing offers exist, share timelines, not numbers, to accelerate decisions.
05 Total Comp Breakdown

Total Comp Breakdown

CTC typically includes: Basic, HRA, special allowance, PF employer contribution, gratuity provision, and projected variable. Ask for in-hand estimate after tax. ESOPs at startups may not appear in CTC, model dilution and vesting cliff separately.

06 Market Snapshot

Market Snapshot

As of 8 July 2026, knok jobradar is tracking 166 Engineering Manager openings in Bangalore, the most active city for this role in India by a wide margin. Delhi is a distant second with 49 openings, followed by Chennai (18), Pune (15), Hyderabad (11), and Mumbai (10). The Bangalore market spans three distinct employer types: Global Capability Centres (GCCs) of large multinationals, product companies with India-based engineering teams, and homegrown fintechs and startups. Each has a very different interview style, growth ceiling, and work culture, so knowing which type suits your goals is the first step before you start applying.

GCCs (Thales, Trane, Disney, Bosch): These are India engineering centres of large global companies. Roles tend to be stable with strong pay structures, but your team's scope is usually defined by what the global org assigns to the India centre. Expect structured hiring with multiple rounds that may include global stakeholders.

Product companies (Okta, Impact Analytics, Glean): Your team directly ships the product. You own roadmap decisions alongside PMs, are accountable for delivery velocity, and have a clearer path to senior leadership if the company scales. Interviews typically include a leadership case study and an architecture trade-off discussion.

Fintechs and startups (KrazyBee, Lendingkart, Vegapay): Fast-moving environments where you may manage across engineering, data, and sometimes product. The breadth can strengthen your resume, but check the company's funding runway and team size before committing.

09 How To Stand Out

How To Stand Out

Engineering Manager hiring in Bangalore is competitive because the candidate pool is deep. Here is how to move to the front of the queue: 1. Lead with outcomes, not activities
Do not write 'managed a team.' Write 'grew the team, cut the release cycle by half, and shipped the core module ahead of schedule, here is what I did to make that happen.' Numbers from your own work history are always fair to use. 2. Match your resume to the employer type
For GCCs, highlight cross-functional and cross-geography collaboration. For product companies, show roadmap ownership and delivery velocity. For fintechs, show that you can operate with ambiguity and wear multiple hats. 3. Show people development, not just people management
Top companies ask 'how many of your direct reports got promoted?' Prepare two or three concrete examples of how you coached engineers into senior or staff roles. 4. Demonstrate system-level thinking
EM interviews at product companies almost always include a system design or architecture trade-off discussion. You do not need to write code, but you do need to speak the language of your engineers fluently. 5. Have a specific answer to 'why this company'
Generic answers get filtered out fast. Read the company's engineering blog, look at their recent product launches, and connect something specific in their work to your own background.

10 Application Strategy

Application Strategy

With 166 openings in Bangalore right now, the opportunity is real, but so is the noise. Here is a focused plan: Pick your lane first
Choose one employer type, GCC, product company, or fintech, as your primary focus, based on where your last few years of experience maps best. Go deep there before spreading to other types. Go direct before going through recruiters
For companies like Okta and Glean, their engineering blogs and LinkedIn pages often name hiring managers. A thoughtful message referencing a specific product decision or technical post lands better than a cold application through a job board. Prioritise high-signal companies
Thales (8 openings) and KrazyBee (8 openings) are both running active campaigns. When a company has multiple EM openings at once, it usually signals a real org build-out rather than a one-off backfill, your chance of getting a response is higher. Follow up once, then move on
If you have applied and heard nothing after about a week and a half, a single follow-up message to the recruiter is appropriate. After that, redirect your energy to the next opportunity. Q Questions

Frequently asked

Which companies are hiring the most Engineering Managers in Bangalore right now?

As of 8 July 2026, Thales Group and KrazyBee top the list with 8 open EM roles each, followed by Okta and Trane Technologies with 7 each. Lendingkart and The Walt Disney Company each have 5 openings. These numbers come from knok jobradar and reflect live postings, not historical averages or accumulated backlog.

What salary can I expect as an Engineering Manager in Bangalore?

Knok jobradar data shows Manager-level roles ranging from ₹35-60 LPA, Senior Managers from ₹55-90 LPA, and Director-level from ₹90-150+ LPA. The actual number varies significantly by company type, product companies and funded fintechs tend to offer higher cash or meaningful equity, while GCCs typically offer structured bands with strong non-cash benefits. For company-specific figures, Glassdoor and levels.fyi are your best public sources.

Should I target a GCC or a product startup as an Engineering Manager?

It depends on what you want from the next few years. GCCs like Bosch and Thales offer stability, global exposure, and structured career ladders, but your scope is usually shaped by global priorities. Product companies and fintechs give you more ownership, over roadmap, hiring, and culture, but come with more ambiguity and execution pressure. Neither is better in the abstract; they reward different strengths and risk appetites.

What does the interview process look like for EM roles in Bangalore?

Most companies run several rounds for EM roles: an HR screen, a hiring manager call, a technical or system design discussion, a leadership or behavioural panel, and sometimes an additional bar-raiser round at product companies. GCCs often add a call with a global stakeholder. Prepare examples in the STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result) for people management, conflict resolution, and delivery under pressure.

Is Bangalore the best city in India for Engineering Manager roles?

By the numbers, yes, 166 of the Engineering Manager openings tracked by knok jobradar are in Bangalore, compared to 49 in Delhi and 18 in Chennai. If you are open to relocation, Delhi has a growing base of product and consulting firms with active EM hiring. But for GCCs and product companies specifically, Bangalore remains the clear centre of gravity.

How do I know if a job listing is still open or already filled?

Job boards often leave listings live long after a role is filled. Stronger signals that a role is genuinely active: the posting date is recent, the company has multiple similar openings at once (suggesting a real hiring push, not a one-off), and a recruiter responds when you reach out directly. Knok jobradar refreshes listings nightly and removes stale postings, so the counts here reflect real-time availability rather than accumulated backlog.

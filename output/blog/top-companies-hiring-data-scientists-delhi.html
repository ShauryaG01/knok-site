04 Negotiation Tips

Negotiation Tips

  1. Anchor with data from 3+ offers or trusted peers at the same level.
  2. Negotiate fixed first; treat variable and ESOP separately.
  3. Ask for signing bonus if base is capped.
  4. Get the offer letter with break-up (basic, HRA, PF) before resigning.
  5. If competing offers exist, share timelines, not numbers, to accelerate decisions.
05 Total Comp Breakdown

Total Comp Breakdown

CTC typically includes: Basic, HRA, special allowance, PF employer contribution, gratuity provision, and projected variable. Ask for in-hand estimate after tax. ESOPs at startups may not appear in CTC, model dilution and vesting cliff separately.

09 How To Stand Out

How To Stand Out

Tailor your resume to the employer type. GCCs want to see evidence that you can handle scale, work with global stakeholders, and document your methods. Startups want to see that you shipped something end-to-end. Services firms want client impact, so quantify business outcomes wherever possible.

Show Python and SQL as non-negotiable basics. Delhi NCR job descriptions consistently ask for these two alongside a framework like scikit-learn, TensorFlow, or PyTorch. If you are applying to AI-native startups like Level AI or OneShot AI, familiarity with LLMs and prompt engineering has become a strong differentiator in 2025-2026.

Build a visible portfolio. A well-commented GitHub repository, a competitive Kaggle notebook, or a write-up on a real business problem you solved carries more weight than certifications alone. This matters especially for GCC roles where the hiring panel is technical.

Prepare for case rounds. Nielsen, Mastercard, and Adobe typically include a data case or take-home problem in their process. EXL and analytics services firms often use SQL and statistics tests. Know which kind of interview to expect and prepare for it specifically.

Network inside the GCC ecosystem. Delhi NCR has a dense community of data professionals at GCCs. LinkedIn posts, local meetups, and internal referrals matter here because the hiring circles at companies like Nielsen and Mastercard are relatively tight.

10 Application Strategy

Application Strategy

Start with the employers hiring the most. EXL Service has 6 openings right now, and Nielsen and Circle K have 4 each. Companies in an active hiring cycle move faster and are more likely to consider candidates who apply promptly.

Apply across more than one employer type. Do not limit yourself to only GCCs or only startups. Applying across the mix gives you real data on which interview process suits you best and increases your overall chances.

Apply even when you do not meet every requirement. If your profile meets most of the requirements listed, go ahead and apply. Hiring managers in Delhi often write aspirational job descriptions and hire on demonstrated ability and attitude.

Follow up after applying. A short, professional message to the hiring manager or recruiter on LinkedIn, referencing your application, gets noticed. This is especially effective at mid-size firms like Capri Global Capital or Hanu Software Solutions, where recruiters handle smaller pipelines.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Is Delhi NCR a good city for Data Scientists, or should I move to Bangalore?

Delhi NCR is a solid market with 46 active openings as of July 2026, though Bangalore has 166. The real difference is in the type of work: Delhi leans toward GCCs and analytics services, which offer stability and structured growth. If you prefer product-first or pure-play AI startups, Bangalore has more of those. If you are already based in Delhi, the market is deep enough to build a strong career without relocating.

Which companies in Delhi NCR are best for freshers?

EXL Service hires the most Data Scientists in Delhi NCR right now (6 open roles) and has a known fresher intake. Analytics Training Hub and Hanu Software Solutions also tend to be open to early-career candidates. GCCs like Nielsen and Mastercard typically prefer some prior experience for their 'Data Scientist' titles, though they do hire freshers into analyst roles that feed into data science tracks over time.

What is the difference between a GCC Data Scientist role and a startup Data Scientist role in Delhi?

At a GCC like Circle K, Nielsen, or Adobe, your work is scoped to one business domain, processes are mature, and pay follows global benchmarks. At a startup like Level AI or OneShot AI, you build from scratch, move faster, and often handle data engineering and model deployment yourself. GCCs suit people who want structure and mentorship; startups accelerate your skills faster but come with more ambiguity.

What skills do Delhi NCR employers specifically ask for?

Python and SQL are the baseline across all employer types. GCCs like Mastercard and Adobe frequently ask for experience with cloud platforms (AWS or Azure) and ML pipeline tools. Analytics service firms like EXL emphasise business communication and the ability to present findings to non-technical stakeholders. AI startups ask for LLM familiarity, API integration, and rapid prototyping skills.

How long does the hiring process typically take at these companies?

Publicly reported timelines on Glassdoor suggest GCCs like Mastercard and Adobe typically run multiple interview rounds spread across several weeks. Analytics service firms like EXL often move faster once you clear the initial test. Startups can decide quickly when they are in a growth phase, but a smaller team means fewer open slots, so hiring can also stall if the manager is stretched. Following up politely after each round helps keep your application visible.

Should I apply to multiple roles at the same company?

Yes, as long as your profile genuinely fits each role. EXL Service has 6 open Data Scientist positions in Delhi right now, and they may sit in different business units with separate hiring teams. Applying to more than one shows strong interest in the company. Just tailor your message for each specific role rather than sending the same template, since recruiters at the same company often share notes.

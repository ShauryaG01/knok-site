05 How To Stand Out

How To Stand Out

1. Show working projects
A GitHub profile with live deployments matters more than a long list of job responsibilities. Hiring managers at Bangalore startups often look at your work before they look at your degree. 2. Go deep on React
React is the dominant framework across Bangalore's Frontend listings. Know it beyond the basics: rendering behaviour, state management trade-offs, performance profiling. Product companies will probe this in interviews. 3. Add TypeScript
TypeScript is increasingly a requirement rather than a nice-to-have, especially at larger codebases like Razorpay or Whatfix where team size makes type safety non-negotiable. 4. Know your Core Web Vitals
Being able to discuss LCP, CLS, and INP, and show how you improved them on a real project, is a strong differentiator at mid and senior levels. 5. Build something AI-adjacent
With companies like Sarvam and Harvey actively hiring, Frontend Engineers who have built streaming UIs, chat interfaces, or other AI-powered patterns have an advantage. A personal project counts here.

06 Application Strategy

Application Strategy

Apply early
Most roles in this snapshot are recent. Applying in the first week of a role going live gives you a better shot at a screening call before the pipeline fills. Customise the top third of your resume
Lead with the frameworks the job description mentions and one concrete impact statement, for example, 'reduced page load time, which improved user retention', rather than a generic duties list. Message the recruiter or hiring manager directly
A short, relevant note on LinkedIn to the recruiter, mentioning the specific role and one thing you have built, gets you noticed outside the main applicant queue. This is standard practice in Bangalore's tech hiring scene and is not considered pushy. Follow up once, politely
If you have not heard back after about a week or two, a single polite follow-up message is appropriate. Keep it brief. Let automation handle the volume

Is Bangalore a good city for Frontend Engineers right now?

Yes, based on current data. knok jobradar showed 94 open Frontend Engineer roles in Bangalore as of 8 July 2026, well ahead of any other Indian city. What salary should a mid-level Frontend Engineer expect in Bangalore?

Based on knok jobradar data, mid-level engineers (3-5 years of experience) typically fall in the ₹12-22 LPA range in Bangalore. Publicly reported data on Glassdoor suggests engineers with strong React and TypeScript skills tend to land toward the upper half of that band. Do I need to know React specifically, or will other frameworks work?

React dominates Bangalore's Frontend listings by a clear margin, based on what knok jobradar tracks. Vue and Angular appear occasionally, but most product companies, especially startups, default to React or Next.js. If you are considering upskilling to improve your options, React is the safest bet for the Bangalore market. TypeScript alongside React is increasingly expected at mid and senior levels.

The safest approach is to check each job description carefully and ask about flexibility during the first call. Even roles listed as 'Bangalore-based' are sometimes open to remote candidates, particularly at the senior level.

Bangalore has both the most openings (94) and the largest pool of Frontend Engineers among Indian cities, so it is active but competitive.

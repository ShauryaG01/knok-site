01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

EXL Service is a global analytics and digital operations company with a strong India presence across Noida, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, and Gurugram. It works with major clients in insurance, healthcare, banking, and travel, helping them use data to make faster and better decisions. EXL sits in a space between analytics consulting and business process management, which makes it a compelling option for professionals who want to blend domain expertise with data skills.

As of July 2026, EXL has 398 open roles across its hiring channels. That is a healthy volume and signals that multiple teams are actively growing. Positions span data analytics, data science, technology, operations, actuarial, finance, and domain consulting. Both freshers and experienced candidates can find relevant openings.

Getting hired at EXL is not primarily a coding challenge. The company values people who understand business problems, work comfortably with clients, and can present data insights in a way that drives decisions. If you have domain knowledge in insurance or healthcare plus solid analytics skills, you are a strong fit for many of the 398 roles currently open.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

The EXL hiring process generally moves through several stages. The exact number of rounds varies by role, level, and business unit, so treat this as a representative guide.

Resume screening. EXL recruiters review incoming applications and match them against the job description. For active roles with many applicants, shortlisting can be competitive. A tailored resume that highlights relevant domain experience, analytics tools, and measurable outcomes from past work will stand out.

HR screening call. A recruiter reaches out for a short conversation about your background, current role, notice period, and compensation expectations. Keep this focused and honest. Be ready to explain why you want to join EXL and what draws you to the specific domain or function.

Online assessment. Many roles include a proctored test covering quantitative reasoning, SQL, Excel, or domain knowledge depending on the function. For analytics and data science roles, expect SQL queries, data interpretation questions, and scenario-based problem solving. This round acts as a filter before the main interviews.

Technical or functional interview. Usually one or two rounds with a senior analyst, domain expert, or manager. Expect questions on your past project work, the tools you use, and how you have turned data into business outcomes. Analytics roles often include a short case study or take-home exercise. Operations roles focus more on process improvement scenarios and client-handling experience.

Managerial round. The hiring manager evaluates your communication style, maturity, and ability to handle cross-functional work. Questions here explore how you handle ambiguity, how you work with clients, and what your career goals look like over the next few years.

HR final discussion. The last step covers your offer, compensation components (fixed pay, variable pay, and benefits), and joining timeline. If you have other offers on the table, this is the right time to bring them up professionally.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

EXL's hiring signals are fairly consistent across job descriptions and candidate feedback shared publicly.

Analytical thinking with business context. EXL does not just want people who can run queries. It wants people who can interpret the output, connect it to a business question, and explain the finding to a client. SQL is essential for analytics roles, and Python or R is increasingly expected for data science positions. But the communication of insights matters as much as the analysis itself.

Domain expertise. Insurance, healthcare, banking, and travel are EXL's core verticals. Candidates with genuine domain knowledge, whether from prior work, a relevant certification, or academic background, get noticed faster. If your background is purely technical, take time to study the industry before your interview.

Client communication skills. A large share of EXL's work involves interaction with global clients. Strong spoken and written English, comfort presenting data to non-technical audiences, and an ability to manage expectations are qualities hiring managers look for consistently.

Structured problem-solving. EXL favors candidates who can break down a complex problem into steps, identify what data they need, and work through it methodically. This shows up in both case-style interview questions and in day-to-day project work.

Team orientation. EXL operates in a matrix structure, with analysts working alongside domain consultants, technology teams, and client leads. People who collaborate well and take initiative without needing constant direction tend to advance faster.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Research EXL's business before you apply. Read about the verticals EXL operates in, particularly your target domain. If you can name a specific business challenge EXL helps clients solve in that space, it signals genuine interest during the interview.

Practice SQL and Excel seriously. The online assessment is a real filter for analytics roles. Be comfortable writing queries with joins, aggregations, and window functions. For Excel, focus on pivot tables, lookup functions, and summarising messy data into clear output.

Prepare a few strong project stories. Structure each one around the business context, what you did, and what changed as a result. Vague answers like 'I worked on a dashboard' do not land well. Interviewers want specifics: what the data showed, what you recommended, and what the client or business did with it.

Brush up on domain basics. For insurance roles, understand concepts like claims processing, underwriting, and loss ratios. For healthcare, get familiar with revenue cycle management or clinical data workflows. Even surface-level familiarity shows you are serious about the domain.

Practice case-style questions. EXL interviewers often describe a business problem and ask how you would approach it. There is no single correct answer. They want to see how you frame the question, what assumptions you make, and how you would present findings to a non-technical stakeholder.

Have your documents ready early. Background verification at EXL involves checking educational certificates, past offer letters, relieving letters, and salary slips. Gaps in documentation have delayed joining dates for some candidates, so get these sorted before you start applying.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

EXL positions itself as an analytics company rather than a traditional BPO or IT services firm. This distinction shapes the internal culture more than most people expect before they join.

Data-driven decision making. Teams are expected to support their recommendations with data, not just experience or intuition. This creates an environment where analytical rigour is valued across all levels, not just among data scientists.

Work hours and shifts. Most India offices work in shifts or hybrid schedules to align with US and UK client time zones. Analytics teams often have more standard hours, while operations teams may rotate. Confirm the expected schedule during the HR discussion before you sign.

Learning and upskilling. EXL has publicly invested in training programs for analytics, AI, and domain certifications. Employees who actively pursue learning tend to move faster within the organisation. Lateral movement between domains (for example, from insurance analytics to healthcare operations) is possible with a good internal track record.

Team size and visibility. Teams tend to be small to mid-sized, which gives individual contributors more direct exposure to managers and senior leadership. This is a real advantage for people early in their careers. The trade-off is that accountability is visible and personal.

Inclusion initiatives. EXL has publicly committed to diversity programs, including support for women returning to work and career development for underrepresented groups. These efforts are more prominent in the Noida and Bengaluru offices.

Compensation and growth. Glassdoor reviews and publicly reported feedback describe EXL as competitive within its segment, with annual performance reviews tied to individual and business unit results. Health insurance, variable pay, and access to learning platforms are commonly cited as part of the overall package.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

How long does the EXL hiring process take from application to offer?

Candidates commonly report the full process taking a few weeks to well over a month, depending on the role and the number of interview rounds. Lateral hires with niche skills sometimes move faster. The online assessment and background verification steps are the most common sources of delay, so prepare your documents early and respond to recruiter messages promptly.

Does EXL hire freshers, or is it mostly for experienced professionals?

EXL hires both. Fresh graduates typically enter through campus drives or structured trainee programs focused on analytics and operations. Experienced professionals apply directly through job portals or referrals. With 398 open roles as of July 2026, there are openings at multiple experience levels across different functions simultaneously.

What salary can I expect at EXL for analytics roles?

Compensation varies widely by role, location, and experience level. Glassdoor and publicly reported data suggest EXL is broadly competitive within the mid-tier analytics and operations segment in India, with LPA figures spanning a wide range across junior and senior positions. The best approach is to get the full breakdown of fixed pay, variable pay, and benefits during your HR discussion rather than relying on external averages.

Is domain knowledge in insurance or healthcare mandatory to get hired?

It is not always mandatory, but it gives you a clear advantage for the majority of EXL's open roles. The company operates heavily in insurance, healthcare, and banking, so candidates who already understand how these industries work tend to move through interviews more smoothly. If you come from a pure technology background, even a few weeks of focused reading on the domain can make a noticeable difference in how your interviews go.

What tools and skills does EXL test during interviews?

For analytics and data science roles, SQL is nearly always tested, and Python or R is expected at senior levels. Excel skills, including pivot tables and lookup functions, come up frequently in assessments. Domain knowledge questions in insurance or healthcare are part of the process for vertical-specific roles. Communication ability and case-solving approach are evaluated across all rounds, not just the technical ones.

Does EXL offer work-from-home or hybrid options?

EXL has moved toward hybrid arrangements for many analytics and technology roles, consistent with broader industry trends post-2024. Operations and client-delivery roles more often require in-office presence for at least part of the week. The specific work mode is usually mentioned in the job posting, so check it before applying if this is an important factor in your decision.

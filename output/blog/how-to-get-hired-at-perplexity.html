01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Perplexity is one of the most talked-about AI startups right now, building an answer engine that competes directly with traditional search. The company moves fast, ships often, and expects the same from its team. As of early July 2026, Perplexity has 82 open roles spanning engineering, machine learning research, product, design, sales, and operations.

Most positions are based in San Francisco, but Perplexity has historically been open to hiring strong candidates regardless of geography for certain technical roles. If you are an Indian professional targeting Perplexity, the most realistic paths are roles that allow remote work or sponsoring a US visa, so check each job description carefully before you apply.

The hiring bar is high. Perplexity operates as a small, high-output team, which means every new hire is expected to contribute meaningfully from day one. That said, 82 open roles is a sizeable number for a company of this size, and it signals genuine growth momentum worth paying attention to.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Perplexity's hiring process is not publicly documented in full detail, so what follows is based on publicly reported candidate experiences and industry norms for AI startups of this type. Treat this as a general guide, not a guarantee.

Stage 1: Application review
Your resume goes to a recruiter or hiring manager. At a company like Perplexity, referrals carry real weight. If you know anyone on the team, a warm introduction can move your resume to the top of the pile. Otherwise, tailor your resume to the specific role and make your impact clear with concrete results.

Stage 2: Recruiter call
A short call to check your background, motivation, and availability. Based on publicly reported experiences, these typically run around 20-30 minutes. Be ready to explain why Perplexity specifically, not just 'I want to work in AI.' They want people who use and care about the product.

Stage 3: Technical or role screen
For engineers, this is usually a coding round focused on data structures, algorithms, and sometimes a short system design question. For ML roles, expect questions on model fundamentals, training pipelines, and real-world tradeoffs. For product or business roles, expect a case study or a product critique of Perplexity itself.

Stage 4: Full interview loop
This is the main event. Candidates typically go through multiple rounds covering technical depth, system design, product thinking, and behavioural questions. Publicly reported loops for Perplexity include three to five rounds. For senior roles, a take-home project or a live coding session may also be included.

Stage 5: Reference checks and offer
Perplexity moves quickly once they decide. Reference checks can happen in parallel with later rounds. If you reach this stage, be responsive and have your references ready in advance.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Perplexity is looking for people who are genuinely curious about AI, information retrieval, and how people find answers. A few qualities that come up consistently in publicly reported hiring feedback:

Deep technical craft. For engineering and ML roles, strong fundamentals matter more than familiarity with any one framework. Can you reason through a hard problem from first principles? That matters more than knowing the latest library.

Product intuition. Even in purely technical roles, Perplexity values engineers who think about user experience. They build a consumer product that people rely on for real answers, so understanding what makes a search result good, and what makes it bad, is useful across the whole team.

Speed and ownership. Perplexity is a small team shipping at a high pace. They want people who can pick up a task, figure it out, and deliver without needing constant direction. Past examples of shipping fast and independently are worth highlighting in interviews.

Genuine passion for the mission. The company is trying to change how people access information. Candidates who use Perplexity regularly and have real opinions about how it could improve tend to stand out.

Clear communication. Whether you are writing code, writing a document, or explaining a decision, clarity is valued. In interviews, walking through your thinking out loud helps interviewers follow your reasoning.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Use the product seriously. Before you apply, spend time using Perplexity's answer engine. Form opinions about what it does well, where it falls short, and what you would change. You will almost certainly be asked about this, and a thoughtful, specific answer is a strong signal.

Revisit your fundamentals. For engineering roles, practise coding problems focused on arrays, trees, graphs, and dynamic programming. For ML roles, be ready to explain attention mechanisms, fine-tuning approaches, retrieval-augmented generation, and evaluation metrics in plain language. Levels.fyi and Glassdoor have publicly reported interview experiences from Perplexity candidates that can give you a sense of the question style.

Prepare a strong 'why Perplexity' answer. This is not a place for a generic answer about loving AI. Talk about a specific feature you find interesting, a limitation you noticed, or a product direction you think they should explore. Specificity shows you have done your homework.

Have your impact stories ready. For every significant project in your career, know the problem, your specific contribution, the outcome, and what you would do differently. Use concrete results wherever possible.

Read their technical writing. Perplexity's team publishes research and product updates publicly. Reading a few of these gives you vocabulary for the interview and shows genuine interest.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Perplexity operates with the urgency of a startup that knows it is in a competitive race. The culture, based on publicly reported employee accounts and the company's own communications, leans toward a few defining traits.

High autonomy, high accountability. Team members are expected to figure things out without a lot of hand-holding. If you thrive with a clear mandate and the freedom to decide how to get there, this environment suits you. If you prefer detailed guidance and structured processes, it may feel unstructured at times.

Curiosity as a core trait. The company's product is literally about answering questions. That same intellectual curiosity is expected internally. People who ask 'why' and 'what if' tend to fit well here.

Speed over perfection. Perplexity ships quickly and iterates in public. The culture tolerates imperfection in service of learning fast. This is different from companies where everything goes through long approval chains before release.

Small team, large impact. With a relatively small headcount relative to the scale of their product, each person carries significant weight. This can be energising or exhausting depending on what you are looking for. Senior hires in particular are expected to define scope, not just execute it.

Mission alignment matters. The company's stated goal is to make access to accurate information better for everyone. Employees who genuinely believe in this mission tend to stay energised through the pace.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does Perplexity hire engineers from India, or are all roles US-only?

Most of Perplexity's 82 current open roles are US-based, primarily in San Francisco. However, AI companies at this stage have been known to hire strong candidates globally, especially for research and certain engineering roles. Check each job description for location requirements and look for roles that explicitly mention remote eligibility or visa sponsorship. A referral from someone inside the company can also help your application get a closer look regardless of location.

What kind of coding questions does Perplexity ask in interviews?

Based on publicly reported candidate experiences, Perplexity's coding rounds focus on core data structures and algorithms, similar to other top-tier tech companies. System design questions are common for senior roles. ML-specific interviews may include questions on retrieval systems, language model internals, and evaluation methods. Glassdoor has a few publicly reported questions from Perplexity interviews that are worth reviewing as part of your preparation.

How important is it to have an AI or ML background to get hired at Perplexity?

For many engineering roles, strong fundamentals matter more than a specific AI background. Perplexity hires backend engineers, infrastructure engineers, and full-stack developers alongside ML researchers. That said, having a working understanding of how large language models and retrieval systems function will help in almost any technical interview there, even if AI is not your main specialisation. Showing that you have used and thought critically about the product goes a long way.

What is the best way to stand out in a Perplexity application?

Two things stand out most: a referral from someone inside the company, and a very specific answer to 'why Perplexity.' Generic AI enthusiasm is common. What is less common is a candidate who has used the product deeply, has a clear point of view on its strengths and weaknesses, and can tie that perspective to their own skills. That combination gets attention from hiring teams at companies like this.

How long does Perplexity's interview process typically take?

Based on publicly reported experiences, the process can move quickly once it starts, sometimes completing within a few weeks from first contact to offer. Perplexity is known for moving fast when they want a candidate. However, response times after the initial application can vary, so following up if you have not heard back is reasonable. Having your references prepped in advance avoids delays at the final stage.

Is it worth applying to Perplexity if I do not have a top-tier college background?

Perplexity, like many AI startups, tends to weight demonstrated ability and relevant work experience heavily. A strong portfolio, open-source contributions, or a track record of shipping impactful products can compensate for the absence of a brand-name degree. The interview process is skills-based enough that preparation and execution matter significantly. A referral or a standout project that shows you understand their product area is often more useful than where you studied.

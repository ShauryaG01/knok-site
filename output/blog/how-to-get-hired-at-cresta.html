03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Cresta's hiring process typically runs four to five stages, though the exact order can vary by team and role.

Recruiter screen: A short call to confirm your background, your interest in Cresta, and basic logistics like availability and location. Be ready to speak clearly about your current work and what draws you to this company specifically.

Technical or functional screen: For engineering roles, this is usually a coding round covering data structures, algorithms, or system design basics. For non-technical roles, expect questions around your domain, past results, and how you think through customer problems.

Take-home task or live coding session: Some engineering teams send a short take-home task related to NLP, machine learning pipelines, or backend services. Others prefer a live coding session. Product and go-to-market candidates may receive a case study instead.

Panel interviews: This is the core of the process. You will typically speak with a hiring manager, two or three peers, and sometimes a cross-functional stakeholder in one day or spread across a week. Expect behavioural questions alongside technical depth.

Final review and offer: Cresta generally moves quickly once the panel is complete. Expect feedback or an offer within a week of your final round, though timelines can shift based on team bandwidth.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Cresta sits at the intersection of AI, NLP, and enterprise SaaS, so the skills they value reflect that mix.

For software engineers, strong fundamentals in Python and distributed systems matter most. Experience with large language models, speech processing, or real-time data pipelines is a strong differentiator. They want people who can ship production-grade ML, not just research prototypes.

For data scientists and ML engineers, hands-on experience with fine-tuning models, building evaluation frameworks, and working with messy, real-world conversational data carries more weight than academic credentials alone.

For product managers, they want people who can translate complex AI capabilities into clear customer value. Prior experience in enterprise software or contact centre technology is useful but not always required.

For customer success and solutions engineers, the ability to understand a client's business process, map it to Cresta's platform, and drive adoption is central. Technical curiosity is valued even in non-engineering roles.

Across all roles, Cresta looks for people who are comfortable with ambiguity, move fast, and communicate well across functions.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Research Cresta's product before any interview. Watch demos, read their blog, and understand how their AI coaching platform works inside a live contact centre. Interviewers notice quickly when candidates have done this homework.

For engineering roles, revise coding problems at the medium to hard difficulty level, focusing on arrays, trees, graphs, and dynamic programming. Also brush up on system design, especially for distributed and low-latency systems. If you have ML experience, be ready to explain model training, evaluation, and deployment end to end.

For ML and data science roles, prepare to discuss specific projects where you took a model from idea to production. Be ready to talk about how you measured success, what went wrong, and what you would do differently.

Prepare your 'why Cresta' answer honestly. They are building in a competitive space and want people who genuinely care about the problem of real-time AI coaching. A generic answer about 'exciting AI work' will not stand out.

Use the STAR format for behavioural questions (Situation, Task, Action, Result). Cresta interviewers commonly ask about times you dealt with ambiguity, drove alignment across teams, or shipped something fast under pressure.

Ask sharp questions at the end. Questions about how the team measures impact, what the roadmap looks like for your area, or how engineering and product collaborate show you are thinking like someone already in the role.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Cresta describes itself as a place where speed and craft are both expected. The culture is ambitious: they are trying to reinvent how customer-facing teams work using AI, and they hire people who find that kind of challenge energising rather than overwhelming.

The team is relatively small for the scope of what they are building, which means individuals carry real responsibility early. Engineers regularly own features end to end. Product managers work directly with customers. Customer success managers have a direct line to the product team. If you have mostly worked in large, slow-moving organisations, the pace may feel different at first.

Collaboration across functions is a stated value and shows up directly in how interviews are structured. You will meet people from different teams, not just your direct peers, because they want to know you can work across boundaries.

Cresta has been publicly vocal about responsible AI development, which matters deeply in the contact centre space where privacy and compliance are real concerns for enterprise customers. If you have experience or strong views in this area, bring them into the conversation.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does Cresta hire from India, or are their roles mostly in the US?

Cresta hires globally and many of their engineering and technical roles are remote-friendly. Indian engineers based in India or those with valid US work authorisation both have realistic paths into the company. Check individual job listings for location requirements, since some roles in sales and customer success may require specific geographies or US time zone overlap.

How long does Cresta's hiring process typically take?

From application to offer, the process commonly spans a few weeks. The recruiter screen and technical screen happen early on, followed by the panel round. Cresta tends to move faster than large enterprises, though specific team workloads can affect timelines. Following up politely with your recruiter after each stage is perfectly fine and often helps keep momentum going.

What salary can I expect at Cresta?

Cresta does not publicly list compensation bands, so publicly reported figures on Glassdoor and levels.fyi are your best benchmark for a realistic range. For India-based roles, compensation typically aligns with local market rates for AI-focused product companies. Actual numbers vary by level, location, and negotiation, so always go into an offer conversation with current market data in hand.

Is prior contact centre or CX industry experience required to apply?

Not for most technical roles. Cresta values AI and engineering skills far more than domain-specific background for engineering and ML positions. For customer success, solutions engineering, or sales roles, experience with enterprise SaaS or CX platforms is a genuine advantage. Even without it, showing you have researched the industry signals the right kind of curiosity.

What is the best way to get noticed when applying online?

Tailor your resume to highlight AI, ML, or NLP projects with clear outcomes. Cresta's recruiters look for people who have shipped things, so describe your impact in specific language even if you avoid exact proprietary numbers. A referral from someone already at the company significantly improves your visibility, so check your LinkedIn connections before applying cold.

Are there internship or new graduate opportunities at Cresta?

Cresta does hire interns and new graduates, particularly for engineering roles, though the volume varies by year. New grad roles at AI-focused companies are competitive, so a strong portfolio of projects involving NLP, ML, or real-time systems matters more than grades alone. Internship hiring commonly opens in the early months of the year, so watch their careers page from January onwards.

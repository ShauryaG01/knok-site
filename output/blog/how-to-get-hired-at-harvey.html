01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Harvey is one of the most talked-about legal AI companies right now, they build AI tools that help law firms and in-house legal teams do complex work faster. As of early July 2026, Harvey had 344 open roles listed across engineering, product, go-to-market, and legal-domain functions. That is a significant hiring push for a company at their stage, which means doors are genuinely open across seniority levels. For Indian professionals, Harvey is relevant in two ways: they hire remotely for some technical and product roles, and their work sits at the intersection of AI and law, a space that rewards people with backgrounds in machine learning, software engineering, or legal research. If you have skills in any of these areas, Harvey is worth a close look.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Harvey's interview process is competitive and moves at a deliberate pace. Based on publicly reported candidate experiences, here is how it typically unfolds: Stage 1, Application review
Your resume and cover letter are screened for role fit. Harvey values clear, specific writing, so a tailored cover letter genuinely helps here. Generic applications get filtered out quickly. Stage 2, Recruiter call
A short introductory conversation with a recruiter to cover your background, interest in Harvey, and logistics. Come prepared with a clear story about why legal AI excites you, broad 'I love AI' answers do not stand out here. Stage 3, Hiring manager interview
A deeper conversation about your past work and how it maps to the role. For engineering roles, expect questions about system design and past projects. For product or GTM roles, expect case-style questions around legal workflows and AI adoption. Stage 4, Technical or domain assessment
Depending on the role, you may get a take-home or live coding exercise (for engineers) or a written/case exercise (for product and legal roles). Harvey cares a lot about the quality of thinking, not just the final answer. Stage 5, Final panel interviews
A set of back-to-back interviews with team members, cross-functional partners, and sometimes a senior leader. Expect questions on collaboration, handling ambiguity, and your specific domain knowledge. Stage 6, Offer and reference checks
Harvey typically checks references before extending a written offer. Keep a list of former managers or colleagues ready to go. The full process usually spans several weeks. Timelines vary by role and team, so it is perfectly fine to ask your recruiter for a rough schedule upfront.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Harvey hires people who are deeply curious about both AI and the legal industry, not just one or the other. Here is what consistently shows up across role descriptions and publicly shared interview feedback: Technical depth (engineering and AI roles)
Strong fundamentals in software engineering, machine learning, or NLP. Experience building production systems matters more than academic credentials alone. Domain fluency (legal and product roles)
You do not need to be a lawyer, but you should understand how legal teams actually work, what a contract review involves, why a large law firm's workflows differ from an in-house team's, and where AI can genuinely save time versus where it creates risk. Clear communication
Harvey's product touches highly regulated, high-stakes work. They want people who can explain complex ideas simply, in writing and in conversation. Bias for action
This is a company moving fast. They prefer people who can make decisions with incomplete information and iterate quickly rather than waiting for perfect clarity. Mission alignment
Harvey talks openly about wanting to change how the legal profession works. Candidates who are genuinely excited about that mission, not just about AI in general, tend to do better throughout the process.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Understand Harvey's product before your first call
Read their public blog posts, watch any founder interviews you can find, and understand what problems their product actually solves. Interviewers will ask why Harvey specifically, and 'because it is a hot AI company' is not the answer they are looking for. Know the legal AI landscape
Familiarise yourself with how large language models are being used in legal work, contract review, legal research, due diligence, drafting. You do not need a law degree, but you should be able to talk intelligently about the use cases and their limits. Prepare STAR-format stories
For behavioural questions, have three or four concrete examples ready that show how you handled ambiguity, disagreed with a decision, or shipped something difficult. Legal AI is still early-stage, Harvey wants people who have navigated uncertainty before. Polish your writing
Harvey's work involves a lot of written communication. If you have a portfolio of writing, technical docs, blog posts, product specs, have it handy. For any written assessments, draft your answer first, then cut it down ruthlessly. For engineering candidates
Revise system design fundamentals and be ready to discuss how you would build or scale an AI-powered feature. Focus on trade-offs, not just solutions. For product and GTM candidates
Practise talking through a B2B or legal-tech product you admire. How does it solve a real workflow problem? What would you change about it? 06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Harvey is a mission-driven company building in a space where the stakes are genuinely high, legal mistakes cost clients real money and reputation. That shapes the culture in a few important ways. High standards, high ownership
People at Harvey are expected to own their work end to end. There is not a lot of hand-holding. If you thrive in environments where you are trusted to figure things out, that is a strong fit. If you need heavy structure to do your best work, it may feel uncomfortable. Respect for the domain
Legal is a profession with centuries of practice and established norms behind it. Harvey's team takes that seriously, they are not trying to replace lawyers, they are trying to make legal work better. Candidates who approach the space with intellectual humility tend to fit in well. Fast-moving but thoughtful
Harvey moves quickly, but not carelessly. Because their product touches high-stakes decisions, they are deliberate about what they ship. Expect a culture that values speed and rigour at the same time, not one at the expense of the other. Collaborative across disciplines
Engineers, product managers, and people with legal backgrounds work closely together. Being genuinely curious about what your colleagues do, outside your own lane, is valued here. Overall, Harvey suits people who want to work on a genuinely hard problem, care about their craft, and are excited about reshaping a traditional industry with AI.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does Harvey hire remotely, and can Indian professionals apply?

Harvey does list remote-eligible roles, particularly in engineering and product. Indian professionals can and do apply for these positions. Read each job description carefully, as some roles specify location requirements or time zone overlap expectations. When in doubt, ask the recruiter directly during your first call.

How competitive is it to get an interview at Harvey?

Harvey is a high-profile company in a fast-growing space, so application volume tends to be significant. That said, with 344 open roles as of early July 2026, they are hiring at real scale across multiple functions. A tailored resume and a cover letter that speaks specifically to your interest in legal AI will help you stand out far more than a generic application.

Do I need a legal background to work at Harvey?

Not for most technical and product roles, engineers and ML practitioners do not need law degrees. For roles closer to the product or client side, familiarity with legal workflows is a clear advantage. Even when a formal legal background is not required, showing genuine curiosity about how the legal industry works will strengthen your candidacy.

What does Harvey pay for technical roles?

Harvey does not publish a standard salary grid publicly. Levels.fyi and publicly reported data suggest compensation at well-funded legal AI companies is competitive with other late-stage AI startups, typically including meaningful equity. For remote roles with India-based employees, ask the recruiter directly about the pay band, currency, and equity structure early in the process so there are no surprises.

How long does the full hiring process take?

Based on publicly reported candidate experiences, the process from application to offer typically spans several weeks, though timelines vary by role, team, and how quickly both sides move. If you have a competing offer or a hard deadline, let the recruiter know early, Harvey, like most companies, can often accelerate when there is a genuine reason to do so.

What is the best way to get noticed by Harvey's recruiters?

A strong, tailored application remains the most reliable path in. Engaging with Harvey's published content, their blog, founder interviews, product announcements, gives you genuine talking points that interviewers notice immediately. Building in public on topics related to legal AI or NLP can also surface your profile organically. Referrals from current employees carry significant weight, so thoughtful networking is worth the effort.

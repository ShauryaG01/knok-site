01 Salary Overview

Salary Overview

Most DevOps job listings in Pune skip the salary line. That is a deliberate strategy: hiring teams collect your current CTC first, then anchor the offer close to it. The number shown in a listing (when one appears at all) is often the floor, not the midpoint.

knok's jobradar tracked 37 active DevOps Engineer openings in Pune as of 8 July 2026. The salary bands in this guide come from that same live data. The full range runs from 6 LPA for freshers to 70+ LPA for Staff and Lead engineers. That spread exists because 'DevOps Engineer' covers a wide spectrum: a junior automating CI pipelines and a Staff SRE owning multi-cloud reliability for a global firm can share the same job title.

One thing to keep in mind before reading the numbers: CTC (Cost to Company) is not take-home pay. Variable components, provident fund contributions, and gratuity inflate the headline figure. When comparing offers, always ask for the fixed, in-hand breakup separately.

02 By Experience Level

By Experience Level

Your salary as a DevOps Engineer in Pune depends heavily on where you sit on the experience curve. The bands below are drawn from knok's jobradar data as of July 2026.

Entry (0-2 years, 6-12 LPA): This is the learning phase. Companies look for scripting basics, Linux familiarity, and some exposure to tools like Docker or Jenkins. Salaries at the lower end go to trainees; reaching the upper end of this band usually requires a relevant internship or a cloud certification.

Mid (3-5 years, 15-28 LPA): This is where Pune's DevOps market gets competitive. Engineers with hands-on Kubernetes, Terraform, or AWS/GCP experience and a track record of shipping production pipelines find multiple offers. The jump from Entry to Mid is steep because companies pay a premium for real production experience over classroom knowledge.

Senior (6-9 years, 30-50 LPA): Senior engineers own reliability and release engineering for entire products. Architecture decisions, mentoring, and cross-team coordination become core parts of the role. Performance bonuses and equity start to move the total package significantly at this level.

Lead / Staff (45-70+ LPA): Staff and Principal DevOps engineers influence how engineering teams build and ship software at scale. The salary range at this tier overlaps with engineering managers. Equity, whether ESOPs or RSUs, often forms a large share of total compensation here.

04 Negotiation Tips

Negotiation Tips

Salary negotiation for DevOps roles in Pune follows a few patterns worth knowing before you enter any discussion.

Anchor on market data, not your current CTC. Most recruiters open with 'What is your current CTC?' rather than sharing a range. You can respond: 'I am targeting market rate for my experience level, which puts me in the 15-28 LPA range for mid-level DevOps in Pune.' Quoting a band from live data shifts the conversation to your value, not your history.

Certifications move the needle at Entry and Mid levels. AWS Certified DevOps Engineer, CKA (Certified Kubernetes Administrator), and Terraform Associate are frequently cited by hiring managers as justification for offers at the higher end of a band. If you hold a relevant cert, mention it explicitly during the discussion.

Compete offers when you can. Telling a recruiter you are in final rounds elsewhere creates urgency. A competing process, even an early-stage one, gives you leverage to request a faster decision or a stronger offer.

Separate fixed pay from CTC. Ask for the fixed and variable components broken out separately. A 28 LPA offer with a large variable portion is very different from a fixed 28 LPA. The same logic applies to stock: ask for the vesting schedule and cliff before counting ESOPs as part of your income.

Make sure a switch is worth it. Changing companies involves ramp-up time, loss of unvested equity, and relationship rebuild costs. Make sure the fixed pay increase justifies those costs, not just the CTC headline.

05 Total Comp Breakdown

Total Comp Breakdown

A DevOps Engineer's package in Pune is rarely a single number. Understanding each component helps you compare offers accurately.

Fixed Base Salary: The guaranteed monthly amount before tax. This is the only part of your CTC you can count as certain income. Everything else has conditions attached.

Variable Pay / Performance Bonus: Set as a portion of your base and paid annually or quarterly. Ask what share of employees actually hit their variable targets, because the 'at target' figure is not always what lands in your account.

Provident Fund (PF): Both you and the employer contribute monthly. The employer's contribution appears in your CTC but not in your in-hand salary. It is deferred savings, not spendable income right now.

Gratuity: Paid out only after a minimum period of continuous service set under law. Companies include a projected gratuity figure in the CTC breakup, but it is conditional on staying long enough to qualify.

ESOPs / RSUs: Stock grants are common at product companies and startups, vesting over a multi-year period with a cliff before the first tranche. Stock in a private company has value only at a liquidity event. Listed company RSUs are more predictable in value.

Joining Bonus: Offered by some companies to offset your notice period or relocation costs. It is a one-time payment, often with a clawback clause if you leave within a year.

When you receive two offers, build a simple side-by-side: fixed pay, target variable, employer PF contribution, and realistic equity value. That is the apples-to-apples picture. Q Questions

Frequently asked

How current is this salary data?

The salary bands and job counts in this guide come from knok's jobradar as of 8 July 2026. Job market data shifts with hiring cycles, funding rounds, and broader economic conditions. Treat these bands as a strong starting point for negotiation, not as a guarantee. Cross-check with recent offers shared in communities like LinkedIn or Blind for the freshest signal from active candidates.

Why do most DevOps job listings in India not show salary?

Indian labour law does not require employers to disclose pay in job postings. Many companies omit the range deliberately so they can ask for your current CTC and anchor the offer close to it. This practice is more common in traditional IT services firms than in product startups, where salary bands are increasingly shared upfront to attract competitive candidates and reduce negotiation friction.

What skills help a DevOps Engineer in Pune move from Entry to Mid level pay?

The jump from 6-12 LPA to 15-28 LPA is mostly about production experience: having shipped and maintained real pipelines that teams depend on daily. Skills that consistently appear in mid-level job descriptions include Kubernetes (especially CKA certification), Terraform for infrastructure as code, and hands-on experience with at least one major cloud provider. Demonstrable incident response and on-call experience also signals readiness to hiring managers, since it shows you have operated under pressure.

Is Pune a good city to grow a DevOps career?

Pune has a solid DevOps hiring market, with 37 active openings tracked in early July 2026. The city's mix of product companies, IT services firms, and manufacturing-tech teams means DevOps roles exist across industries, giving you options rather than locking you into one sector. Salary levels are slightly below Bangalore at the same experience level, as publicly reported in industry surveys, but cost of living offsets part of that difference. Engineers who want Bangalore-level packages sometimes use Pune experience as a stepping stone before relocating.

Should I count ESOPs when comparing two job offers?

Count ESOPs cautiously. In a listed company, RSUs have a market price you can look up and verify. In a private company, ESOPs are worth something only at an IPO or acquisition, and the timeline is unpredictable. Ask about the strike price, the current company valuation, and the most recent funding round before assigning any number to them. Negotiate on fixed pay first and treat ESOPs as potential upside, not guaranteed income.

How do I negotiate when a company says the offer is fixed?

'The offer is fixed' is a common opening position, not always a hard ceiling. Ask if other components are flexible: joining bonus, accelerated vesting, remote work policy, or an earlier performance review cycle. If the base truly cannot move, get a written commitment for a salary review after a specific period. That assurance is often worth more in the long run than fighting over a small increment at offer time.

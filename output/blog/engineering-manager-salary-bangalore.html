01 Salary Overview

Salary Overview

Most Engineering Manager job listings in India skip the salary line entirely. You clear a phone screen, two technical rounds, and a hiring manager chat, and only then does HR share a number. This disclosure gap is a structural feature of Indian tech hiring in 2026, not an oversight, and it makes benchmarking genuinely hard without reliable data. Knok jobradar tracked 166 Engineering Manager openings in Bangalore as of 8 July 2026. Where employers chose to disclose pay, the data falls into three broad bands:

LevelSalary Band
Manager₹35-60 LPA
Senior Manager₹55-90 LPA
Director₹90-150+ LPA

These figures reflect only listings where salary was disclosed, a subset of the full market. Industry surveys suggest top-of-band and above-band offers exist, particularly at global product companies and well-funded startups. Use these bands as a credible floor to enter salary conversations, not a ceiling to accept.

02 By Experience Level

By Experience Level

Your title, team size, and reporting structure matter more than raw years of experience when it comes to which band you land in. Manager, ₹35-60 LPA
This covers first-time Engineering Managers and those owning a single team. You are close to the code, balancing 1-on-1s with technical decisions daily. Offers at the lower end of this band typically come from smaller engineering orgs or early-stage companies; the upper end usually signals a well-funded startup or a large Global Capability Centre (GCC) in Bangalore. Senior Manager, ₹55-90 LPA
At this level you manage managers or lead multiple squads. Scope expands to cross-functional execution, org-level hiring, and roadmap ownership across product areas. The overlap with the Manager band exists because some companies use 'Senior Manager' where others use 'Engineering Manager 2' or 'Group Manager', the title matters less than the actual scope. Director, ₹90-150+ LPA
Director roles carry P&L awareness, VP-facing communication, and responsibility for engineering culture across a large group. Publicly reported total compensation at this level, including equity and annual bonuses, often exceeds the base band at top-tier product companies.

04 Negotiation Tips

Negotiation Tips

Most Engineering Manager offers in India have more room than HR's first number suggests. Here is how to push without burning the offer: Anchor before they do. When a recruiter asks 'what are you expecting,' name a number first, ideally at the top of the relevant band or slightly above. Industry surveys on compensation negotiations consistently find that candidates who anchor high get higher final offers. Use competing timelines, not just competing offers. You do not need a rival offer letter in hand. Being in late rounds elsewhere creates genuine urgency and gives you leverage without overstating anything. Ask about each component separately. If base salary is stuck, ask about a joining bonus, accelerated vesting, or a higher variable pay target. Companies often have more flexibility on these than on the fixed pay line. Get equity details in writing. For startups and growth-stage companies, ESOPs are a large part of the real offer. Ask for the strike price, the cliff, the full vesting schedule, and the last fundraise valuation, all before you sign. Do not accept on the spot. Ask for a couple of days to review the full offer carefully. This is standard practice and no company will rescind an offer for it.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

What is the typical salary range for an Engineering Manager in Bangalore in 2026?

Based on listings tracked by knok jobradar as of July 2026, Engineering Manager salaries in Bangalore range from ₹35-60 LPA at the Manager level, ₹55-90 LPA at Senior Manager, and ₹90-150+ LPA at the Director level. These figures come from listings where employers chose to disclose pay, many do not, so treat them as a credible benchmark rather than an exhaustive picture of the market. Industry surveys suggest top-of-band and above-band offers exist, particularly at global product companies.

How many Engineering Manager jobs are open in Bangalore right now?

Knok jobradar tracked 166 Engineering Manager openings in Bangalore as of 8 July 2026, covering roles across product companies, GCCs, startups, and consulting firms that posted publicly. This count fluctuates week to week as new roles open and old ones close. Bangalore consistently holds the largest share of these roles among all Indian cities by a significant margin.

Does Bangalore pay more for Engineering Managers than Delhi or Hyderabad?

Publicly reported compensation data and industry surveys suggest Bangalore tends to offer higher base salaries for Engineering Managers compared to most other Indian cities, largely because the concentration of global product companies and well-funded startups drives pay up. Delhi NCR has a strong market of its own, 49 open roles in the same snapshot, and companies there can be competitive, especially in fintech and e-commerce. Hyderabad and Pune have smaller markets for this role right now, though individual offers can still be strong depending on the employer.

What skills help an Engineering Manager move from the ₹35-60 LPA band to ₹55-90 LPA?

The jump from Manager to Senior Manager is usually about scope, not technical depth. Hiring managers at the Senior Manager level look for experience running multiple teams or managing other managers, a track record of cross-functional execution, and the ability to own a roadmap independently without close direction. Demonstrating measurable impact, headcount growth, product outcomes, reliability improvements, in your current role is more persuasive than additional certifications or courses.

Do Engineering Managers in Bangalore receive equity as part of their compensation?

At most mid-to-large product companies and funded startups, equity is a standard part of the Engineering Manager package. At listed companies this comes as RSUs (Restricted Stock Units), with a vesting schedule commonly cited as 4 years including a one-year cliff. At pre-IPO startups, ESOPs are more common, their real value depends heavily on the company's future valuation and whether a liquidity event happens. Always ask for the full equity details in writing before accepting any offer.

How should I answer 'what are your salary expectations' as an Engineering Manager?

Name a number rather than deflecting, candidates who anchor first tend to get better outcomes, per industry surveys on salary negotiations. Use the relevant band (₹35-60, 55-90, or 90-150+ LPA depending on your level) as your reference point and anchor at the top or slightly above it. If the recruiter pushes back on base salary, shift the conversation to other components, joining bonus, variable pay target, and equity often have more flexibility than the fixed pay line.

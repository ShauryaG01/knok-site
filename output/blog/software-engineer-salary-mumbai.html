01 Salary Overview

Salary Overview

Software Engineer Salary in Mumbai (2026) The honest starting point: Most Indian job listings do not mention salary. Recruiters use phrases like 'as per industry standards' or ask for your current CTC before sharing anything. This guide cuts through that using real market data from knok jobradar (as of 8 July 2026) alongside publicly reported benchmarks. In Mumbai, Software Engineers earn anywhere from 6 LPA at the entry level to 65+ LPA for senior leads, a wide band that makes role title, years of experience, and company tier the three biggest salary levers. Mumbai is India's financial capital, not its tech capital, so the volume of pure software roles here (65 open roles tracked on knok jobradar) is smaller than Bangalore or Hyderabad. But the city has strong demand in fintech, BFSI tech, and global delivery centres of large MNCs, segments that often pay competitively once you clear the negotiation stage.

Entry (0-2 years, 6-12 LPA): Fresh graduates and early-career engineers in Mumbai often join product startups, IT services firms, or BFSI tech teams. Your college tier, internship quality, and interview performance matter more than anything else at this stage. Mid (3-5 years, 15-25 LPA): This is where specialisation starts paying. Engineers who can own a feature end-to-end, backend, APIs, some system design, see strong jumps, especially when switching companies rather than waiting for an internal hike. Senior (6-9 years, 28-45 LPA): Senior engineers in Mumbai who work at MNC delivery centres, fintech product companies, or global banks can reach this band. Stack matters: cloud-native, data engineering, and platform roles tend to sit at the higher end. Lead / Staff (10+ years, 40-65+ LPA): Principal engineers, engineering managers, and staff-level individual contributors. The '+' after 65 is real, publicly reported data on levels.fyi shows top-of-band outcomes well above 65 LPA for staff roles at large product companies, though those are a small slice of the Mumbai market.

04 Negotiation Tips

Negotiation Tips

How to Negotiate Your Salary in Mumbai 1 Never give your current CTC first. Recruiters routinely ask 'current CTC?' before disclosing their budget. You are not legally required to share it. A simple reply: 'I am targeting the market range for this role and experience level, happy to align once we discuss the scope.' 2. Use the bands in this guide as your anchor. When a recruiter says 'we offer as per industry standards,' you can reference the range directly: 'Market data puts this band at 28-45 LPA for senior roles, where does your budget sit?' 3. Separate base from total comp. Mumbai-based MNCs and banks often load a significant portion of pay into bonus, RSUs, or allowances. Ask for the fixed component and variable component separately before comparing offers. 4. Job-switching is your strongest lever. Internal hikes in Indian tech companies are commonly cited at single-digit percentages annually, while job switches routinely yield much larger jumps according to industry surveys. If you are mid-level and have not switched in three years, the market will pay you more than your current employer will. 5. Time your negotiation after the offer, not before. Once a company decides they want you, their switching cost goes up. That is the moment to push, not during the first screening call.

05 Total Comp Breakdown

Total Comp Breakdown

What Makes Up Your Total Compensation In Mumbai, especially in BFSI and MNC roles, your total compensation often has several moving parts beyond the base salary: Fixed Pay (Base + allowances): The number that hits your bank account every month. In Indian payslips, this is typically split into Basic, HRA, and Special Allowance. HRA is particularly relevant in Mumbai given the city's housing costs. Variable / Performance Bonus: Commonly cited as a percentage of fixed pay for mid-senior roles in services firms, and higher in product and fintech. Ask what the average payout has been in recent years, a stated target bonus means nothing if the company rarely pays it in full. Stock / RSUs: Product companies and MNC engineering centres increasingly offer RSUs. For listed Indian companies or US-listed parents, these vest over multiple years and can add material value. Check the vesting cliff before you accept. Joining Bonus: Often offered to offset the loss of unvested equity or a bonus you leave behind. Typically clawed back if you exit within the first year, so read the clause carefully. Benefits: Health insurance, term life cover, meal cards, and internet reimbursement are standard. Mumbai-based employers sometimes add commute allowances given the city's travel costs. When comparing two offers, convert everything to a single-year cash equivalent, that is the only honest apples-to-apples number. Q Questions

Frequently asked

Is Mumbai a good city for software engineering jobs in 2026?

Mumbai has a solid but specialised software job market, 65 open roles tracked by knok jobradar in July 2026, concentrated in fintech, BFSI tech, and MNC delivery centres. It is not Bangalore in terms of sheer volume, but if your background is in financial services technology, payments, or enterprise software, Mumbai employers are actively hiring. The smaller pool also means your relevant resume gets more attention per application.

What salary should a fresher software engineer expect in Mumbai?

Freshers with 0-2 years of experience can expect between 6 and 12 LPA in Mumbai based on current market data. The lower end reflects IT services and smaller startups; the higher end is achievable at product companies or MNC captives if you clear a strong technical interview. Your specific tech stack, college background, and any internship experience shift the number meaningfully within that range.

How much does a senior software engineer earn in Mumbai?

Senior engineers with 6-9 years of experience typically earn 28-45 LPA in Mumbai. Roles in platform engineering, cloud infrastructure, data pipelines, and fintech product development tend to sit at the higher end of that band. Publicly reported data on levels.fyi shows staff and principal roles at large MNCs can go above 65 LPA, though those are a small portion of the Mumbai market.

Does Mumbai pay less than Bangalore for software engineers?

The salary bands for equivalent experience levels are broadly similar between the two cities based on currently available market data. However, Bangalore has far more open roles, 684 versus Mumbai's 65 in the July 2026 knok snapshot, which gives Bangalore candidates more competing offers to use as leverage in negotiations. More competition between companies for talent generally pushes salaries upward over time.

Why do so few Mumbai job listings mention salary?

Salary disclosure is not mandated for most private-sector roles in India, so companies default to 'as per industry standards' to preserve negotiating room. BFSI and MNC employers in Mumbai are often especially tight-lipped until the offer stage. Your best move is to apply broadly, get to the offer conversation, and then anchor the discussion with market data like the bands in this guide.

Should I relocate from Mumbai to Bangalore for a software engineering role?

If your job search is urgent or you are targeting product companies that are primarily Bangalore-based, relocation is worth considering, Bangalore had 684 open software engineer roles versus Mumbai's 65 in the same July 2026 snapshot. But if your background is in fintech, banking technology, or BFSI software, Mumbai roles are a strong fit and the smaller pool means less competition per opening. Relocation is a personal decision, but the data suggests Bangalore offers significantly more raw options.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

The Brex hiring process typically moves through several stages. Here is what candidates publicly report:

Stage 1: Application and Resume Screen
Your application goes to a recruiter or hiring manager who checks for role fit. A strong resume that maps your skills directly to the job description helps here. Generic applications rarely pass.

Stage 2: Recruiter Call
A short introductory call with a recruiter covers your background, motivations, and basic role expectations. This is a two-way check: they assess your fit, and you can ask about the team and the role.

Stage 3: Technical or Role-Specific Screen
Depending on the role, this is a focused session with a hiring manager or technical interviewer. For engineers, expect coding problems. For product or business roles, expect case questions or past-work discussions.

Stage 4: Virtual Onsite (Loop Interviews)
This is the most intensive stage. A series of back-to-back interviews covers technical depth, system design (for engineers), behavioural questions, and cross-functional collaboration. Candidates commonly report four to six conversations in this loop.

Stage 5: Offer and References
Successful candidates receive an offer, sometimes after a reference check. Brex moves relatively quickly at this stage compared to some larger tech companies.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Brex looks for people who can operate with autonomy and move fast. Key traits that come up repeatedly in publicly available interview feedback:

  • Problem-solving clarity: Can you explain your thinking step by step, even under pressure?
  • Ownership mindset: Brex values people who treat problems as their own, not as someone else's job.
  • Cross-functional communication: Most roles require working across engineering, product, and finance teams. They want people who can bridge these conversations without friction.
  • Relevant depth: Your experience should map clearly to the role. Shallow breadth without domain depth tends not to pass the bar.

For engineers specifically, strong data structures knowledge and system design at scale are important. For business roles, a track record of measurable outcomes matters more than credentials alone.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Here is a practical preparation plan:

  1. Tailor your resume to the exact job description. Use the same language Brex uses in the posting. Generic resumes do not pass the screen.
  2. Research Brex's products: Understand how Brex cards, expense management, and treasury products work. Read their engineering blog and recent press.
  3. Practice coding problems if you are applying for an engineering role. Focus on arrays, trees, graphs, and dynamic programming, then move to system design for senior roles.
  4. Prepare STAR stories: For behavioural rounds, have three to five stories ready about times you took ownership, resolved conflict, or delivered under pressure.
  5. Prepare sharp questions to ask: Interviewers notice when candidates ask specific, informed questions about the team or product direction.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Brex has a reputation for a high-performance culture with a startup pace even as the company has grown. A few things define how they work:

  • Speed over perfection (within reason): Brex expects people to ship, learn, and iterate. Long deliberation without output is not rewarded.
  • Direct communication: Feedback is expected to be honest and specific, not softened. This can feel blunt if you are used to more hierarchical workplaces.
  • Remote-first but outcomes-driven: Brex operates across time zones and trusts people to deliver without heavy supervision. Self-discipline matters.
  • Diversity focus: Brex has publicly stated commitments to building inclusive teams, and interview panels are typically diverse.

For Indian professionals accustomed to formal corporate cultures, the directness and autonomy at Brex can be an adjustment. Candidates who thrive here tend to be self-starters who are comfortable disagreeing respectfully and moving fast.

Frequently asked

Does Brex hire in India?

Brex does hire globally and has had India-based openings, though availability changes frequently. Check their careers page regularly or use a tool like knok that monitors 150+ job sites nightly. Remote roles open to India-based applicants also appear from time to time, so it is worth keeping a close eye.

How many open roles does Brex currently have?

As of early July 2026, Brex has 278 open roles across all functions and locations. The mix spans engineering, product, sales, finance, and operations. Role counts change frequently, so treat this as a snapshot rather than a fixed number.

How long does the Brex hiring process take?

Candidates publicly report the full process taking a few weeks from application to offer, though timelines vary by role and team. The virtual onsite is typically the longest milestone to schedule. Following up with your recruiter after each stage is completely normal and expected.

What salary can I expect at Brex?

Brex compensation is competitive with US tech company standards. For India-based roles, publicly reported figures on Glassdoor and levels.fyi suggest compensation varies significantly by level and function. Brex also offers equity as part of total compensation, which candidates should factor into any comparison.

Is there a coding test before the interviews?

Many engineering candidates report a take-home or online coding assessment before the technical phone screen, though the format varies by team. What is the biggest mistake candidates make in Brex interviews?

The most common feedback is arriving unprepared on Brex's actual products and business model. Interviewers expect genuine interest in fintech and spend management, not just technical skills. Showing product curiosity alongside technical or functional depth is what separates strong candidates from average ones.

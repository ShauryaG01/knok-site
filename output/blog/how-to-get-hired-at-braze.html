01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Braze is a customer engagement platform used by brands around the world to send personalised messages across mobile, email, web push, and in-app channels. The company operates across North America, Europe, and Asia Pacific, with teams spanning engineering, product, sales, customer success, and marketing.

As of 2026-07-08, knok jobradar shows Braze has 252 open roles across its global offices. That is a healthy pipeline, meaning the company is actively growing. Indian professionals most often find a fit in software engineering, data, solutions consulting, and customer success roles, many of which involve working with APAC or EMEA clients.

Competition for Braze roles is real. The company attracts strong candidates globally, so the bar is high. That said, Braze hires consistently and rewards people who combine technical depth with a genuine interest in how brands connect with their customers.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Braze's hiring process runs in distinct stages, though the exact format can vary by team, seniority, and location. The following is a general guide based on publicly reported candidate experiences. Treat it as a helpful map, not a guaranteed script.

Stage 1: Recruiter screen. A recruiter reaches out for an initial conversation, usually a short call covering your background, what you are looking for, and basic role fit. Have a clear, concise summary of your experience ready, and know your salary expectations going in.

Stage 2: Hiring manager conversation. This is a deeper discussion with the person you would report to. Expect questions about your specific past work, how you approach problems, and why Braze interests you. For technical roles, there is often a light technical discussion here to calibrate level.

Stage 3: Technical or skills assessment. Engineering candidates typically face coding rounds covering data structures, algorithms, and system design. Product, marketing, and customer success candidates often receive a case study or take-home exercise instead. Braze is looking for clear thinking and good communication, not just the right answer.

Stage 4: Panel or loop interviews. Candidates who clear the assessment meet a panel of cross-functional interviewers, which may include engineers, product managers, and team leads. Expect a mix of behavioural questions, role-specific deep dives, and sometimes a short presentation. This stage can span multiple sessions over one or two days.

Stage 5: Offer and reference check. If the panel is aligned, HR returns with an offer. Reference checks are common at this point, especially for senior roles. Negotiate thoughtfully using data from Glassdoor or levels.fyi for your function and geography.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Braze looks for people who combine solid technical or functional skills with genuine curiosity about customer engagement and data. A few qualities come up repeatedly in publicly reported hiring conversations.

Strong fundamentals. For engineers, comfort with backend systems, APIs, and data pipelines matters more than knowing any one specific framework. For non-technical roles, clear analytical thinking and structured communication matter most.

Customer empathy. Braze's entire product exists to help brands connect with end users. People who can think from the perspective of both the brand and the end customer stand out in interviews.

Cross-functional communication. Braze teams work closely across product, engineering, sales, and customer success. Being able to explain technical ideas in plain language, or business problems in precise terms, is a real advantage.

Ownership mindset. Braze values people who pick up problems and see them through without waiting to be told the next step. Interviewers listen for this in your past examples.

Familiarity with the space. If you have worked with marketing automation tools, CRM platforms, mobile SDKs, or customer data platforms, make that visible on your resume and in your answers. Domain knowledge is a genuine differentiator here.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Preparing for a Braze interview goes beyond brushing up on algorithms. Here is a practical approach that covers the full picture.

Understand the product first. Spend real time with Braze's documentation and look at how brands use Braze for push notifications, in-app messages, email campaigns, and canvas flows. Being able to reference actual use cases in your interview signals serious interest and helps you ask better questions.

Prepare your behavioural stories. Braze uses structured behavioural interviews. Use the STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to prepare specific examples of times you solved a hard problem, navigated ambiguity, handled conflict, or led a project from start to finish.

Practice system design for engineering roles. Be ready to design scalable systems that process high volumes of real-time events. Think through message queues, database choices, caching strategies, and fault tolerance. Braze operates at serious scale, so interviewers want to see that you think beyond the happy path.

Research the company genuinely. Read recent Braze blog posts, look at their product announcements, and check LinkedIn for people in the team you are applying to. Walking into an interview with informed, specific questions is one of the clearest ways to stand out.

Quantify your resume impact. Hiring teams at Braze want to see what you actually built, improved, or delivered, not just a list of tools you used. Replace vague phrases like 'worked on backend systems' with specific outcomes.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Braze describes its culture around values that emphasise boldness, customer focus, and growing together as a team. Employees on public review platforms commonly describe the environment as collaborative and fast-paced, with leadership that is relatively transparent about company direction.

Braze has a globally distributed workforce. Indian professionals working in engineering, solutions consulting, or customer success roles typically collaborate across time zones with colleagues in the US, Singapore, and Europe. The company publicly encourages internal mobility, which means strong performers often move across teams or regions over time.

Work-life expectations can be demanding during product launch cycles and quarter-end periods, which is standard for a company at this scale and growth stage. That said, publicly reported reviews mention a generally supportive management culture and meaningful flexibility outside of peak periods.

Diversity and inclusion are stated priorities at Braze, and the company participates in programmes aimed at bringing more underrepresented groups into tech. For Indian candidates specifically, Braze's Asia Pacific presence creates real pathways, though most India-based candidates end up in roles tied to regional hubs like Singapore or Dubai, or in remote positions serving global accounts.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does Braze hire candidates based in India?

Braze does not currently operate a large India-based office the way some global tech companies do. Most Indian candidates apply to roles in Singapore, Dubai, or remote-eligible positions covering the APAC or EMEA regions. Check the Braze careers page carefully and filter by location to find roles that list India or remote as eligible.

How long does the full Braze hiring process take?

Based on publicly reported candidate experiences, the process from first recruiter call to offer can take anywhere from a few weeks to a couple of months, depending on the role, seniority, and team. Senior or specialised positions tend to run longer because the panel involves more stakeholders and scheduling across time zones adds friction.

What compensation can I expect from Braze?

Braze does not publish India-specific salary bands publicly. For global roles, Glassdoor and levels.fyi list compensation data for Braze positions in markets like the US and Singapore, which can give you a useful reference point. For any role you are interviewing for, ask the recruiter for the band early in the process so you are not surprised at the offer stage.

How difficult is the Braze technical interview for engineering roles?

Publicly reported feedback suggests Braze's coding rounds are competitive but not extreme. The focus is on clean, working solutions and clear communication while you think through the problem, rather than obscure algorithmic tricks. System design rounds tend to be more practical, focusing on real-world scalability challenges that relate to Braze's actual product.

Can I apply to more than one Braze role at the same time?

Yes, applying to multiple roles is generally fine. It is worth being upfront with the recruiter about all the positions you are interested in so they can route you to the best fit. Focus your applications on roles that genuinely match your background rather than applying broadly, as misaligned applications can dilute your credibility with the recruiting team.

How important is prior MarTech or customer engagement experience?

It is a meaningful advantage. Braze's product serves the marketing and growth function, so candidates who have worked with customer data platforms, marketing automation tools, or mobile SDKs tend to connect faster with interviewers. If you do not have direct domain experience, show that you have done genuine homework on how Braze's product works and what problems it solves for brands.

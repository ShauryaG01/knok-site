01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Airwallex is a global fintech company building financial infrastructure for businesses that operate across borders. It helps companies move money, manage accounts, and issue cards internationally, all through one platform. As of July 2026, Airwallex has 673 open roles across engineering, product, design, sales, compliance, finance, and operations. That is a large active pipeline, which means hiring is ongoing rather than sporadic. Roles span offices in Singapore, Hong Kong, the US, the UK, and more, many with remote-friendly or hybrid options for the right candidate. For Indian professionals, Airwallex is an interesting target: it operates in a space, cross-border payments and global financial infrastructure, that increasingly intersects with India's fintech boom. If you have experience in payments, banking technology, or B2B SaaS, your background translates well here.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Airwallex's hiring process is reported to typically run four to five stages, though the exact shape varies by role and team. Here is what candidates commonly describe: Stage 1, Application and Resume Screen
Your resume is reviewed against the job description. A strong match on key skills and relevant domain experience (fintech, payments, SaaS) moves you forward. Tailoring your resume to reflect the specific role language helps significantly. Stage 2, Recruiter Call
A brief introductory conversation with a recruiter. Expect questions about your background, motivation for joining Airwallex, and salary expectations. This is also your chance to ask basic questions about the role, team size, and location setup. Keep your answers focused and direct. Stage 3, Hiring Manager Interview
A deeper conversation with the person you would report to. For technical roles, this often includes a discussion of past projects, system design thinking, or a short take-home or live problem. For business roles, expect questions around how you have driven outcomes, handled ambiguity, and worked cross-functionally. Stage 4, Technical or Skills Rounds
For engineering roles, candidates commonly report one to two technical interview rounds covering data structures, algorithms, or system design depending on seniority. For product roles, this often takes the form of a product case or strategy exercise. For sales and operations, expect role-play scenarios or analytical exercises. Each round is a focused session, and publicly reported feedback suggests Airwallex interviewers value clear thinking over rote answers. Stage 5, Final Round or Panel
Senior leadership or cross-functional stakeholders may join at this stage. Expect values-alignment questions and scenario-based discussions. Culture fit is assessed more formally here. Offer and Background Check
If selected, you will receive an offer followed by a background verification process. The overall process from application to offer commonly runs several weeks.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Airwallex looks for people who combine strong domain expertise with genuine interest in the problems they are solving, moving money globally, reducing friction for businesses, and building reliable financial infrastructure. For engineers, the priorities commonly cited are: strong fundamentals in distributed systems, comfort with ambiguity at scale, and the ability to work across time zones with minimal hand-holding. Experience in payments, financial APIs, or high-throughput backend systems is a clear plus. For product managers, they want people who can translate complex regulatory and technical constraints into simple, user-friendly products. Data fluency and experience working with engineering teams in agile environments matter here. For sales and partnerships, relationship-building skills and an understanding of B2B financial products are key. If you have sold to CFOs, treasury teams, or enterprise finance buyers, that experience is directly relevant. For compliance and legal, knowledge of cross-border payment regulations, AML frameworks, and financial licensing across jurisdictions is highly valued, especially for India, Southeast Asia, or APAC-facing roles. Across all roles, Airwallex values ownership, speed of execution, and the ability to work well across cultures and time zones.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Research the product deeply. Use the Airwallex website to understand their core offerings, global accounts, payment gateway, expense management, and card issuance. Know who their customers are (SMEs, marketplaces, platforms) and why businesses choose them over alternatives. Study the fintech landscape. Be ready to discuss trends in cross-border payments, the role of real-time payment rails, and how regulatory environments, including RBI guidelines for Indian candidates, shape product decisions. Prepare structured stories. Airwallex interviewers tend to ask behavioural questions in a structured format. Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to prepare stories that demonstrate ownership, working through ambiguity, and cross-functional collaboration. For engineers: practise system design. Questions around designing payment systems, handling idempotency, or building reliable APIs at scale are commonly reported. Brush up on distributed systems concepts and be ready to talk about trade-offs. Know your numbers. Whatever your role, be ready to talk about impact in concrete terms, revenue influenced, latency reduced, customers onboarded. Airwallex is a metrics-driven company. Ask smart questions. At every stage, come prepared with thoughtful questions about the team's current challenges, how success is measured in the role, and how the team collaborates across offices. It signals genuine interest. 06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Airwallex describes itself as a company built on speed, ownership, and global thinking. Having scaled quickly from a startup to a large fintech, it retains a high-ownership, move-fast culture even as processes have matured. Global by default. Teams are spread across Asia-Pacific, Europe, and the Americas. Working across time zones and cultures is not an edge case, it is the norm. Candidates who are comfortable with async communication and flexible hours tend to thrive. Builder mindset. Airwallex values people who build things, not just manage them. Even in non-technical roles, there is an expectation that you will roll up your sleeves, identify problems, and drive solutions without waiting to be told. Customer obsession. The company's customers are businesses navigating genuinely complex financial challenges. Employees are expected to deeply understand customer pain points and make decisions that reduce friction for them. Diversity of thought. With a team that spans dozens of nationalities and backgrounds, Airwallex places real value on different perspectives. Indian professionals bring strong engineering talent, financial services knowledge, and familiarity with high-growth emerging markets, all directly relevant to Airwallex's expansion. High bar, high reward. The culture is competitive and the pace is fast. But for the right person, it offers exposure to global markets, complex technical problems, and a product that is genuinely reshaping how businesses move money.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

How many jobs is Airwallex currently hiring for?

As of July 2026, Airwallex has 673 open roles across engineering, product, sales, compliance, and operations. This is a large active pipeline, suggesting ongoing hiring rather than a single recruitment drive. Roles are spread across multiple countries, with a mix of in-office, hybrid, and remote options depending on the team.

Does Airwallex hire candidates based in India?

Airwallex does not currently have a major India office, but many of their roles are open to candidates who can relocate to or work from Singapore, Hong Kong, or other global hubs. Some roles are also remote-friendly. Indian professionals with fintech, payments, or engineering backgrounds are well-suited for their global roles, check each listing carefully for location requirements before applying.

What salary can I expect at Airwallex?

Airwallex does not publish salary bands publicly for most roles. Glassdoor and levels.fyi have some publicly reported figures for their engineering and product positions, but sample sizes are limited so treat these as rough indicators. Compensation typically includes a base salary, equity, and benefits, discuss specifics with the recruiter during the introductory call stage.

How long does the Airwallex hiring process take?

Candidate reports suggest the end-to-end process, from application to offer, commonly runs several weeks, depending on role seniority and team availability. Senior roles with panel interviews may take longer. Staying responsive to recruiter messages and completing any take-home exercises promptly can help keep the process moving on your end.

What should I highlight in my resume for Airwallex?

Highlight any experience in fintech, payments, financial services, or B2B SaaS. Quantify your impact wherever possible, revenue influenced, systems scaled, customers onboarded. Tailor your resume to match the specific job description's language, as Airwallex receives a large volume of applications and keyword alignment matters in the initial screen.

Is Airwallex a good place to work for Indian professionals?

Airwallex offers exposure to global markets, complex financial infrastructure problems, and a fast-paced build culture, all of which can significantly accelerate a career. Industry surveys of fintech employers generally rate companies like Airwallex positively for learning and growth opportunities. The main consideration is that it is a high-ownership, high-pace environment, which suits self-driven professionals better than those who prefer a more structured corporate setting.

01 Overview

Overview

Agoda is a travel technology platform under Booking Holdings, with strong roots in Asia-Pacific and growing reach in India. As of mid-2026, Agoda has 299 open roles, making it one of the more active companies hiring product talent right now. Their PM interviews lean heavily on data thinking, marketplace dynamics, and product metrics, because the core business runs on search, pricing, and supply-demand for hotels and flights.

Candidates typically report a process spanning several rounds: a recruiter screen, one or more product sense interviews, an analytical or case-style round, and a behavioral interview. Some roles include a take-home product assignment. The exact format varies by team and seniority level, so it is worth asking your recruiter about the structure before you start preparing.

Agoda PMs work across hotel search, flights, experiences, payments, loyalty, and property supply management. Knowing which team's role you are interviewing for lets you tailor your examples sharply. The skills tested across all teams are consistent: clear user problem framing, comfort with data and trade-offs, and the ability to influence across engineering, design, and commercial stakeholders.

03 Sample Answers (STAR Format)

Sample Answers (STAR Format)

Q: Tell me about a product you shipped. What was the hardest trade-off you made?

*Situation:* At my previous company, our mobile checkout flow had significant drop-off at the payment step, particularly on mid-range Android devices.

*Task:* My goal was to reduce abandonment while keeping engineering scope minimal, because the team was already committed to a major feature launch in the same quarter.

*Action:* I analyzed session recordings and ran short user interviews, then identified that users were overwhelmed by the number of payment options shown upfront. I proposed a 'smart default' approach: surface the most contextually relevant payment method first, based on user location and past behavior. The hard trade-off was de-emphasizing several less-used payment options, which added one extra tap for a smaller segment of users. I aligned stakeholders by showing that the improvement for the majority outweighed the minor friction for the minority, and I set a clear rollback condition if the affected segment showed a meaningful drop in completion.

*Result:* Post-launch data showed a meaningful improvement in checkout completion. The engineering effort was a fraction of a full redesign, and we shipped on time alongside the main feature launch.

---

Q: Tell me about a time you made a product decision without complete data.

*Situation:* I had to decide whether to prioritize a 'Price Alert' feature on our app. User interviews were positive, but we had no quantitative signal on actual engagement or willingness to enable notifications.

*Task:* The team needed a go-or-no-go decision within a week to plan the next sprint.

*Action:* I leaned on proxy signals: support tickets consistently asking about price tracking, competitor app reviews that frequently praised price alerts as a loved feature, and session data showing users repeatedly bookmarking properties without booking. I framed the recommendation as a time-boxed bet: build a lightweight version first, with a clear success metric and an explicit decision point to invest further or cut the feature. I was transparent with leadership that we were acting on directional signals, not hard evidence, and got alignment on the exit condition upfront.

*Result:* The lightweight version launched and engagement came in well above our internal target, giving the team confidence to build the full feature in the following cycle.

---

Q: A hotel partner says their property is getting fewer bookings after an algorithm update. How do you respond?

*Situation:* After we updated our hotel ranking algorithm to weight recent guest reviews more heavily, a mid-size partner flagged a sharp booking drop and threatened to delist their property.

*Task:* I needed to determine whether the algorithm caused the drop and resolve the partner's concern, without compromising the integrity of the ranking logic.

*Action:* I pulled booking trend data alongside the algorithm change timestamps, then checked the property's recent review score trajectory. The data showed their recent reviews had dipped, which was legitimately lowering their rank. The algorithm was working as intended. I prepared a data-backed explanation for the partner showing the exact correlation between their review trend and rank movement. I worked with the partner success team to give the property a concrete action plan: respond to guest feedback faster, address recurring complaints in the listing, and request a re-evaluation after their score recovered. I did not offer any manual rank adjustment.

*Result:* The partner appreciated the transparency and stayed on the platform. Their review score improved over the following weeks, and bookings gradually recovered.

04 Answer Frameworks

Answer Frameworks

For product sense questions: Start by defining the user and their core problem before jumping to solutions. Walk the interviewer through your reasoning: user segment, key pain point, prioritized solutions, and the metric you would use to measure success. Do not propose features until you have framed the problem clearly.

For metrics and diagnosis questions: Use a funnel breakdown. Start at the top of the user journey and work down to find where the drop occurs. Then list hypotheses by category: technical issues, user behavior changes, competitive shifts, or seasonal patterns. Agoda interviewers respond well to candidates who separate correlation from causation before proposing fixes.

For prioritization questions: Ground your answer in business goals first. State the objective for Agoda (typically revenue per session, supply quality, or user retention), then evaluate options against that goal using an impact-versus-effort frame. Avoid saying 'it depends' without immediately following up with exactly what it depends on and why.

For behavioral questions (STAR): Keep the Situation and Task sections brief. Spend most of your time on the Action section, because that is where interviewers evaluate your judgment and decision-making process. Be specific about what you personally did, not just what the team achieved.

For design and strategy questions: Narrow the scope deliberately before brainstorming. Pick a specific user segment and a specific scenario. Agoda interviewers tend to reward focused, well-reasoned answers over broad feature lists that try to solve for every traveler type at once.

05 What Interviewers Want

What Interviewers Want

Data-first instincts: Agoda is a highly analytical company. Interviewers want to see you reach for metrics naturally, not as an afterthought. When discussing a past decision, mention what data you had, what you wished you had, and how you closed the gap.

Marketplace and supply-demand thinking: Agoda operates a two-sided marketplace, with travelers on one side and property or supply partners on the other. Candidates who factor in both sides when solving problems stand out. Do not treat Agoda like a simple consumer app.

Comfort with ambiguity: Travel is seasonal, global, and price-sensitive. Interviewers look for PMs who can scope down a fuzzy problem, make a reasoned call, and move forward without waiting for perfect information.

Clear articulation of trade-offs: Strong candidates do not just propose solutions. They explain what they are giving up, who might be negatively affected, and how they plan to monitor for unintended consequences.

Ownership and cross-functional influence: Agoda PMs work closely with engineering, data science, and commercial teams. Behavioral answers should clearly show how you drove alignment and influenced decisions across different functions, not just within your own team.

06 Preparation Plan

Preparation Plan

Understand the product first: Use Agoda as a real traveler before your interview. Search for hotels, go through checkout, explore the app on both hotel and flight flows. Note what feels broken, confusing, or missing. This direct product experience is the foundation for answering every case question with specificity.

Build your behavioral story bank: Write out stories covering: a product you shipped end to end, a decision made with incomplete data, a conflict with a stakeholder, a time you failed and what you learned, and a prioritization call made under resource constraints. Map each story to the STAR format and practice out loud, not just in your head.

Practice case and metrics questions: Take several questions from the list above and work through each as if in a real interview. Record yourself or do a mock session with a peer. For every case answer, explicitly state your assumptions, define the user, and name the specific metric you would move.

Research the team and role: Read Agoda's publicly available blog posts and product announcements from 2024 to 2026. Ask your recruiter which team the role sits in, and research that product area specifically. Prepare a few thoughtful questions for your interviewers about current challenges on their team.

Before each round: Review your resume line by line and have a crisp story ready for every product or feature mentioned. Confirm the round format with your recruiter. 07 Common Mistakes

Common Mistakes

Jumping to solutions without framing the problem. Agoda interviewers frequently report that candidates skip user framing and go straight to feature ideas. Spend the first part of any case question establishing who the user is and what specific problem matters most before proposing anything.

Using vague metrics. Saying 'I would track engagement' is not enough. Name the exact metric: booking conversion rate, sessions per user per month, supplier listing click-through rate. Vague metrics signal shallow product thinking to experienced interviewers.

Ignoring the supply side. Agoda is a marketplace. Many candidates answer only from the traveler's perspective. Strong answers consider how a decision affects hotel, activity, or supply partners, not just end users.

Telling team stories instead of personal stories. In behavioral rounds, interviewers want to know what you specifically did. Answers heavy on 'we' without a clear 'I' lose credibility. Be specific about the decisions and actions that were yours.

Over-engineering the solution. Some candidates propose large, complex features when a simple, well-reasoned fix would be more credible. Agoda interviewers value pragmatism. A focused solution with clear success criteria beats an ambitious roadmap with no grounding.

Not asking clarifying questions. Diving into a case without clarifying scope, user segment, or business context is a red flag. Take a moment at the start to ask a few targeted questions. It signals structured thinking, not uncertainty.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

How many interview rounds does Agoda typically have for PM roles?

Candidates report going through several rounds, commonly including a recruiter call, a product sense interview, an analytical or case round, and a behavioral interview. Some roles also include a take-home assignment before the final rounds. The exact number varies by level and team, so asking your recruiter after your first call is the best way to plan your preparation. Do not assume the same structure applies across all Agoda PM roles.

What salary can I expect as a PM at Agoda in India?

Agoda does not publicly disclose its salary ranges, but industry surveys and Glassdoor data for PM roles in Indian tech companies show these commonly cited bands: Associate PM in the 12-20 LPA range, PM with three to six years of experience in the 24-40 LPA range, Senior PM in the 40-60 LPA range, and Group or Principal PM at 55 LPA and above. Actual offers depend on your specific experience, the role's scope, your location, and your negotiation.

Does Agoda ask a take-home case or assignment during the PM interview?

Some candidates report receiving a take-home product assignment, particularly for senior or specialized roles. The assignment typically involves analyzing a product area, defining a strategy, or proposing a feature with supporting rationale. Treat it like a concise product document: clear user framing, a defined north star metric, and explicit trade-offs. Confirm with your recruiter whether this step applies to your specific role before you start preparing for it.

Do I need travel industry experience to get a PM role at Agoda?

Travel industry experience is not a hard requirement, but familiarity with marketplace dynamics, pricing, and international user behavior is a real advantage. Interviewers care more about how you think than where you have worked before. Candidates from fintech, e-commerce, and logistics backgrounds tend to do well because of overlapping skills in conversion optimization and two-sided marketplace thinking. Spending time using Agoda's product deeply before your interview helps bridge any domain gap quickly.

How long does Agoda's PM hiring process typically take from first call to offer?

Candidates typically report the end-to-end process taking a few weeks from the first recruiter call to an offer, though timelines can stretch longer for senior roles or when there are scheduling gaps between rounds. Following up with your recruiter after each round to confirm next steps and expected timelines is a good habit. Some roles move faster depending on team urgency, so staying responsive to recruiter outreach helps you avoid unnecessary delays.

Does Agoda hire for Associate PM or entry-level PM roles?

Agoda does hire for Associate PM roles and currently has active PM openings across multiple seniority levels. For Associate PM candidates, expect a stronger focus on analytical ability, structured thinking, and product instincts rather than deep industry experience. Building a strong product case study or a project that demonstrates user empathy and data-driven reasoning can significantly strengthen your application at this level. Reviewing the specific job description carefully will help you tailor your preparation for the level they are hiring at.

01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Palantir is an American data analytics company whose platforms, Foundry, Gotham, and the AI Platform (AIP), are used by governments, defence agencies, and large commercial enterprises. India hiring has grown steadily, and as of July 2026, Palantir has 276 open roles across engineering, product, operations, and business development.

Palantir is genuinely selective. They are not trying to hire in large batches. They want people who can think through hard, ambiguous problems and who believe in the mission behind the work. The process is thorough, sometimes long, and designed to test both your technical ability and the way you approach problems you have never seen before.

Roles span Forward Deployed Engineers (FDEs), software engineers, product managers, and deployment strategists. FDE roles in particular blend deep technical work with client-facing problem solving, a combination Palantir values highly across many positions.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

The stages below are based on publicly reported candidate experiences and may vary by role, team, and location. Treat this as a general guide rather than a guaranteed sequence.

Stage 1: Application and resume screen
Your resume goes through an internal screen. Palantir looks for strong academic or professional backgrounds, meaningful projects, and evidence of independent thinking. A well-targeted resume that shows problem-solving impact matters more than a generic one.

Stage 2: Online assessment or take-home (for many engineering roles)
Many candidates receive a timed online coding assessment. Problems tend to test algorithmic thinking and clean code rather than esoteric tricks. Some roles skip this in favour of a direct recruiter call.

Stage 3: Recruiter call
A short call covering your background, interest in Palantir, and logistics. Come with a clear answer to why Palantir specifically, not just why a data or AI company in general.

Stage 4: Technical phone interview
For engineering roles, expect one or two rounds of live coding or system design. For non-engineering roles, expect a case-style problem or a structured discussion of past work. Interviewers pay close attention to how you think out loud, not just whether you reach the right answer.

Stage 5: Final round (sometimes called a Superday or on-site)
Typically involves multiple back-to-back interviews in a single day. This may include technical depth interviews, a problem-solving session using a dataset or scenario, and a culture or values conversation. The goal is to see you under sustained pressure and to assess how you collaborate.

Stage 6: Offer and background check
Offers can take time to arrive after the final round. Follow up politely with your recruiter if you have not heard back within a couple of weeks.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Genuine problem-solving ability
Palantir cares less about what you have memorised and more about how you approach a problem you have never seen. They want structured thinking, comfort with ambiguity, and the ability to make reasonable assumptions and move forward without waiting for a perfect brief.

Mission alignment
Palantir works on sensitive, high-stakes problems for governments and large institutions. They look for candidates who have thought seriously about the kind of work the company does and who are interested in real-world impact, not just technical puzzles.

Strong communication
Many roles, especially FDE positions, require you to explain complex technical ideas to non-technical clients. Even purely engineering roles benefit from candidates who can write and speak clearly under pressure.

Ownership and initiative
Palantir teams are small by design. Each person is expected to own outcomes, not just tasks. Candidates who show evidence of taking initiative, solving problems without being asked, and seeing projects through to completion stand out.

Technical depth (for engineering roles)
Solid fundamentals in algorithms, data structures, and system design are expected. Palantir does not usually ask the most obscure competitive programming problems, but they expect clean, efficient code and thoughtful discussion of trade-offs.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Research the company seriously
Read about Palantir Foundry, Gotham, and AIP. Understand the kinds of clients they serve and the problems those clients face. Generic answers about 'making an impact' will not land. Specific, informed answers about why their approach to data integration or AI deployment interests you will.

Practise thinking out loud
Palantir interviewers want to follow your reasoning. Solve problems with a friend or mock interviewer and narrate your thought process as you go. Silence while you think is less useful to them than a running commentary on your assumptions and trade-offs.

Revise algorithms and system design
For engineering roles, cover common data structures, graph algorithms, dynamic programming, and system design principles. Platforms like LeetCode for coding practice, paired with system design primers that are widely used by candidates, will cover most of what comes up.

Prepare strong behavioural stories
Use the STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result) for past experiences. Focus on situations where you took initiative, dealt with ambiguity, or delivered under pressure. Palantir values these stories as much as the technical rounds.

Prepare sharp questions for your interviewers
Candidates who ask informed questions about the team's work, the deployment challenges they face, or how decisions get made tend to leave a stronger impression. Avoid generic questions in early rounds.

Apply to the right roles
With 276 open roles at Palantir right now, spend time finding the ones that genuinely match your background. A targeted application with a resume tailored to the specific job description will outperform a broad spray-and-pray approach every time.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Palantir has a distinctive culture that not everyone will find comfortable, and the company is fairly upfront about that. Understanding it before you apply will help you decide whether the fit is genuine.

Mission over comfort
The company was built on the belief that good technology can solve genuinely hard societal problems. Employees are expected to care about the outcomes of their work, not just the elegance of the code. This is not a place where work is purely abstract.

Small teams, high ownership
Teams at Palantir tend to be lean. Each person carries significant responsibility. There is limited hand-holding, and the expectation is that you will figure things out when the path is not clear.

Intellectual intensity
Conversations at Palantir tend to be direct and substantive. Disagreement is expected and valued when it is well-reasoned. If you prefer environments where ideas are accepted without debate, this culture may feel uncomfortable.

Meritocracy
Promotion and recognition are driven by results and demonstrated impact rather than seniority or tenure. This works well for high performers who want to move quickly, and less well for those who prefer predictable career ladders.

Honest note on hours
Publicly reported employee reviews on Glassdoor suggest that hours at Palantir can be demanding, particularly for FDE roles that involve travel and client-facing work. This varies by team and project cycle, so it is worth asking your recruiter directly.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

How long does Palantir's hiring process take from application to offer?

Publicly reported timelines vary widely, from a few weeks to a few months, depending on the role and how quickly rounds are scheduled. Engineering roles with multiple technical stages tend to take longer than business or operations roles. It is reasonable to follow up with your recruiter if you have not heard back within a couple of weeks of completing any stage.

Does Palantir hire freshers or only experienced candidates?

Palantir does hire new graduates, particularly into software engineering and FDE roles, through campus recruitment and direct applications. They are selective at every level, though. New graduates who stand out typically have strong internship experience, notable personal projects, or a strong academic record in computer science or a closely related field.

What is a Forward Deployed Engineer (FDE) role at Palantir?

An FDE is an engineer who works directly at client sites, helping to deploy and customise Palantir's platforms for specific use cases. The role blends software engineering with consulting and client communication, and it typically involves significant travel. Both technical depth and the ability to explain complex systems to non-technical stakeholders are required.

Is it possible to get hired at Palantir from India?

Yes. Palantir has offices and distributed hiring across multiple regions, and Indian candidates have joined through both India-based roles and international positions. With 276 open roles as of mid-2026, there are opportunities across engineering, operations, and business development. Checking the careers page regularly and tailoring your application to specific roles improves your chances significantly.

How competitive is Palantir compared to other top tech companies?

Palantir is considered highly selective, comparable in difficulty to other top-tier technology employers. The interview process is particularly focused on problem-solving approach and mission alignment, which makes preparation different from a standard algorithm grind. Candidates who research the company deeply and practise explaining their thinking tend to perform better than those who only revise data structures.

What kind of salary can I expect at Palantir?

Palantir is commonly cited as a top-paying employer in the technology sector. Glassdoor and levels.fyi list compensation data for Palantir roles, though figures vary significantly by location, role level, and whether stock compensation is included. Researching role-specific data on those platforms before you reach the offer stage will give you a more accurate picture than any general estimate.

01 Salary Overview

Salary Overview

Engineering Manager compensation in India varies by city, company stage, and whether the role is product, platform, or growth-focused. Fixed pay is quoted in LPA (lakhs per annum); variable pay and ESOPs are common at startups. Always clarify fixed vs CTC before negotiating.

04 Negotiation Tips

Negotiation Tips

  1. Anchor with data from 3+ offers or trusted peers at the same level.
  2. Negotiate fixed first; treat variable and ESOP separately.
  3. Ask for signing bonus if base is capped.
  4. Get the offer letter with break-up (basic, HRA, PF) before resigning.
  5. If competing offers exist, share timelines, not numbers, to accelerate decisions.
05 Total Comp Breakdown

Total Comp Breakdown

CTC typically includes: Basic, HRA, special allowance, PF employer contribution, gratuity provision, and projected variable. Ask for in-hand estimate after tax. ESOPs at startups may not appear in CTC, model dilution and vesting cliff separately.

06 Market Snapshot

Market Snapshot

knok jobradar tracked 53 open Engineering Manager roles in Delhi NCR as of 8 July 2026. The city ranks second nationally for this role, behind Bangalore (182 roles), but well ahead of Pune (20), Chennai (20), Hyderabad (16), and Mumbai (12).

The Delhi NCR EM market has a distinct character shaped by three forces: a strong GCC (global capability centre) presence, a growing fintech and SaaS product company scene, and a cluster of well-funded startups building in logistics, robotics, and travel tech. This mix means Engineering Manager job descriptions vary widely. Some roles are about running a large delivery team inside a multinational. Others are about owning a product area end-to-end in a lean, fast-moving company. Knowing which environment suits you before you apply saves a lot of time and leads to better-fit applications.

09 How To Stand Out

How To Stand Out

Delhi NCR hiring managers shortlist candidates who can point to specific, concrete team-building work. A few things that consistently move profiles to the top of the pile:

Lead with team scope and outcomes. In your resume and LinkedIn headline, state the size of the team you have managed and a concrete result: a product shipped, incident rate reduced, hiring target met ahead of schedule. 'Managed a team of engineers' is weak. A specific example of what your team delivered under your leadership is what gets you a call.

Show that you have hired before. GCCs and funded product companies are expanding their India engineering centres and actively need EMs who have run hiring pipelines, not just sat in a few interview loops. If you have built a team from scratch, put that front and centre.

Tailor your story for GCC versus product company. For Deutsche Telekom or Bupa, emphasise global stakeholder management, process maturity, and cross-timezone delivery. For Paytm, GreyOrange, or ixigo, emphasise speed, ownership, and the constraints your team shipped under.

Technical credibility still matters. EM interviews in Delhi NCR commonly include a system design round and sometimes a coding component. Be ready to discuss architecture trade-offs with senior engineers, not just people-management philosophy.

Research before you apply. Wingify and Shipsy both maintain active engineering blogs. Referencing a specific technical decision or product challenge in your cover note signals genuine interest and stands apart from the majority of generic applications.

10 Application Strategy

Application Strategy

With 53 roles spread across GCCs, product companies, and startups, a focused approach beats applying to everything that appears in a job board search. Here is how to work the Delhi NCR EM market efficiently:

Pick your lane first. Decide whether you want GCC stability, product-company ownership, or startup upside before you start tailoring your resume. Mixing all three in your applications without adjusting your story makes every application weaker and your profile harder to read.

Start with the anchor companies. Deutsche Telekom Digital Labs (12 open roles) and Paytm (8 open roles) are actively scaling their Delhi NCR engineering organisations. High hiring volumes mean processes move faster and referrals carry more weight because more internal people are part of the decision.

Do not overlook the smaller names. GreyOrange, Wingify, Shipsy, and ixigo each have fewer open roles, but competition is lower too. A well-researched application to a company whose product you genuinely find interesting often outperforms a generic application to a better-known employer.

Follow up directly on LinkedIn. Hiring managers at companies like Skeps and Hanu often respond to a short, personalised message from an applicant. Two lines is enough: one on what caught your attention about their engineering work, one on what you bring to the role.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Which type of company is better for an Engineering Manager in Delhi NCR: GCC, product company, or startup?

It depends on what you optimise for. GCCs offer defined career ladders, global exposure, and structured compensation with bonuses. Product companies and startups give more ownership over roadmap and team decisions, but the pace and ambiguity are higher. If you are early in your EM career, a GCC provides more structural support and clearer feedback cycles. If you have already managed teams and want faster growth with more accountability, a product company or funded startup is usually more rewarding.

Is 53 open roles a healthy job market for Engineering Managers in Delhi NCR?

It is a solid, active market rather than an overheated one. Bangalore has more than three times the volume for this role, reflecting its larger engineering talent concentration. Delhi NCR's 53 active roles represent a selective market where tailored applications matter more than volume. The upside is that competition per role is somewhat lower than in Bangalore, especially at product companies and startups where fewer senior candidates apply.

What experience level do most of these Delhi NCR EM roles target?

Most open roles sit at the Manager level and commonly require a solid track record of direct people management alongside strong technical depth. Senior Manager roles at companies like Deutsche Telekom and Paytm typically expect experience managing multiple teams or leading other team leads. Director roles are fewer and tend to be filled through referrals or executive search rather than open job postings, so they rarely show up in standard job board searches.

How long does the hiring process typically take for EM roles at these companies?

GCCs like Deutsche Telekom Digital Labs and Bupa typically run multi-round processes involving HR screens, technical panels, and leadership interviews spread across several weeks. Product companies like Paytm and ixigo tend to move faster once a candidate looks strong. Startups like Skeps and Wingify can close quickly when there is a clear fit. These timelines are based on general industry patterns rather than company-disclosed data, so treat them as rough guidance only.

Should I negotiate salary differently for a GCC versus a startup offer?

Yes, the approach differs meaningfully. For GCC offers, the base salary is often less flexible, but performance bonuses, allowances, and benefits have more room. Ask about the variable component structure and how consistently the bonus pays out at target. For startup offers, the fixed salary negotiation window is wider, but ESOPs are where the real long-term upside sits. Ask about the vesting schedule, cliff period, and the most recent funding valuation before placing much weight on stock numbers.

Is it worth applying to Lateral - APAC and Hanu Software Solutions if I am targeting a product company role?

Lateral is a staffing and RPO firm, so their EM openings are typically client placements rather than internal roles. A well-connected recruiter there may have visibility into roles that are not publicly posted, which can work in your favour if you are open about the type of company you want. Hanu Software Solutions is an IT services and cloud solutions firm. If your background is in services delivery, it is a strong direct fit. If you are aiming for a pure product role eventually, be upfront with the recruiter about your direction so you do not get placed somewhere misaligned with your goals.

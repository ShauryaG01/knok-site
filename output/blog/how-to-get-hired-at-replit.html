01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Replit is a browser-based platform where anyone can write, run, and deploy code instantly without local setup. The company builds for developers around the world and has a large and growing community in India, where students and working professionals use it to learn coding and ship projects. As of early July 2026, Replit is actively hiring across 103 open roles spanning software engineering, infrastructure, product, design, and business teams.

Replit runs a lean, high-output team with a strong remote-first culture. Many engineering and product roles are open to candidates outside San Francisco, which makes it accessible for skilled professionals in cities like Bengaluru, Hyderabad, and Pune. If you enjoy building tools that developers love and want to work somewhere your code reaches a large global user base fast, Replit is worth a serious look.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Replit's hiring process is reported to move quickly and focuses on real coding ability and product thinking rather than textbook puzzles. The stages below are based on publicly reported candidate experiences and may vary by role and team.

Stage 1: Application and resume screen. The recruiting team reviews your resume for relevant experience. Open-source work, side projects, and a strong GitHub profile carry real weight at this stage.

Stage 2: Recruiter or hiring manager call. A brief introductory call to discuss your background, your interest in Replit, and the specifics of the role. Expect questions about why you want to work at a developer tools company and what you have actually shipped recently.

Stage 3: Technical screen. For engineering roles this is usually a live coding session. Problems tend to be practical rather than purely algorithmic, often involving building a small feature or solving a real product scenario. Replit's own IDE is sometimes used for this round.

Stage 4: Technical and cross-functional interviews. A deeper set of rounds covering system design, coding, and sometimes a product or architecture discussion. Depending on the role you may speak with engineers, a product manager, or a team lead.

Stage 5: Final round and offer. A conversation with senior leadership or a hiring committee, followed by an offer if all rounds go well. Replit is publicly reported to give feedback faster than most large tech companies.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Replit values builders above all else. Across engineering, product, and design roles the company looks for people who have a habit of shipping things rather than just talking about them.

Genuine product sense. Replit is a product-led company. Engineers are expected to care about the user experience, not just the code underneath it. If you have strong opinions about what makes a developer tool delightful or frustrating, that is a real plus.

Comfort with ambiguity. The team is lean relative to the scale of the product. You will be expected to own problems end-to-end, define your own scope, and make decisions without heavy process or hand-holding from managers.

Depth in a core skill. Whether you are a frontend engineer with deep React knowledge, an infrastructure engineer who understands distributed systems, or a designer with a sharp portfolio, Replit wants clear evidence that you are genuinely excellent at something specific.

Side projects and open-source contributions. Replit's users are builders by nature. The company tends to trust candidates who build things in their own time. Repls, GitHub repositories, or anything you have shipped publicly will strengthen your application significantly.

Written communication. As a remote-first company, async writing is core to daily work. Clear, direct writing in your cover letter and during interviews signals that you can collaborate well across time zones without constant check-ins.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Build something on Replit before you apply. This sounds obvious but many candidates skip it. Use the platform to build a real project, understand its quirks, and form a genuine opinion about it. You will almost certainly be asked what you would improve about Replit, and a lived experience answer beats a guessed one every time.

Sharpen your live coding skills. Replit's technical screens are practical. Practice solving problems in a live environment while talking through your thinking as you go. Focus on clean, working code rather than clever one-liners that are hard to explain.

Prepare system design answers with real examples. For senior engineering roles, be ready to discuss how you would design systems that handle real scale. Ground your answers in things you have actually built, not frameworks you memorised from a prep book.

Study Replit's recent product launches. Follow Replit's blog, changelog, and social channels to understand what the team has shipped recently. Showing familiarity with their product direction signals genuine interest rather than a generic application.

Write a strong cover letter or application note. Replit is a writing-heavy remote culture. A short, direct note explaining why you want to work on developer tools, what you have built, and what you would bring to the team can separate you from candidates with similar resumes.

Apply across the open roles that fit you. With 103 open roles live right now, there is real breadth across functions and seniority levels. Identify the roles that match your background most closely and apply directly. A referral from someone already at Replit can speed up the process considerably.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Replit describes its mission as bringing software creation to as many people as possible. In practice this shapes a culture that prizes speed, ownership, and a genuine love of building things.

Small team, large impact. Replit has a lean team relative to its product scale. Engineers and product managers typically own large surface areas and wear multiple hats. This is energising if you want to move fast and see your work matter quickly. It can feel overwhelming if you prefer clear role boundaries and structured support.

Remote-first and async by default. The team is distributed across time zones. Written communication, documented decisions, and async collaboration are core to how work gets done. Strong performers here are self-directed and proactive about sharing context without being asked.

Builder culture. The company was founded by developers for developers. There is a strong internal norm of actually using the product you are building. Internal hackathons, shared Repls, and enthusiasm for new technology are part of everyday life at the company.

Direct feedback and fast iteration. Replit is publicly reported to have a culture of honest, direct feedback internally. Teams ship fast, iterate in public, and discuss failures openly rather than burying them. If you come from a more hierarchical or process-heavy environment, expect a real adjustment period.

Global and inclusive hiring. Replit's user base spans the world and the company reflects that in its hiring. Candidates from India and other countries outside the US have joined the team across engineering, product, and business roles.

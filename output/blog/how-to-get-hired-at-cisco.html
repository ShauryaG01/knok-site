01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Cisco is a global technology leader in networking, cybersecurity, cloud, and collaboration tools. In India, the company runs large engineering and operations centres, primarily in Bangalore and Chennai, making it one of the most sought-after tech employers in the country. As of July 2026, Cisco has 208 open roles, spanning software engineering, network engineering, cybersecurity, product management, sales engineering, and customer support functions.

Entry-level positions come through campus hiring programs, while mid-to-senior roles are filled through direct applications, employee referrals, and specialist recruiters. Getting hired at Cisco requires a mix of strong technical fundamentals, clear communication, and alignment with the company's focus on security-first thinking and customer outcomes. Competition is high, but Cisco hires at scale and actively looks beyond a handful of 'brand-name' colleges.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Cisco's hiring process typically runs across several stages. The exact number of rounds can vary by role, level, and team, but here is what candidates generally experience.

Application and Resume Screen: Your resume is reviewed by the talent team. Roles are posted on Cisco's careers site, LinkedIn, and major Indian job portals. A strong match on skills like networking protocols, cloud platforms, programming languages, or cybersecurity frameworks improves your shortlisting chances.

Recruiter Call: A recruiter reaches out to shortlisted candidates for a short conversation covering your background, notice period, compensation expectations, and interest in the specific role. This is also your chance to ask about the team structure and day-to-day work.

Technical Screening: For engineering roles, this is usually a coding or system design round on an online platform. Networking roles may include questions on protocols like BGP, OSPF, or MPLS. Cybersecurity positions often feature scenario-based questions on threat detection and incident response.

Technical Interviews (1-2 rounds): These are in-depth sessions with engineers or architects from the hiring team. Expect data structures and algorithms, system design for senior roles, debugging exercises, and domain-specific deep dives. Interviewers pay close attention to how you think through problems, not just whether you reach the correct answer.

Hiring Manager Round: The hiring manager evaluates cultural fit, motivation, and team alignment. This round typically involves behavioral questions about past projects, how you handle conflict, and your approach to working with ambiguity.

Offer: Successful candidates receive a verbal offer, followed by a formal offer letter after background verification is complete.

Publicly reported candidate experiences suggest the full process can take anywhere from a few weeks to a couple of months, depending on role level and team availability.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Cisco looks for a combination of technical depth and collaborative instincts. For engineering roles, strong fundamentals in networking, cloud, or software development are central. Python, Go, Java, and automation skills appear frequently across job descriptions. For security roles, familiarity with SIEM tools, zero-trust frameworks, and incident response processes is valued.

Beyond technical skills, Cisco places real weight on communication. Candidates who can explain complex technical decisions to non-technical stakeholders tend to stand out. Problem-solving under pressure, an ownership mindset, and comfort working across global time zones are traits that interviewers actively probe for.

Cisco also values diversity of thought and hires from varied educational and professional backgrounds. Relevant certifications like CCNA, CCNP, AWS, or Azure can strengthen your profile, particularly for network engineering and cloud roles, though they are not always listed as hard requirements.

05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Start with the fundamentals relevant to your target role. For software engineering, revise data structures, algorithms, and system design concepts. For networking roles, brush up on routing protocols, switching, and network troubleshooting scenarios. For cybersecurity, review common attack vectors, mitigation strategies, and compliance frameworks.

Study Cisco's product portfolio. Understanding how technologies like Webex, Meraki, Umbrella, and SecureX connect to real business outcomes shows genuine interest and helps you have sharper conversations during interviews. Cisco interviewers appreciate candidates who understand the 'why' behind technology choices, not just the 'what'.

Practise behavioral questions using the STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result). Cisco interviews frequently include questions about handling ambiguity, leading without formal authority, or turning around a struggling project. Have two or three concrete, specific stories ready before you walk in.

Check publicly shared interview experiences on Glassdoor. These give you a realistic sense of question patterns and difficulty levels at each stage, and they are updated regularly by candidates who have been through the process recently.

Referrals carry real weight at Cisco. If you know someone at the company, a warm introduction can get your resume noticed significantly faster than a cold application. Connect genuinely with Cisco employees on LinkedIn, especially those in teams you want to join, well before you apply.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Cisco runs on what it calls its Conscious Culture, built around values like inclusion, innovation, and giving back to the communities it operates in. In practice, this means the company invests in employee resource groups, mental health benefits, flexible work arrangements, and social impact programs that employees can participate in directly.

The work environment in India offices is collaborative and globally connected. Teams in Bangalore and Chennai regularly work with counterparts in the US, Europe, and APAC regions. Strong written communication and comfort with asynchronous collaboration are day-to-day requirements, not optional extras. If you have experience working across time zones or with distributed teams, mention it clearly during interviews.

Cisco has consistently appeared on Great Place to Work lists in India. The company is known for internal learning programs, support for professional certifications, and encouraging engineers to take on stretch assignments outside their immediate role. Career growth at Cisco tends to reward those who take initiative, build cross-functional relationships, and stay current with technology shifts in areas like AI-driven networking, cloud security, and infrastructure automation.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

How many roles is Cisco currently hiring for in India?

As of July 2026, Cisco has 208 open roles across its India operations. These span engineering, product, sales, and support functions across offices in Bangalore and Chennai. The number shifts regularly as roles are filled and new ones open, so checking frequently through a job aggregator or directly on Cisco's careers page gives you the most current picture.

Does Cisco hire freshers straight out of college?

Yes, Cisco runs campus hiring programs that target engineering graduates from select institutions across India. These programs typically look for students from computer science, electronics, or information technology backgrounds. Freshers who do not get picked through campus drives can also apply to entry-level roles through the general careers portal, though those positions tend to attract more competition from experienced candidates as well.

How important are Cisco certifications like CCNA for getting hired?

For networking roles, a CCNA or CCNP certification is a strong differentiator and signals that you understand Cisco's technology stack at a practical level. For software engineering or product roles, certifications like AWS or Azure tend to be more relevant than Cisco-specific ones. Certifications are rarely listed as hard requirements but can tip the balance when two candidates have similar experience on paper.

What salary can I expect at Cisco India?

Compensation at Cisco India varies by role, level, and location. Glassdoor and levels.fyi both carry publicly reported salary data for Cisco India positions across engineering and non-engineering tracks, and these are the most reliable places to benchmark expectations before your offer conversation. Industry surveys consistently place Cisco among the higher-paying tech employers in India, particularly for senior engineering and architecture roles.

How long does Cisco's hiring process usually take?

Based on publicly reported candidate experiences, the process from application to offer can take anywhere from a few weeks to a couple of months. Senior roles and specialised positions tend to take longer because more rounds are involved and more stakeholders weigh in on the final decision. Staying responsive to recruiter messages and following up politely after each stage can help keep things from stalling.

Does Cisco offer remote or hybrid work for India-based roles?

Cisco has been among the more flexible large tech companies when it comes to work arrangements, and many India-based roles are listed as hybrid. The exact setup depends on the specific team, business unit, and role type. It is worth confirming expectations around office attendance during the recruiter call, since policies can differ significantly across departments.

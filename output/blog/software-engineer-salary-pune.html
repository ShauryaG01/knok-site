01 Salary Overview

Salary Overview

Software Engineer Salary in Pune (2026) Here is the uncomfortable truth about job hunting in India: the majority of Software Engineer listings on major job portals do not display a salary figure. You apply, clear two or three rounds, and only then find out whether the pay matches your expectations. This makes it genuinely hard to benchmark yourself before you invest time. The numbers below come from knok's jobradar data as of 8 July 2026, covering 169 active Software Engineer openings in Pune. Salary figures are in LPA (lakhs per annum), the standard used across Indian hiring. The broad range for Software Engineers in Pune runs from 6 LPA at the entry level all the way to 65+ LPA for lead and staff engineers. That wide spread is not noise, it reflects real differences in experience, tech stack, company tier, and whether the role comes with equity. Where a particular offer falls within that range depends almost entirely on the band your experience puts you in and the kind of company you are joining.

Entry (0-2 years): Freshers and early-career engineers in Pune typically land between 6 and 12 LPA. Product companies and funded startups tend to sit at the higher end of this band; IT service firms commonly start at the lower end. Mid-level (3-5 years): This is where salaries jump noticeably. A mid-level engineer with solid full-stack, backend, or cloud skills is looking at 15-25 LPA. Specialisations like DevOps, data engineering, or machine learning engineering push offers toward the upper end of that range. Senior (6-9 years): Senior engineers command 28-45 LPA in Pune. At this level, the ability to design systems, mentor juniors, and own deliverables end-to-end matters as much as raw coding ability. Lead / Staff (10+ years): Roles like Tech Lead, Staff Engineer, or Principal Engineer cross 40 LPA and can reach 65 LPA or beyond, especially at product companies. Equity and bonuses become a significant part of total compensation at this tier.

04 Negotiation Tips

Negotiation Tips

How to Negotiate Your Salary 1 Anchor on market bands, not your last CTC. Many companies still ask for your last drawn salary. You are not legally required to share it in most states, and the practice is increasingly being challenged. Lead with the market range for the role and your experience band instead. 2. Separate base, variable, and equity. An offer letter quoting a CTC figure may bundle in a variable component that is never fully paid out. Ask for the fixed take-home number, the variable structure and payout conditions, and any equity vesting schedule, all in writing, before you sign. 3. Use competing offers. A second offer is the single strongest negotiation tool you have. Even at the same level, holding two offers gives you real leverage to push both companies higher. 4. Ask for a joining bonus to cover unvested stock. If you are leaving unvested RSUs or ESOPs at your current company, ask the new employer to compensate with a one-time joining bonus. This is a standard ask at product companies and most hiring managers are prepared for it. 5. Set a correction review date if you join below band. If you accept an offer below what the market data shows, get a written commitment from your manager for a salary correction review within six months. Ask for it to be noted in your offer letter if the company agrees.

05 Total Comp Breakdown

Total Comp Breakdown

What Goes Into Total Compensation Base salary is what lands in your bank account each month. But at many companies, especially product firms and MNCs, total compensation has several other components that matter just as much: - Base salary: The fixed monthly payout. This is your foundation and the figure to compare first across any two offers.
- Variable pay / performance bonus: Paid quarterly or annually, linked to individual and company targets. The actual payout history at a company matters more than the headline figure, ask current employees what they realistically receive.
- ESOPs / RSUs: Equity in the company. For unlisted startups, ESOPs are speculative until a liquidity event happens. For listed MNCs, RSUs vest over a multi-year schedule and convert to real money on vesting dates.
- Gratuity and PF: Statutory contributions that form part of your CTC on paper but are not part of your liquid monthly income.
- Allowances: HRA, travel, meal vouchers, and internet allowance. These reduce your taxable income and can make a meaningful difference to actual take-home pay. Always ask for the fixed-to-variable split and the equity grant details before comparing offers side by side. A CTC number alone tells you only part of the story. Q Questions

Frequently asked

What is the salary range for a Software Engineer in Pune in 2026?

Based on knok's jobradar data covering 169 openings in Pune as of July 2026, salaries range from 6-12 LPA at the entry level to 40-65+ LPA for lead and staff engineers. The mid and senior bands sit at 15-25 LPA and 28-45 LPA respectively. Quoting a single 'average' is less useful than knowing which band your years of experience puts you in, because the spread across levels is very wide.

Is Pune a good city for Software Engineers compared to Bangalore?

Bangalore has 958 active Software Engineer openings in knok's data versus 169 in Pune, so raw job volume is significantly higher there. However, Pune has a strong MNC and product company presence, and its cost of living is publicly reported to be lower than Bangalore's. For many engineers, a Pune salary can match Bangalore in real take-home value once rent and commute costs are factored in.

How much can a fresher Software Engineer expect in Pune?

The entry band in knok's data runs from 6 to 12 LPA for engineers with 0-2 years of experience. Product companies and well-funded startups tend to offer toward the higher end of this band, while IT service companies commonly start at the lower end. Skills like cloud infrastructure, full-stack development, and strong fundamentals in data structures and algorithms can help push a fresher offer higher within the band.

Why do so many Software Engineer job postings not mention salary?

Indian labour law does not require companies to disclose salary in job advertisements, and most choose not to, preferring to negotiate based on a candidate's last drawn CTC. This practice is increasingly being questioned but remains widespread across job portals. Using market data like the salary bands above and speaking to people currently in similar roles are the most reliable ways to benchmark yourself before applying.

Which skills push a Software Engineer's salary toward the top of their band?

Within each experience band, skills in cloud platforms, distributed systems design, machine learning engineering, and DevOps or SRE consistently pull offers toward the upper end of the range, according to industry surveys. At the senior and lead levels, system design ability and a clear track record of owning large deliverables tend to matter more than any single technology listed on your resume.

Should I count variable pay and ESOPs when comparing two offers?

Yes, but with care. Variable pay should be checked against the company's actual payout history, ask current or former employees if you can. ESOPs at unlisted startups carry real uncertainty about when and whether they become liquid. RSUs at publicly listed companies are more predictable. Always get the fixed take-home figure clearly stated first, and compare that before layering in variable or equity components.

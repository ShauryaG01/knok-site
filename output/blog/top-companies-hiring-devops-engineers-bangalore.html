01 Salary Overview

Salary Overview

DevOps Engineer compensation in India varies by city, company stage, and whether the role is product, platform, or growth-focused. Fixed pay is quoted in LPA (lakhs per annum); variable pay and ESOPs are common at startups. Always clarify fixed vs CTC before negotiating.

04 Negotiation Tips

Negotiation Tips

  1. Anchor with data from 3+ offers or trusted peers at the same level.
  2. Negotiate fixed first; treat variable and ESOP separately.
  3. Ask for signing bonus if base is capped.
  4. Get the offer letter with break-up (basic, HRA, PF) before resigning.
  5. If competing offers exist, share timelines, not numbers, to accelerate decisions.
05 Total Comp Breakdown

Total Comp Breakdown

CTC typically includes: Basic, HRA, special allowance, PF employer contribution, gratuity provision, and projected variable. Ask for in-hand estimate after tax. ESOPs at startups may not appear in CTC, model dilution and vesting cliff separately.

06 Market Snapshot

Market Snapshot

As of 8 July 2026, knok's job radar counted 165 active DevOps Engineer openings in Bangalore, the highest of any Indian city for this role right now. For comparison, Delhi and Pune each show 35 openings, Hyderabad 23, and Mumbai and Chennai 10 each. Bangalore's dominance reflects a cluster effect that has been building for years: global MNCs running large GCCs, product-led tech companies with Indian engineering hubs, and a funded startup ecosystem are all competing for the same talent pool simultaneously. That competition is good news for candidates, it keeps salaries pushed upward and gives you genuine choice across company types and work cultures.

09 How To Stand Out

How To Stand Out

With 165 live roles spread across GCCs, product companies, and startups, the bar varies, but these signals work broadly: Cloud certifications are now a baseline filter
AWS, GCP, or Azure certification appears in a large share of Bangalore DevOps JDs. A Kubernetes certification (CKA or CKAD) is fast becoming equally common, especially at GCCs and product companies. Tool depth beats surface breadth
Hiring teams at companies like Okta and Nutanix have publicly noted they prefer candidates who can walk through Terraform state management or Helm chart rollbacks in depth over those who list a long roster of tools without hands-on proof. Show production experience, not just lab work
Startups like TrueFoundry and PocketFM move fast and want engineers who have handled on-call incidents, cost-optimisation drives, or real outage response, even from internships or side projects. Mention outcomes, not just tools used. A public GitHub repo helps at startups
For companies like Signzy and TrueFoundry, a visible repo showing CI/CD pipelines, Ansible playbooks, or infrastructure-as-code work speaks louder than resume claims with no external proof.

10 Application Strategy

Application Strategy

Start with the largest pools, then work down
Okta (12 roles) and Bosch (10 roles) are the biggest active pools right now. Apply there first, structured hiring processes mean clearer timelines and defined feedback loops. Startups like Signzy and TrueFoundry list fewer roles per company, so competition per slot can be higher even when the JD looks approachable. Tailor your resume by company type
For Bosch or FIS Global, emphasise enterprise-scale infra, compliance tooling, and ITSM integration. For Okta or Oracle, front-load cloud-native and security-adjacent experience. For startups, lead with ownership: 'built the CI/CD pipeline from scratch for an early-stage team' beats 'contributed to DevOps pipeline.' Message the hiring team directly for startup roles
For PocketFM, Signzy, or TrueFoundry, a short LinkedIn note to the engineering manager often shortcuts the applicant queue. Keep it to one paragraph: your relevant experience, why that company specifically, and one concrete problem you could help solve. Follow up or fall through the cracks
Applications go cold quickly without a follow-up. Set a reminder per application and reach back out after a week. Q Questions

Frequently asked

Which company has the most DevOps openings in Bangalore right now?

Okta leads with 12 open DevOps roles as of 8 July 2026, followed by Bosch Group with 10. Okta's Bangalore GCC works on identity and access management infrastructure at a global scale, so cloud security and zero-trust tooling feature heavily in their interviews. Both companies have been consistently active hirers and represent some of the most stable GCC opportunities currently visible in the Bangalore market.

Are DevOps roles at startups like TrueFoundry or Signzy stable?

Funded startups carry more risk than GCCs, but TrueFoundry (an MLOps platform) and Signzy (an established fintech company) are both venture-backed and actively expanding their engineering teams. The trade-off is real: job security is comparatively lower, but scope is far wider, you are likely to own infrastructure decisions rather than execute narrowly defined tickets. Before applying, check their funding history and headcount growth on LinkedIn to get a sense of current trajectory.

Do GCC DevOps roles pay as well as product company roles in Bangalore?

GCCs like Oracle, Okta, and FIS Global generally pay at the upper end of market bands, according to publicly reported data on Glassdoor. Pure product companies like Nutanix may match or exceed GCC base salaries and add meaningful stock (RSUs or ESOPs) that can significantly lift total compensation over a vesting period. The gap between the two segments narrows at senior and lead levels, where both compete hard for a relatively small pool of experienced engineers.

What experience level do most Bangalore DevOps openings target?

The majority of active roles sit in the mid level (3-5 years) band, which is typical of Bangalore's market structure. Entry-level roles exist but are fewer, most companies expect at least some production environment exposure before hiring. Senior roles (6-9 years) command 30-50 LPA and lead or staff roles 45-70+ LPA, but both typically come with on-call responsibility and architecture ownership expectations.

Is a Wipro DevOps role a good career move in Bangalore?

Wipro's current listings are likely client-delivery roles, meaning you work embedded with an enterprise client's infrastructure team rather than on Wipro's own products. The upside is exposure to large, complex environments and a structured career ladder with predictable increments. The downside is that industry surveys commonly cite IT services salaries as sitting below GCC or product company equivalents, and role scope can be narrowly defined by the client contract you are assigned to.

How do I choose between a GCC, product company, and startup for a DevOps role?

Ask yourself what you want your resume to say a few years from now. GCCs (Okta, Bosch, FIS Global, Oracle) give you enterprise scale and globally recognised brand names. Product companies like Nutanix offer stronger engineering culture and equity upside. Startups like PocketFM, Signzy, and TrueFoundry give you breadth and speed, you ship, break, and fix faster, which compresses your learning curve significantly. There is no universally correct answer; the best choice depends on your current experience gaps and where you want to specialise next.

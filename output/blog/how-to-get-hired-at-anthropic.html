01 Hiring Overview

Hiring Overview

Anthropic builds Claude, one of the most capable AI assistants in the world, and it is one of the most mission-driven companies in tech right now. AI safety is not a tagline here. It shapes what they build, who they hire, and how teams work day to day.

As of July 2026, Anthropic has 426 open roles across research science, software engineering, policy, trust and safety, product, and business operations. Most roles are based in San Francisco, with select remote-friendly positions available. For Indian professionals, competition is global and the bar is genuinely high. But if you have a strong research or engineering background and care deeply about responsible AI, this is one of the most meaningful places you can build a career right now.

03 Interview Process

Interview Process

Anthropic's hiring process is thorough and runs in several stages. The exact steps vary by role and team, but here is what candidates commonly report:

1. Application review
The team screens resumes for relevant background in ML, software engineering, policy, or whichever domain the role covers. A generic CV gets filtered quickly. Tailor your application to the specific team and role you are targeting.

2. Recruiter call
If your application clears the initial screen, a recruiter reaches out for an introductory conversation. Expect questions about your background, why you want to work at Anthropic specifically, and a walkthrough of what the role involves.

3. Technical screen or take-home
For engineering and research roles, this typically means a coding screen or a short written exercise. Research candidates may be asked to share past work or complete a focused technical task. Policy and operations roles sometimes skip this stage entirely.

4. Loop interviews
This is the core of the process. Candidates go through multiple back-to-back conversations covering technical depth, system design, and past experience. Research roles often include a paper discussion or short presentation. Every role includes at least one conversation focused on values and working style.

5. Final review and offer
The hiring team debriefs, references may be checked, and an offer is extended if there is consensus. The full process from application to offer takes several weeks, based on publicly reported candidate timelines.

For remote or international candidates, some stages may be asynchronous or conducted over video rather than an on-site visit.

04 What They Look For

What They Look For

Anthropic does not hire on credentials alone. Here is what actually moves the needle:

  • Genuine care about AI safety. This is not something you can fake in interviews. Interviewers probe whether you have thought seriously about the risks of advanced AI, not just whether you can recite talking points.
  • Technical depth. For research roles, a strong track record in ML (through publications, competitive research, or impactful open-source work) matters a lot. For engineering roles, systems thinking and clean code under pressure are what get tested.
  • Clear communication. Anthropic teams work across functions. Being able to explain a complex idea simply, whether in writing or in conversation, is valued in every role.
  • Intellectual honesty. Interviewers notice candidates who say 'I do not know' and then reason carefully, versus those who bluff. The culture actively rewards intellectual humility over false confidence.
  • Collaborative instinct. The work touches hard problems with no clear answers. Interviewers want to see that you can disagree constructively and update your views based on evidence.
05 How To Prepare

How To Prepare

Read Anthropic's research first
Look up recent papers from the Anthropic team. Constitutional AI, interpretability, and model evaluations are recurring themes. Even for engineering or policy roles, showing you have actually engaged with the company's published work signals genuine interest.

Prepare your 'why Anthropic' answer honestly
This question carries more weight here than at most companies. A generic answer about 'working on exciting AI' will not land. Tie your answer to specific aspects of the company's safety mission or a research direction you find compelling.

Walk through your past impact clearly
For engineering roles, be ready to discuss projects where your decisions had real consequences. For research roles, be ready to defend the choices you made, including what you would do differently in hindsight.

Brush up on ML fundamentals for technical roles
Transformer architecture, training dynamics, and evaluation methods come up often in technical screens. Anthropic's own published research is a solid and directly relevant study resource.

Ask thoughtful questions
Interviewers notice candidates who ask about the team's current open problems or research directions. Asking only about perks or compensation timelines during the technical round is a common misstep.

06 Culture And Values

Culture And Values

Anthropic describes itself as a safety-focused AI company and the culture reflects that seriousness. A few things stand out for anyone considering a role here:

The mission is load-bearing.
Employees at Anthropic, based on publicly available interviews and posts, frequently cite the mission as a primary reason they stay and turn down higher-paying offers elsewhere. If you are motivated mainly by prestige or compensation, that tends to surface during interviews.

Writing is central to how decisions get made.
Long-form written communication, internal memos, research writeups, and detailed async updates are core to how teams operate. If you enjoy thinking through ideas in writing, this environment will suit you well.

The pace is intense but thoughtful.
Anthropics moves quickly on the product side while investing heavily in careful evaluation and testing. It is not a 'move fast and break things' culture. Rigor is prized at every level.

Debate is welcome, consensus is earned.
The team tackles problems where reasonable people disagree. You are expected to hold and defend positions with evidence, not simply defer to whoever is most senior in the room.

The team is deliberately cross-disciplinary.
Anthropics hires from academia, policy, philosophy, and engineering alike. Being only an engineer or only a researcher matters less than how deeply and clearly you think.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Does Anthropic hire remotely or sponsor visas for Indian candidates?

Most Anthropic roles are based in San Francisco, though the company does list select remote-friendly positions. For international candidates, visa sponsorship for H-1B and similar work authorizations is commonly offered by US AI companies at this level, but you should confirm sponsorship availability directly on the job listing or with the recruiter during your first call. Relocation support has been publicly reported for senior hires, so it is worth asking if you are at that stage.

What does Anthropic pay compared to Indian tech companies?

Anthropic compensation packages are publicly reported on sites like Glassdoor and levels.fyi, and they tend to be competitive with top-tier US tech companies, including significant equity components. A direct comparison to Indian tech salaries is difficult because these are primarily US-based roles with a very different cost-of-living context. If you are evaluating a potential offer, checking current data on levels.fyi for comparable roles at similar-stage AI companies is a practical starting point.

How important are research publications for landing a technical role?

Publications are not a strict requirement for all technical roles, but they carry significant weight for research scientist and research engineer positions. If you do not have publications, a strong portfolio of open-source ML work, competition results, or high-impact projects at previous companies can serve a similar purpose. What Anthropic actually values is evidence that you can do original, rigorous technical work, regardless of where that evidence comes from.

Can I apply without a degree from IIT or a top-ranked university?

Anthropic does not publicly list a degree filter and has hired people from a range of educational backgrounds. For highly competitive research and engineering roles, strong academic credentials do appear frequently in the backgrounds of hired candidates based on publicly available LinkedIn data. What matters most is demonstrable skill and original thinking. A compelling portfolio of ML work or impactful engineering projects can make up for a less well-known institution.

How long does the full hiring process take from application to offer?

The full process from initial application to offer typically takes several weeks, based on publicly reported candidate experiences. The timeline can be shorter for senior or urgent roles and longer when interview loops span multiple time zones. It is reasonable to ask your recruiter for a rough expected timeline after your first introductory call so you can plan your preparation schedule accordingly.

Which roles at Anthropic are most accessible for Indian professionals?

Software engineering, ML infrastructure, trust and safety, and policy roles are areas where Indian professionals have a strong presence in the US AI industry, based on publicly available hiring patterns. Research scientist roles are the most competitive globally and usually require a very strong ML research background. Operations, communications, and go-to-market roles are also available but tend to be more US-focused in their hiring.

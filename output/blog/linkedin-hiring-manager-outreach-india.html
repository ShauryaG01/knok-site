01 Problem Overview

Problem Overview

Most Indian job seekers apply through job portals and then wait in silence. Messaging a hiring manager directly on LinkedIn feels like the smarter move, but it rarely works the way people expect. You write a message, hit send, and nothing happens. The hiring manager may have seen it, read the first line, and moved on.

The problem is not your effort. It is the approach. Most messages look like copy-paste templates that dozens of other candidates also sent that same week. Hiring managers at large companies and growing startups receive many of these daily. They have no time for a long paragraph about your career journey. If your profile does not immediately signal relevance, they close the chat before finishing your first sentence.

This guide covers what actually works when you reach out to a hiring manager on LinkedIn in India, and how to avoid the mistakes that get your message ignored.

02 What To Look For

What To Look For

When crafting your LinkedIn outreach, check for these qualities in every message you send:

  1. Personalization beyond the name. A message that mentions a specific post they wrote, a team they built, or a challenge their company is publicly facing shows real research. Generic openers like 'I came across your profile' signal zero effort and get ignored.
  1. One clear ask. Do not ask for a job, a referral, a call, and feedback all in one message. Pick one. The clearest ask is usually 'Would you be open to a short call?' or 'Could you point me to the right person on your team?'
  1. A short value hook. Tell them in one sentence why you are relevant. Not your full career story. Something like: 'I have spent the last few years building data pipelines at a fintech, and I noticed your team is scaling its analytics work.'
  1. Timing relative to their activity. If they posted on LinkedIn recently, reference it. If they joined a new company in the past few months, they may be actively building their team and more open to inbound messages.
  1. Connection request vs InMail choice. A connection request with a short note often gets more attention than a cold InMail, because it feels more human. LinkedIn limits connection request notes to a short character count (commonly cited as around 300), so every word matters. Use InMail when there is no other path to reach them.
  1. Mutual connection leverage. If you share a mutual connection, mention it early. 'Ravi suggested I reach out' changes how your message is received entirely.
  1. Follow-up discipline. One follow-up within a week of your first message is fine. Two messages total is the maximum before moving on.

03 Top Options

Top Options

There are several distinct approaches to reaching hiring managers on LinkedIn. Each fits a different situation.

Connection request with a short note
Best for most situations where you want to start a conversation. The note must be concise, so lead with relevance, not pleasantries. Skip 'Hi, I hope you are doing well' and go straight to why you are reaching out and what makes you a fit for their team.

Cold InMail (LinkedIn Premium)
Best for reaching people outside your network who have not enabled open profiles. LinkedIn gives you a set number of InMail credits per month with Premium. Reserve them for senior decision-makers you genuinely cannot reach any other way.

Comment-first approach
Best for building warm familiarity before sending a direct message. If a hiring manager posts regularly, comment thoughtfully on a few posts over a few weeks. Then send a connection request. Your name is already familiar when your message arrives.

Warm introduction through a mutual contact
Best for any situation where you have a genuine mutual connection. This is consistently the most effective approach. Ask your mutual contact to make a brief introduction, then follow up directly. The hiring manager already has a reason to trust you.

Reaching out via the job post itself
Best for roles that were posted directly on LinkedIn by the hiring manager. Commenting on that post is a natural conversation starter. It shows you found them through their own work, not just a candidate database.

Contacting a recruiter at the same company
Best when you cannot find the hiring manager but can find a recruiter at the company on LinkedIn. Recruiters are generally more responsive to inbound messages than hiring managers, and they can route you internally to the right team.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Should I connect first or send an InMail directly?

Connect first with a short note, unless the person has open InMail enabled on their profile. A connection request feels less intrusive and gives them a chance to check your profile before deciding to respond. If you send a cold InMail to someone who does not know you, your message may land in a separate request folder that many people rarely open. Connecting first also means your future messages arrive in their main inbox, which increases the chance of being seen.

How long should my message to a hiring manager be?

Keep it short. Commonly cited advice puts the ideal length at around 100 words or fewer. Your message should answer three things in that space: who you are, why you are relevant to them specifically, and what you are asking for. Hiring managers skim, and anything longer risks being saved for later, which usually means never read.

Is it okay to message a hiring manager after I already applied through the company portal?

Yes, and it can actually help. Mention that you applied, reference the specific role, and keep the message short. Something like: 'I applied for the product manager role last week and wanted to connect directly. Happy to share more context if useful.' This shows initiative without being pushy. Just make sure you applied first, so you have something concrete to reference in your message.

How many follow-up messages should I send?

One follow-up, sent within a week of your first message, is acceptable. Keep it to a couple of sentences. Do not send a third message. If someone has not replied after two messages, they have seen it and are not interested right now. Following up a third time tends to leave a negative impression and can close the door for future opportunities at that company.

Does LinkedIn Premium actually help with job searching in India?

LinkedIn Premium gives you InMail credits and lets you see who viewed your profile, which can be useful signals. Industry surveys suggest many Indian job seekers find the most value in the InMail credits and the visibility boost that comes with the Premium badge. That said, the core strategy of warm outreach and a strong, keyword-rich profile matters more than any paid feature. Premium can amplify a good approach, but it will not fix a weak one.

What if the hiring manager never replies at all?

Move on after one follow-up. Silence is not always rejection. Timing, team bandwidth, and internal role changes all affect whether someone responds. Keep a simple record of who you contacted and when, and revisit in a few months if the company is still on your target list. Meanwhile, focus your energy on finding warm paths into other companies rather than chasing a cold contact who has not responded.

Salary Overview

04 Negotiation Tips

Negotiation Tips

  1. Anchor with data from 3+ offers or trusted peers at the same level.
  2. Negotiate fixed first; treat variable and ESOP separately.
  3. Ask for signing bonus if base is capped.
  4. Get the offer letter with break-up (basic, HRA, PF) before resigning.
  5. If competing offers exist, share timelines, not numbers, to accelerate decisions.
05 Total Comp Breakdown

Total Comp Breakdown

CTC typically includes: Basic, HRA, special allowance, PF employer contribution, gratuity provision, and projected variable. Ask for in-hand estimate after tax. ESOPs at startups may not appear in CTC, model dilution and vesting cliff separately.

06 Market Snapshot

Market Snapshot

Delhi NCR has 36 active Frontend Engineer openings as of 8 July 2026, based on knok jobradar data. That puts it in second place nationally, behind Bangalore (102 openings) and ahead of Pune (11), Mumbai (6), Hyderabad (5), and Chennai (3).

What sets Delhi NCR apart is its mix. You will find Global Capability Centres (GCCs) of large multinationals, Indian product companies, and early-stage startups all hiring at the same time. Deutsche Telekom Digital Labs is a typical GCC, bringing MNC structure and a global product roadmap. Adobe Systems represents large-product-company rigour. Sprinklr, Paytm, webengage, and a cluster of growth-stage startups give the market a product-first, move-fast energy. This means roles range from well-structured engineering orgs with clear career tracks to smaller teams where you own both the component library and the performance budget.

If you are open to relocating, Bangalore has far more volume. But for engineers who want to build careers in the north, Delhi NCR is a solid and growing market.

09 How To Stand Out

How To Stand Out

Frontend engineering in Delhi NCR is competitive. Most JDs overlap heavily on React and TypeScript. Here is what actually moves your profile up the stack.

Show product impact, not just technology. Hiring managers at product companies like Sprinklr and webengage want to see outcomes: pages that got faster, flows that converted better, a design system adopted across multiple teams. GCCs like Deutsche Telekom want to see delivery at scale and process adherence. Frame your project descriptions in the language the company cares about.

React alone is not enough. Nearly every JD assumes React. What differentiates you is the layer underneath: state management trade-offs (Zustand vs Redux vs Context), rendering strategies (SSR, SSG, ISR), bundle optimisation, and Core Web Vitals. Mention specific tools and the reasoning behind your choices, not just that you used them.

Accessibility and performance matter more than people admit. Adobe and enterprise-facing roles will actually test for WCAG awareness. Even when other companies do not screen for it explicitly, mentioning it signals seniority and production-readiness.

Your portfolio should practice what you preach. A portfolio site with a very slow load time is a red flag for a frontend engineer. Make sure your own work reflects the performance standards you claim to care about.

For GCC roles, keep your LinkedIn current. Recruiters at Deutsche Telekom Digital Labs and similar firms scout LinkedIn actively. A profile with a skills section that matches the JD keywords can get you shortlisted without formally applying.

10 Application Strategy

Application Strategy

Delhi NCR has 36 openings spread across companies with very different hiring cadences. Here is a practical approach.

Segment by company type before you apply. Decide whether you want a GCC (stability, MNC brand, structured growth), a product company (Sprinklr, Adobe, Paytm, webengage), or a startup (wishlink, wcube, Echos). Your resume framing, salary expectations, and interview prep will differ for each.

Apply early. Smaller companies like industrybuying and EngiNeo Solutions often have short hiring windows. A role can close in days once a good candidate pool forms. Waiting a week to polish your resume can mean missing the window entirely.

Keep two or three resume variants, not ten. One for product companies (impact metrics, technical depth), one for GCCs (delivery, scale, team collaboration), one for startups (ownership, breadth, speed). Tailor the positioning, not the whole document.

Follow up after applying. A short LinkedIn note to the hiring manager or recruiter after submitting through a portal noticeably improves your visibility at smaller companies. Keep it one to two sentences and specific to the role.

Q Questions

Frequently asked

Which company is hiring the most Frontend Engineers in Delhi NCR right now?

Sprinklr leads with 4 open roles, followed by Deutsche Telekom Digital Labs with 3. Sprinklr is a listed SaaS company, so its roles tend to involve React at scale, design systems, and cross-functional product work. Both have structured interview processes, so prepare with system design for UI and deep component-level questions.

What is the difference between working at a GCC versus a product company as a frontend engineer?

A GCC like Deutsche Telekom Digital Labs is the Indian engineering arm of a multinational. Work is tied to the parent company's global product, pay is typically benchmark-driven, and the culture is structured. A product company like Sprinklr or webengage builds its own product, moves faster, and expects more per-engineer ownership. Career growth at a GCC is steady and predictable; at a product company it can be faster but depends heavily on the company's stage and funding.

Is the Delhi NCR market good for frontend engineers compared to Bangalore?

Bangalore has 102 openings versus Delhi NCR's 36, so raw volume is higher there. However, Delhi NCR offers a solid mix of GCCs, product companies, and startups, and competition per role may be somewhat lower. If you are anchored in the north, the market is competitive and growing, and you do not need to relocate to build a strong frontend career.

What skills do these companies actually test for in frontend interviews?

React is baseline for nearly every company on this list, but what companies actually test varies by type. Product companies like Sprinklr go deep on state management, rendering strategies, and UI system design; GCCs like Deutsche Telekom assess delivery track record and process discipline. Startups like wishlink and wcube want breadth, including comfort working alongside designers and some backend awareness. Core Web Vitals and bundle optimisation come up more often than most candidates expect.

How long does it typically take to get an offer from these companies?

Timelines vary significantly. Based on commonly cited industry patterns, startups often move in two to three weeks from first call to offer, while mid-size product companies like Paytm and webengage typically take three to five weeks. GCCs like Deutsche Telekom Digital Labs can run four to eight weeks due to multiple approval layers. Running parallel applications across company types is the best way to keep your options open.

Should I apply to multiple companies at the same time?

Yes, always run parallel tracks. Companies move at different speeds, and waiting for one to finish before starting another wastes weeks. Aim to have two or three active processes going at any point. Be transparent with companies if you receive competing offers, as that often accelerates their process. Keeping a simple log of where you applied and when you last followed up prevents good applications from going cold.

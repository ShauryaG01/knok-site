Q Questions

Frequently asked

Which job-search approach works best for India tech?

There's no single best channel — the right mix depends on your target level. For startup and tech roles, niche boards like Instahyre and Cutshort surface openings faster and with lighter competition than Naukri or LinkedIn. Pair that discovery with a pipeline tool or agent so you can apply at volume without losing track, and lean on recruiters for senior or confidential searches where hidden roles matter most.

Is an AI job-search agent worth it?

It's worth it if you're running a high-volume search — roughly 30 or more tailored applications a week — where the repetitive scanning and tailoring eats your time. An agent handles board coverage and first-draft tailoring so you can focus on outreach and interviews. It's overkill if you're targeting fewer than about ten highly curated roles, where manual effort gives you tighter control.

Are recruiters worth using as a job seeker?

Yes for senior, niche, or confidential searches, where recruiters have access to roles that are never publicly posted and can advocate for you internally. Just remember their incentives are aligned with filling the role and their fee, not necessarily with getting you the best long-term fit. Use them as one channel among several, and keep driving your own applications in parallel.

How many applications does a typical search take?

Most successful candidates in Indian tech apply to somewhere between 40 and 80 well-targeted roles over six to eight weeks, rather than spraying hundreds of generic applications. Quality of targeting and tailoring drives interview rate far more than raw volume, so track your interview rate per 50 applications and adjust your targeting if it's below roughly 10%.
